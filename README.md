## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|

|**2026-05-15**|**Analyzing the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model with the Worldvolume HMC method**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2605.14965](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14965)]|The paper addresses the severe sign problem in the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model, which prevents standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo simulations away from half-filling. The authors apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method to handle this issue. On an \(8 \times 8\) lattice at temperature \(T/t \approx 0.156\) and interaction strength \(U/t = 8.0\), the method successfully predicts physical observables with controlled statistical errors. This work demonstrates that WV-HMC can simulate doped Hubbard systems where conventional approaches fail, offering a promising tool for studying strongly correlated electron systems.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Topological solitons of two-field scalar theories in rotationally symmetric backgrounds**|I. Andrade, M. A. Liao|[[arxiv:2605.14160](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14160)]|This paper studies scalar field theories with topologically nontrivial vacuum manifolds in rotationally symmetric backgrounds of any dimension. The authors develop a Bogomol'nyi framework to find first-order equations and localized topological soliton solutions, overcoming typical higher-dimensional scaling instability by introducing an explicit radial dependence in the potential. A key result is that the soliton’s first-order equations map to a one-dimensional BPS theory, allowing exact solutions in various geometries including Schwarzschild and de Sitter. The work reveals how background geometry controls the existence, confinement, and internal structure of solitons.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Analytic thin disks and rings in a class of nonasymptotically flat static spacetimes**|Shokoufe Faraji, Ruijing Tang|[[arxiv:2605.13890](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13890)]|This paper examines the effects of external matter on the structure of thin accretion disks around compact objects. The method involves analyzing orbital dynamics and accretion flows in a specific, deformed spacetime that includes an external quadrupolar field. Key results show that this quadrupolar distortion significantly alters the disk's orbital dynamics and structure, with the outer edge of the radiating region linked to a transition from radiation to gas pressure dominance. The significance lies in demonstrating that the local spacetime geometry has observable consequences for accretion flow morphology and emission, challenging idealized isolated models.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Enhancing the ergodicity of Worldvolume HMC via embedding generalized thimble HMC**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2508.02659](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.02659)]|The paper addresses ergodicity problems in the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method for sign-problem mitigation when the worldvolume becomes thin. The authors propose embedding Generalized Thimble HMC (GT-HMC) within WV-HMC to improve phase-space exploration, as GT-HMC allows larger molecular dynamics steps and efficient sampling in less problematic regions. They prove the combined algorithm is consistent, showing agreement with standalone methods for the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model on an 8x8 lattice. This approach enables simulations on larger lattices, demonstrated by extrapolating number and energy densities to the zero Trotter step limit with controlled statistical errors.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Applying the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo method to the Hubbard model away from half filling**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2507.23748](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.23748)]|This paper applies the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method to the two-dimensional Hubbard model doped away from half filling, a system with a severe numerical sign problem. The method mitigates the sign problem while avoiding ergodicity issues common in Lefschetz thimble approaches. Key results show that WV-HMC successfully computes the number and energy densities on $6 \times 6$ and $8 \times 8$ lattices in parameter regimes where standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo methods fail. This work demonstrates the effectiveness of WV-HMC for strongly correlated fermion systems, though with a computational cost of $O(N^3)$ using direct solvers.|

|**2026-05-15**|**Geometric construction of superintegrable Poisson projection chains via Poisson centralizers**|Kai Jiang, Guorui Ma, Ian Marquette, Junze Zhang, Yao-Zhong Zhang|[[arxiv:2605.14490](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14490)]|This paper introduces a geometric method for constructing superintegrable systems using Poisson centralizers within the Lie-Poisson algebra of a complex semisimple Lie algebra. The method analyzes chains of reductive subgroups and their invariant Poisson subalgebras, defining a "Poisson projection chain" of affine Poisson varieties to characterize superintegrability. Key results show that the inclusions for a maximal torus create a superintegrable chain, with rank computations confirming the correct split between commuting Hamiltonians and first integrals. The work provides a unified geometric framework and explicit examples, offering a new algebraic approach to building superintegrable systems.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-05-15**|**Dynamical scaling near the pseudogap quantum critical point of the two-dimensional Hubbard model**|Mathias Pelz, Gabriel Kotliar, Jan von Delft, Andreas Gleis|[[arxiv:2605.15060](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15060)]|The paper investigates dynamical scaling in the quantum-critical fan of the pseudogap-metal to Fermi-liquid transition in the two-dimensional Hubbard model. Using a four-patch dynamical cluster approximation with a numerical renormalization group solver, the authors access real-frequency dynamics over several decades at various temperatures. Key results show that near critical doping, susceptibility spectra and optical conductivity exhibit \(\omega/T\) scaling, implying a \(1/T\) cluster dc conductivity and a marginal-Fermi-liquid nodal self-energy. This finding suggests strange-metal optical transport in the quantum critical region, qualitatively matching several experimental observations.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Quantum criticality in the two-dimensional Hubbard model**|Mathias Pelz, Gabriel Kotliar, Jan von Delft, Andreas Gleis|[[arxiv:2605.15059](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15059)]|This paper uses the dynamical cluster approximation to study the two-dimensional Hubbard model, focusing on its normal-state phase diagram. The key finding is a continuous quantum phase transition at a critical doping level, where the system shifts from a pseudogap metal to a normal Fermi liquid. This transition is marked by the collapse of the Fermi-liquid scale, leading to a non-Fermi-liquid regime that persists to arbitrarily low energy scales at the critical point. The results are significant because they reveal how the spectral weight is suppressed in the antinodal region while remaining large in the nodal region, shedding light on the quantum critical behavior relevant to cuprate superconductors.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Analyzing the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model with the Worldvolume HMC method**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2605.14965](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14965)]|The paper addresses the severe sign problem in the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model, which prevents standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo simulations. The authors apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method to overcome this issue. Using an 8×8 lattice at specific temperature and interaction parameters where standard methods fail, they demonstrate the WV-HMC method can predict physical observables with controlled statistical errors. This work is significant because it provides a viable computational approach for studying strongly correlated electron systems away from half filling, which is relevant to high-temperature superconductivity and other phenomena.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Revealing Hidden Correlations in a Fermi-Hubbard system via Interaction Ramps**|Botond Oreg, Carter Turnbaugh, Jens Hertkorn, Ningyuan Jia, Martin Zwierlein|[[arxiv:2605.14909](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14909)]|This paper presents a method to reveal hidden charge-density-wave correlations in a cold-atom simulation of the attractive Hubbard model. The technique involves a rapid increase in interaction strength, which transforms nonlocal pairs into identifiable doublons that mark the pairs' center of mass. The enhancement of these correlations is strongest in the highly correlated regime and allows for clear distinction between unpaired Fermi liquids and pseudogap phases. This approach could enable the observation of exotic pair ordering in spin-imbalanced systems and stripe ordering in repulsive Hubbard models.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Enhancing the ergodicity of Worldvolume HMC via embedding generalized thimble HMC**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2508.02659](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.02659)]|The paper addresses ergodicity issues in the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method, which can emerge when the worldvolume becomes too thin, hindering efficient phase-space exploration. To solve this, the authors propose embedding Generalized Thimble HMC (GT-HMC) into the WV-HMC framework, leveraging GT-HMC's ability to efficiently explore allowed regions with larger step sizes despite its own ergodicity issues near zeros. Key results show that the combined algorithm is consistent with standalone methods within statistical errors for the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model on an 8×8 lattice and enables simulations on larger lattices. This work is significant because it extends the applicability of WV-HMC to larger system sizes and controlled statistical errors, as demonstrated by extrapolating densities to the zero Trotter step limit at a fixed temperature and interaction strength.|
|**2026-05-15**|**Applying the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo method to the Hubbard model away from half filling**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2507.23748](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.23748)]|The paper applies the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method, an algorithm designed to address the numerical sign problem, to the two-dimensional Hubbard model away from half filling. The method mitigates sign issues while avoiding ergodicity problems common in thimble-based approaches. Key results show that WV-HMC successfully computes number density and energy density on \(6 \times 6\) and \(8 \times 8\) lattices at low temperature and strong interaction, where standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo fails. The study demonstrates the method's effectiveness in challenging parameter regimes, using direct solvers with \(O(N^3)\) cost, while noting future work will introduce more efficient pseudofermion-based algorithms.|

|**2026-04-28**|**On non-relativistic integrable models and 4d SCFTs**|Rotem Ben Zeev, Anirudh Deb, Hee-Cheol Kim, Shlomo S. Razamat|[[arxiv:2604.19885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19885)]|This paper explores the link between the generalized Schur index of 4D N=2 superconformal field theories (SCFTs) and the non-relativistic limit of the elliptic Ruijsenaars-Schneider integrable model. The authors show that indices for class S theories can be expressed using eigenfunctions like elliptic Jack functions and the Lamé equation for the A1 case. They use this eigenfunction formulation to verify mappings between theories in the Deligne-Cvitanović series, leading to non-trivial identities for sums of Calogero-Moser eigenfunctions. |**2026-04-28**|**Entanglement inequalities, black holes and the architecture of typical states**|Radouane Gannouji, Ayan Mukhopadhyay, Nicolas Pinochet|[[arxiv:2511.02903](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.02903)]|This paper uses the holographic Araki-Lieb (AL) inequality to show that typical pure states in large N conformal field theories (CFTs) have two length scales set by energy and charges. The method reveals that degrees of freedom between a microscopic scale L_UV and an infrared scale L_IR effectively factorize, with the ultraviolet sector fully determined by global charges. Key results include the isolation of black holes from an asymptotic "corona" region via entanglement wedges where the AL inequality is saturated. The work is significant as it reproduces and generalizes the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis to rotating thermal ensembles within holography.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-04-28**|**Ground state of the Hubbard model with spin-dependent linear potential**|Jacek Dobrzyniecki, Thomas Busch|[[arxiv:2604.24068](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24068)]|This paper studies a one-dimensional Fermi-Hubbard model where attractive interactions compete with a spin-dependent linear potential that pushes each spin component to opposite ends of the lattice. Using DMRG simulations, the authors identify three distinct regimes separated by critical potential gradients: robust pairing, a staircase-like breaking of individual bound pairs, and complete spatial spin separation. Key results show that the number of doubly occupied sites decreases in integer steps within the intermediate regime, and analytical thresholds are derived from a local-density approximation. The findings offer a mechanism for integer-level control over bound fermion pairs, which is directly testable in cold-atom experiments.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Quenched Dipole Pairs in Viscous Fluid Membranes across the Saffman Crossover: Integrable Hamiltonian Dynamics**|Satyagni Bhattacharya, Debdatta Dey, Samyak Jain, Yassir Khan, Tirthankar Mazumder, Aryaman Mihir Seth, Nikhil Mogalapalli, Divyansh Tiwari, Pravallika Vemparala, Rickmoy Samanta|[[arxiv:2604.23868](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23868)]|This paper presents an analytic theory for the hydrodynamics of orientation-fixed (quenched) force dipoles in a viscous membrane surrounded by fluid. The authors demonstrate that the well-known Saffman crossover in single-dipole flow reorganizes the pairwise interactions, leading to integrable Hamiltonian dynamics for two identical dipoles. A key finding is that puller dipoles exhibit a transition in their collapse dynamics, scaling as \(R \sim (t_c - t)^{1/3}\) in the far field compared to \(R \sim (t_c - t)^{1/2}\) in the near field, driven by angular alignment toward an attracting manifold. This work provides a minimal framework for understanding aggregation in viscous fluid membranes by revealing a fundamental shift from effectively one-dimensional to fully two-dimensional coupled dynamics across the Saffman crossover.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Minimal spin-rotor model for Barnett and Einstein--de Haas physics**|Saikat Banerjee|[[arxiv:2604.23768](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23768)]|This paper addresses how the interaction between spin and mechanical rotation changes when the rotation itself is quantum. The authors introduce an exactly solvable model coupling a spin-1/2 to a quantum rotor. They find that while the model behaves classically within a fixed angular-momentum state, superpositions of rotor states cause the effective magnetic field to become an operator, generating coherent spin-rotor entanglement. This work identifies a minimal quantum setting where the Barnett effect departs from its classical form and acquires a new reciprocal manifestation through spin-dependent coherence.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Peak-valley mechanism for Hilbert space fragmentation**|Jianlong Fu, Hoi Chun Po|[[arxiv:2604.23659](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23659)]|The paper addresses the problem of Hilbert space fragmentation (HSF), a form of ergodicity breaking in quantum systems lacking a general organizing principle. The authors propose a "peak-valley" mechanism for one-dimensional integer spin chains, where a local rule assigns emergent quantum numbers based on geometric peak and valley patterns. They show this framework explains known strong HSF models and their variants, and enables construction of new fragmented models, including higher-order HSF cases. The significance lies in providing a unified, systematic principle to understand and generate HSF, advancing the study of nonthermal behavior in disorder-free quantum systems.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Principles of relativistic quantum statistical thermodynamics: a class of exactly solvable models**|A. Yu. Zakharov|[[arxiv:2604.23409](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23409)]|This paper addresses the problem of formulating a relativistic quantum statistical thermodynamics for interacting atoms. The method involves representing the system as interacting atoms plus an auxiliary scalar field that reproduces a given interatomic potential in the non-relativistic limit. Key results show that the relativistic partition function can be exactly calculated by renormalizing the auxiliary field's parameters, that the classical field causes an ultraviolet catastrophe which quantization resolves, and that such systems exhibit a phase transition. This work is significant because it provides exactly solvable models that demonstrate how to consistently unify relativity and quantum statistics in interacting many-particle systems.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Grassmann time-evolving matrix product operators for fermionic impurities coupled to a superconducting bath**|Chu Guo, Wei Wu, Xiansong Xu, Ping-Xing Chen, Changming Yue, Tian Jiang, Ruofan Chen|[[arxiv:2604.23301](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23301)]|The paper addresses the problem of simulating fermionic quantum impurity problems where the impurity is coupled to a superconducting bath, which standard GTEMPO methods cannot handle due to the lack of an analytic influence functional. The authors extend GTEMPO to the Nambu formalism by using a Bogoliubov transformation to derive the influence functional in a form analogous to the normal bath case, allowing the core GTEMPO algorithms to be applied directly. They benchmark their method, called Nambu-GTEMPO, against exact diagonalization and continuous-time quantum Monte Carlo for both integrable and non-integrable systems, achieving accurate results in imaginary- and real-time calculations. This work provides a potentially scalable quantum impurity solver for studying superconducting states in equilibrium and non-equilibrium systems.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Realizing multi-orbital Emery models with ultracold atoms**|Conall McCabe, Jamie Boyd, Kaizhao Wang, Martin Lebrat, Cindy Regal, Adam Kaufman, Ana Maria Rey, Lukas Homeier|[[arxiv:2604.22955](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22955)]|The paper addresses the challenge of simulating multi-orbital Hubbard models, specifically the three-band Emery model relevant to cuprate superconductors, using ultracold atoms. The authors propose a novel optical superlattice architecture that engineer orbital degrees of freedom to reproduce key cuprate band structure features with controllable interactions. Key results include benchmarking via single-particle quantum walks, observation of a finite-temperature metal-insulator crossover with antiferromagnetic correlations using quantum Monte Carlo, and a Hamiltonian learning protocol to infer effective single-band models. This work offers a practical experimental pathway for quantum simulators to study complex multi-orbital physics in strongly-correlated materials.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Correlated dynamics of three-particle bound states induced by emergent impurities in Bose-Hubbard model**|Wenduo Zhao, Boning Huang, Yongguan Ke, Chaohong Lee|[[arxiv:2601.20042](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20042)]|This paper revisits three-particle bound states in the Bose-Hubbard model, using the concept of interaction-induced impurities to identify two distinct types: dimer-monomer bound states and bound edge states. The study finds that during quantum walks, the spread velocity of dimer-monomer bound states is significantly slower than that of a single particle, determined by the band's maximal group velocity. During Bloch oscillations, the oscillation period for these states is reduced to one-third of the single-particle period. The results provide new insights into the fundamental mechanics and collective dynamics of multi-particle bound states.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Fate of diffusion under integrability breaking of classical integrable magnets**|Jiaozi Wang, Sourav Nandy, Markus Kraft, Toma\v{z} Prosen, Robin Steinigeweg|[[arxiv:2511.19110](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.19110)]|This paper investigates the microscopic origins of diffusion by studying how integrability-breaking perturbations affect spin transport in a classical integrable magnet. Using large-scale simulations, the authors measure the spin diffusion constant and the statistics of magnetization transfer in the easy-axis regime. They find a sharp change in the diffusion constant with perturbation strength and a crossover from non-Gaussian to Gaussian statistics in higher-order cumulants. The results suggest that integrable systems possess a distinct, non-trivial diffusion mechanism that abruptly vanishes when integrability is broken.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Scaling of entanglement entropy and correlations in the variable-range extended Ising model**|Harikrishnan K J, Debasis Sadhukhan, Amit Kumar Pal|[[arxiv:2504.01846](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.01846)]|This paper studies the variable-range extended Ising model on a one-dimensional lattice, varying interaction range by changing each qubit's coordination number, Z. The key result shows that correlation functions exhibit algebraic decay only for distances up to Z, then switch to short-range exponential scaling. At the critical point, bipartite entanglement decays as a power law (~Z^{-γ}) with increasing coordination number, regardless of partition size or the interaction exponent α. The findings extend to non-equilibrium dynamics, where long-time averaged entanglement after a sudden quench shows a similar power-law decay with Z, revealing a robust scaling behavior.|

## Nonlinear Sciences

|**2026-04-28**|**Discrete integrable equations with three independent variables**|Ismagil T. Habibullin, Aigul R. Khakimova|[[arxiv:2604.24100](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24100)]|This paper investigates nonlinear integrable equations with three independent variables, including Toda-type lattices and fully discrete Hirota-Miwa models. The authors show that all these equation types can be reduced to Darboux-integrable hyperbolic systems, and that transitions between classes are possible via discretization while preserving characteristic integrals. Using this framework, they performed a comparative analysis of known Hirota-Miwa equations, reducing a list of 13 to just seven distinct models and identifying one potentially new equation. The study establishes a novel correspondence between 3D integrable models and provides Lax pairs for all seven equations, advancing the classification of integrable systems.|
|**2026-04-28**|**Quenched Dipole Pairs in Viscous Fluid Membranes across the Saffman Crossover: Integrable Hamiltonian Dynamics**|Satyagni Bhattacharya, Debdatta Dey, Samyak Jain, Yassir Khan, Tirthankar Mazumder, Aryaman Mihir Seth, Nikhil Mogalapalli, Divyansh Tiwari, Pravallika Vemparala, Rickmoy Samanta|[[arxiv:2604.23868](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23868)]|This paper develops an analytic theory for the hydrodynamic interactions of two fixed-orientation (quenched) force dipoles in a viscous membrane coupled to an external fluid. The key problem addressed is how the Saffman crossover—from an unconfined near-field flow decay to a screened far-field decay—affects dipole-dipole interactions. The authors find that the near-field dynamics is exactly solvable and one-dimensional, while the far-field remains integrable but becomes two-dimensional with coupled radial and angular motion. For puller-type dipoles, this crossover causes a transition in the aggregation scaling law from \(R\sim (t_c-t)^{1/2}\) in the near field to \(R\sim (t_c-t)^{1/3}\) in the far field, demonstrating that the Saffman crossover fundamentally reorganizes the Hamiltonian phase-space structure of dipolar interactions.|
|**2026-04-28**|**The continuous spectrum of bound states in expulsive potentials**|H. Sakaguchi, B. A. Malomed, A. C. Aristotelous, E. G. Charalampidis|[[arxiv:2602.07281](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07281)]|This paper challenges the common expectation that steep expulsive potentials cause delocalization by demonstrating that the 1D and 2D Schrödinger equations with such potentials actually produce normalizable eigenstates. The key result is that these states form full continuous spectra, including even and odd parity in 1D and vortex states with any vorticity in 2D, with asymptotic expressions matching numerical solutions. These findings extend the concept of bound states in the continuum to new linear systems. The significance lies in revealing effective self-trapping in linear models, with potential applications in quantum mechanics and paraxial photonics.|

Popolitov|[[arxiv:2607.06738](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06738)]|This paper examines two classes of quantum integrable systems: those related to type $A$ (including Ruijsenaars and Cherednik DAHA Hamiltonians) and those of type $C^\vee C$ (including Koornwinder Hamiltonians). The authors use the Ding-Iohara-Miki (DIM) algebra and the spherical double affine Hecke algebra (DAHA) as a unifying theoretical framework. |**2026-07-09**|**Regularized Master-Field Approximation for Large- $N$ Reduced Matrix Models**|Reishi Maeta|[[arxiv:2605.10720](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10720)]|This paper proposes a numerical method for large-$N$ reduced matrix models by regularizing the infinite-dimensional master field to a finite dimension, enforcing loop equations as constraints. The method avoids the sign problem at the fundamental level, making it applicable to both Euclidean and Minkowski models. Numerical tests on one- and two-matrix models show that it accurately reproduces exact solutions in the Euclidean case and perturbative results in the Minkowski case. The results demonstrate the method's effectiveness and support the existence of a regularized master-field description for these models.|
|**2026-07-09**|**The diffusion equation is compatible with special relativity**|Lorenzo Gavassino|[[arxiv:2601.19464](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19464)]|This paper addresses the longstanding belief that the diffusion equation is incompatible with special relativity due to its instantaneous spreading and instability under Lorentz boosts. The authors show this interpretation is incorrect by proving that any smooth, localized solution of the diffusion equation corresponds to the particle density of an exact solution of the relativistic Vlasov-Fokker-Planck equation. Key results establish a causal, stable, and thermodynamically consistent relativistic kinetic theory governed by diffusion across all wavelengths. The significance lies in resolving a fundamental conflict by demonstrating that standard arguments for instability and causality violations arise from solutions without a kinetic theory counterpart.|

|**2026-07-09**|**Athinization of irreducible $\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_n$-modules with dominant highest weights**|Mikhail Bershtein, Evgeny Mukhin, Leonid Rybnikov, Aleksandr Trufanov|[[arxiv:2607.07653](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07653)]|This paper presents a method called "athinization" that constructs irreducible $\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}_n$-modules with dominant highest weights. The method specializes generic Verma modules, realized as thin modules over the affine Yangian $Y(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_n)$, by restricting the periodic Gelfand-Tsetlin basis to a subset of permitted patterns. The key result is that this restricted basis yields a well-defined affine Yangian action, providing explicit Gelfand-Tsetlin-type bases and a new combinatorial realization for these modules. This work is significant as it connects the combinatorics to characters of $W$ -algebra minimal models and extends to quantum analogues via the quantum toroidal algebra.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Time-periodic vortices near translating symmetric dipole patches**|Luca Franzoi, Taoufik Hmidi, Riccardo Montalto|[[arxiv:2607.07613](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07613)]|The paper addresses the problem of finding time-periodic solutions near translating vortex dipole pairs in the 2D incompressible Euler equations, going beyond rigidly moving structures. The authors use contour dynamics, a Lyapunov-Schmidt reduction, and a Nash-Moser scheme to handle degeneracies from symmetries and transport effects. They construct families of non-rigid, time-periodic vortex patch solutions for a large Cantor set of parameters, revealing rich nearby dynamics. This work is significant for demonstrating that translating dipoles support complex periodic motions and for providing a method applicable to other nonlocal fluid PDEs with degenerate behaviors.|
|**2026-07-09**|**On difference-differential Lax pairs and integrals of Painlev\'e equations in finite characteristic**|Nalini Joshi, Tomas Lasic Latimer, Pieter Roffelsen|[[arxiv:2607.06980](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06980)]|This paper addresses the problem of deriving integrals of motion for Painlevé equations in finite characteristic. The authors present difference-differential Lax pairs for classical Painlevé equations in a standard 2×2 matrix form and develop a general method to extract conserved quantities from these pairs. Their method yields a countable list of integrals for each relevant Painlevé equation, with each list containing one integral per prime. This work is significant because it systematically extends the study of Painlevé equations and their integrals to finite characteristic, opening new avenues for research in arithmetic and algebraic geometry.|

The key result highlights important structural distinctions between the two system types, despite their overall similarity. The significance lies in deepening the understanding of how DAHA and DIM algebra generate different classes of integrable systems and their associated special functions.|

They also explain that previous claims of instability stem from solutions with no kinetic theory analogue, and that apparent causality violations vanish when signals are defined using microscopic data.|

|**2026-07-09**|**Emergent superconductivity upon disordering a topological insulator**|Carlos Eduardo S. P. Corsino, Hermann Freire, Anurag Banerjee|[[arxiv:2607.07163](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07163)]|This paper investigates how disorder can enhance superconductivity in a topological insulator. Using quantum Monte Carlo simulations, the authors model a quantum spin Hall insulator with varying impurity densities and attractive interactions. They find that impurities significantly reduce the interaction strength needed for superconductivity, with pairing initially nucleating in bound states around the impurities before forming a global superconducting phase. The results highlight a mechanism where impurity-generated bound states, leveraging quantum geometry, promote superconductivity, with implications for flat-band systems like moiré materials.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Universal purification dynamics of monitored Clifford circuits**|Beatrice Magni, Federico Gerbino, Xhek Turkeshi, Andrea De Luca|[[arxiv:2607.06683](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06683)]|This paper examines the purification dynamics of monitored Clifford circuits. The authors show that, in the scaling limit, purification simplifies to a tractable Markovian death process for the density-matrix rank, bypassing the replica trick typically required. They derive exact universal scaling functions for all Rényi entropies, which collapse onto a single curve, and confirm this with stabilizer simulations. The results reveal unique features of Clifford circuits, including finite entropy fluctuations at short times and a periodic modulation in time, distinguishing them from generic monitored systems.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Athermality of generalized Gibbs ensembles**|Riccardo Senese, Bruno Bertini, Katja Klobas, Pasquale Calabrese|[[arxiv:2606.30762](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30762)]|This paper addresses the problem of quantifying how generalized Gibbs ensembles (GGEs) differ from conventional thermal Gibbs ensembles in integrable quantum systems. The authors use the concept of athermality, defined as the relative entropy between a GGE and its closest thermal state, as a quantitative tool. Computing athermality for several quenches in models like the XXZ spin chain and Lieb-Liniger gas, they find it becomes anomalously small when the post-quench Hamiltonian is at a quantum critical point, even at finite energy density. The significance lies in proving that athermality develops a singularity at criticality, revealing a direct link between finite-energy GGE behavior and ground-state quantum phase transitions.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Activation in Vesicle-Mediated Signaling Shaped by Batch Arrival Statistics**|Jan Hauke (Heidelberg University), Julian B. Voits (Heidelberg University), Ulrich S. Schwarz (Heidelberg University)|[[arxiv:2605.06456](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06456)]|This paper addresses how the stochastic nature of vesicle-mediated signaling, where bursts of transmitters are released and then degraded, influences downstream activation. The authors develop an exact mathematical solution for the time-dependent probability distribution of transmitter concentration using generating functions and recursion. A key finding is that activation timing depends critically on the temporal statistics of release events and batch-size variability, not just the mean dynamics, meaning different arrival processes with identical rates can yield distinct outcomes. The significance lies in establishing a clear link between stochastic release dynamics and activation timing, with implications for understanding neurotransmission and hormone release.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Raman Spectroscopic Investigation of Kitaev Quantum Spin Liquids**|Vivek Kumar, Pradeep Kumar|[[arxiv:2602.09709](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.09709)]|This review article discusses the use of Raman spectroscopy to probe Kitaev quantum spin liquids (QSLs), a topologically entangled state with no long-range magnetic order. The authors address the problem that real Kitaev materials exhibit non-Kitaev interactions, disorder, and other factors that complicate experimental validation of the theoretical model. They summarize key Raman signatures of Kitaev QSLs, including quasi-elastic scattering, phonon anomalies, and Fano asymmetry, along with the effects of polarization, magnetic fields, and pressure. The significance lies in establishing Raman spectroscopy as a powerful experimental tool to identify and characterize Kitaev physics in candidate materials such as honeycomb iridates and ruthenates.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Bose-Hubbard model with power-law hopping in one dimension**|Tanul Gupta, Nikolay V. The key finding is that for long-range hopping with $1 < \alpha < 3$, the superfluid-to-Mott insulator transition remains in the same universality class, but this class is distinct from the short-range case ($\alpha > 3$ ). This conclusion is supported by finite-size scaling of the superfluid stiffness and analysis of dynamical and correlation-length exponents. The results provide a reliable benchmark for theoretical models and are directly relevant to experiments with cold atoms, molecules, and ion chains.|
|**2026-07-09**|**Bounds on eigenstate thermalization**|Shoki Sugimoto, Ryusuke Hamazaki, Masahito Ueda|[[arxiv:2303.10069](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.10069)]|The paper addresses the problem of whether the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) holds for all few-body operators in chaotic many-body quantum systems. The authors formulate a conjecture that for generic nonintegrable systems, ETH is valid simultaneously for all m-body operators, with deviations bounded by specific constants dependent on system parameters. Using random-matrix theory and large-scale numerical simulations of spin, Bose, and Fermi systems, they provide evidence supporting this conjecture. The results imply that generic systems satisfy ETH for all few-body operators, including thermal and quantum fluctuations, which is crucial for justifying statistical mechanics in isolated quantum systems.|

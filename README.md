## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|

|**2026-06-08**|**The quantum group structure of long-range integrable deformations**|Koen Schouten, Marius de Leeuw|[[arxiv:2604.27189](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27189)]|This paper addresses the missing quantum group description for long-range deformations of integrable spin chains. The authors show that these deformations up to first order result from a twist of the underlying quantum group algebra, creating a non-associative structure with a Drinfeld associator that encodes the long-range interactions. Key results include proving this twisted algebra has a perturbatively associative substructure, ensuring integrability, and deriving explicit deformed Lax operators and R-matrices. The significance lies in providing a systematic, algebraic framework for these deformations, which were previously understood only at the level of commuting charges.|
|**2026-06-08**|**The Lee-Yang model and its generalizations through the lens of long-range deformations**|Fanny Eustachon|[[arxiv:2603.27031](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27031)]|The paper examines the conjecture that non-unitary conformal minimal models in two dimensions arise as fixed points of scalar field theories with complex interactions. The authors test this by perturbatively studying two long-range constructions based on the minimal model and its Landau-Ginzburg formalism. They find inconsistencies for \(m>2\) when relating the two constructions, but the special case \(m=2\), the long-range Lee-Yang model, behaves analogously to the long-range Ising model. The results clarify the validity of the conjecture and highlight the special status of the Lee-Yang model among such theories.|

|**2026-06-08**|**The massless sine-Gordon model with logarithmic correlations in arbitrary dimension**|Sami Vihko|[[arxiv:2606.06657](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06657)]|This paper studies the massless sine-Gordon model with logarithmic correlations in arbitrary spatial dimensions, treating it as a Euclidean field theory. The authors construct the field and its correlation functions without imposing boundary conditions, using a non-perturbative renormalization approach based on a scale decomposition and the iterated Mayer expansion. Key results include the renormalizability of the partition function and the validity of the construction for all real coupling constants, up to specific thresholds in \(\beta\) that depend on the dimension. This work extends rigorous analysis of the sine-Gordon model to higher dimensions and the massless regime, moving beyond previous results limited to \(d=2\).|
|**2026-06-08**|**The quantum group structure of long-range integrable deformations**|Koen Schouten, Marius de Leeuw|[[arxiv:2604.27189](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27189)]|This paper addresses the problem of identifying the underlying algebraic structure of long-range deformations in quantum integrable spin chains. The authors provide a quantum group-theoretical description for these deformations up to first order in the deformation parameter, showing they arise from a twist of the standard quantum group. A key result is that this twist produces a non-associative algebra with a Drinfeld associator that encodes long-range interaction terms, while a large associative substructure ensures perturbative integrability. The significance lies in offering explicit deformed Lax operators and R-matrices that satisfy the Yang-Baxter equation, thus connecting long-range models to established quantum group theory.|

|**2026-06-08**|**Quantum critical properties of non-Hermitian XY models with magnetic field**|Jia-Jia Luo, Volker Meden|[[arxiv:2606.07275](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07275)]|This paper examines quantum critical properties in non-Hermitian XY spin chains with a magnetic field, addressing ambiguity due to non-unique definitions of states and expectation values. Using exact solutions, the authors analyze energy, magnetization, and correlation function asymptotics, comparing results from both standard and biorthogonal quantum mechanics. They find that critical properties, including the phase diagram, depend on the chosen formalism and ground-state analog, recommending standard quantum mechanics. |**2026-06-08**|**Failure of the Quench Action Formalism for Mott Insulator Initial States**|Garry Goldstein|[[arxiv:2606.07111](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07111)]|The paper identifies a critical problem in the quench action formalism, which assumes that the overlap between an initial state and an integrable model's eigenstate depends smoothly on quasiparticle density. Using a Mott insulator to Lieb-Liniger gas quench as a counterexample, the authors demonstrate that this overlap can be highly singular, invalidating the assumed smooth functional form. The key results, derived in the Tonks-Girardeau limit and via a 1/c expansion, show that the overlap cannot be expressed as an exponential of a smooth function of density. This finding challenges a foundational assumption of the quench action formalism, with implications for its applicability in out-of-equilibrium quantum systems.|
|**2026-06-08**|**Higher-order Symmetric Quantum Mpemba Effect in Fragmented Systems**|Sreemayee Aditya, Sara Murciano, Xhek Turkeshi|[[arxiv:2606.06653](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06653)]|This work investigates whether the quantum Mpemba effect—where a system restores a broken symmetry faster when initially breaking it more strongly—persists in systems with strong Hilbert-space fragmentation. Using random circuits, Hamiltonians, and an exactly solvable dissipative model with simultaneous charge and dipole conservation, the authors identify a "higher-order symmetric quantum Mpemba effect" where the asymmetries for charge and dipole each exhibit Mpemba-like crossings on distinct timescales. The mechanism is traced to the separation of Hilbert space into frozen and active Krylov sectors: frozen fragments retain finite asymmetry that prevents full restoration, while active ones drive the relaxation and crossing behavior. These findings show that fragmentation does not eliminate the Mpemba effect but instead reshapes it, offering a framework for understanding symmetry restoration in constrained systems.|
|**2026-06-08**|**Non-Hermitian Crystalline Braid Topology from Hermitian Projection: A Zero-Mode Resonance Mechanism**|Stefan {\DJ}or{\dj}evi\'c, Vladimir Juri\v{c}i\'c|[[arxiv:2606.06626](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06626)]|This paper proposes a mechanism for generating non-Hermitian topological phases through projection alone, starting from a fully Hermitian and topologically trivial parent lattice. The key method is zero-mode-resonant projection, where coupling to a zero mode in the eliminated subsystem creates a singular self-energy and enables finite-frequency topological behavior in the projected Green's function. Using an exactly solvable square lattice model with an embedded zig-zag brane, the authors show that the complex spectrum forms an abelian two-band braid topology, identified by a quantized complex Berry phase due to conjugated pseudo-Hermiticity. The significance lies in demonstrating that non-Hermitian crystalline braid topology can emerge without gain, loss, or asymmetric couplings, and that the topological transitions appear as observable transmission zeros in topolectrical circuits.|

Using tensor network simulations, the authors confirm that the DQCP remains stable for high phonon frequencies but becomes strongly first-order below a critical frequency due to spin-phonon interactions reducing the Luttinger parameter. The effective theory for this behavior is identified as the double sine-Gordon model, with the critical endpoint belonging to the four-state Potts universality class. The study provides quantitative predictions for the phonon spectral function, offering experimental signatures to probe DQCP-phonon coupling in low-dimensional materials.|

The key result is that strong ETH can emerge even without classical chaos, solely from mean-ergodicity. This challenges the conventional view that strong ETH requires chaotic dynamics, deepening our understanding of thermalization in quantum systems.|

|**2026-06-08**|**The Lee-Yang model and its generalizations through the lens of long-range deformations**|Fanny Eustachon|[[arxiv:2603.27031](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27031)]|This paper tests the conjecture that non-unitary conformal minimal models arise as fixed points of complex scalar field theories.

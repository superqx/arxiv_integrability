|**2026-04-27**|**Trace estimates and improved pointwise bounds for joint eigenfunctions**|Xianchao Wu, Xiao Xiao|[[arxiv:2604.22197](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22197)]|This paper addresses the problem of obtaining sharp pointwise bounds for joint eigenfunctions in quantum integrable systems. The authors improve upon prior work by establishing a bound of \(h^{\frac{-n+k+1}{2}}\) for points with a rank \(k\) non-degeneracy condition. This result is sharp and provides a clearer understanding of eigenfunction concentration, refining previous polynomial improvements to the standard Hörmander bounds.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Random matrix theory of integrability-to-chaos transition**|Ben Craps, Marine De Clerck, Oleg Evnin, Maxim Pavlov|[[arxiv:2604.03669](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03669)]|This paper addresses the lack of quantitative understanding of the transitional level spacing distributions between quantum integrability and chaos. The authors propose that these distributions are governed by the statistics of perturbation Hamiltonian matrix elements in the integrable basis. Using this insight, they construct a random matrix ensemble that accurately reproduces the level spacing distributions across multiple test systems. The key finding is a surprising universality in the matrix element distributions—they follow simple power laws across diverse physical systems.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Exact State Evolution and Energy Spectrum in Solvable Bosonic Models**|Valery Shchesnovich|[[arxiv:2510.20046](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.20046)]|This paper addresses the problem of exactly solving the time evolution of arbitrary initial states in a broad class of solvable bosonic models, such as those describing optical down-conversion. The authors present an exact analytic solution for state evolution and derive the characteristic equation for the energy spectrum. They find eigenstates represented as continued fractions and as principal minors of the associated Jacobi matrix. The work provides a foundational analytical framework for studying exactly solvable bosonic models in quantum optics.|

|**2026-04-27**|**The antiferromagnetic Chern insulator phase in the Kane-Mele-Hubbard model**|Bao-Qing Wang, Can Shao, Takami Tohyama, Hong-Gang Luo, Hantao Lu|[[arxiv:2604.22329](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22329)]|This paper investigates whether the antiferromagnetic Chern insulator (AFCI) phase can exist in the Kane-Mele-Hubbard (KMH) model with a sublattice potential, despite time-reversal symmetry. Using exact diagonalization, the study analyzes excitation gaps, anisotropic antiferromagnetic correlations, and fidelity susceptibility to find consistent evidence for the AFCI phase. The key result is that the spin Chern number calculation reveals a breakdown of adiabatic continuity, indicating time-reversal symmetry breaking from Hubbard-induced antiferromagnetic perturbations. The significance lies in proposing a modified computational scheme that yields a robust quantized Chern number, confirming the AFCI phase's existence in this model.|
|**2026-04-27**|**QAssemble: A Pure Python Package for Quantum Many-Body Theory**|Seongjun Mo, Dongming Li, Mancheon Han, Johan J\"onsson, Byungkyun Kang, Hoonkyung Lee, Gabriel Kotliar, Sangkook Choi|[[arxiv:2604.22223](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22223)]|QAssemble is a pure-Python package designed to address the quantum many-body problem by implementing functional approaches like tight-binding, Hartree-Fock, and GW approximations within a modular, object-oriented architecture. The method represents physical concepts as distinct classes and uses vectorized kernels combined with the discrete Lehmann representation to achieve practical efficiency. Key results include validation on graphene's electronic structure and a 60x speedup over traditional loop-based Matsubara implementations on a five-orbital Hund-Hubbard model. This work demonstrates that a pure-Python framework can effectively balance code clarity, extensibility, and computational performance for quantum many-body simulations.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Beyond Variational Bias: Resolving Intertwined Orders in the Hubbard Model**|Luciano Loris Viteritti, Riccardo Rende, Christopher Roth, Anirvan Sengupta, Giuseppe Carleo, Antoine Georges|[[arxiv:2604.21978](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21978)]|The problem addressed is the conflicting predictions for the ground state of the doped Hubbard model due to competition between ordered phases. Using three different Transformer-based wave functions initialized without mean-field bias, the method achieves nearly identical, state-of-the-art energies but finds qualitatively different spin, charge, and pairing correlations. Only by systematically improving accuracy through symmetry restoration and variance reduction do all three converge on the same physical picture of coexisting superconducting and stripe orders. This demonstrates that variational energy alone is misleading for identifying ground states in systems with competing phases, emphasizing the need to monitor correlation functions as accuracy improves.|
|**2026-04-27**|**d-Wave pair density wave superconductivity in a two-orbital model**|Samuel Vadnais, Arun Paramekanti|[[arxiv:2604.07511](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07511)]|This paper introduces a two-orbital model to explore superconductivity in multi-orbital systems. Using random phase approximation and a bosonic Gutzwiller ansatz, the authors identify a regime where an incommensurate \(d\)-wave pair density wave (PDW) state emerges, driven by interband pairing. The study shows that this PDW state competes with uniform \(d\)-wave superconductivity and charge density waves, stabilizing a period-2 PDW across various fillings. The findings underscore the importance of orbital structure and multiband Fermi surfaces in realizing PDW superconductivity in correlated materials.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Random matrix theory of integrability-to-chaos transition**|Ben Craps, Marine De Clerck, Oleg Evnin, Maxim Pavlov|[[arxiv:2604.03669](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03669)]|This paper addresses the gap in quantitative understanding of the transitional level spacing distributions between quantum integrability and chaos. The authors propose a random matrix ensemble based on the statistics of perturbation Hamiltonian matrix elements in the integrable eigenbasis. Their model accurately reproduces the level spacing distributions across various test systems and reveals a surprising universal feature, namely, that these matrix element distributions are dominated by simple power laws. The work provides a quantitative framework for the integrability-to-chaos transition, which had previously lacked a unified theoretical description.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Functional renormalization group for extremely correlated electrons**|Jonas Arnold, Peter Kopietz, Andreas R\"uckriegel|[[arxiv:2512.15437](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.15437)]|The paper tackles the problem of understanding strongly correlated electrons in the Hubbard model at infinite on-site repulsion, where double occupancy is forbidden. Using a strong-coupling functional renormalization group method, the authors analyze the strict $U=\infty$ limit, focusing on kinematic interactions from Hilbert space projection. Key results show a significant renormalization of the electronic spectrum, including bandwidth reduction and the emergence of a polaronic continuum, with Fermi liquid behavior only at low densities and violations of Luttinger's theorem in paramagnetic and ferromagnetic regimes. This work is significant for advancing the description of extreme correlations beyond perturbative approaches, linking bad metallic behavior to magnetic ordering tendencies.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Free Majorana Fermions with Superconducting Quantum Wires and a Magnetic Impurity**|Karyn Le Hur|[[arxiv:2511.03380](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03380)]|This paper studies a magnetic spin-1/2 impurity coupled to the edge of a Luther-Emery liquid, a system with a spin gap in the bulk. Key findings include the derivation of the bound state wavefunction and the identification of a specific pairing mechanism for these Majorana fermions in the bulk. The work is significant because it proposes a practical realization of these "free Majorana fermions" using a magnetic impurity bridging two s-wave superconducting wires, suggesting a new, potentially more robust platform for Majorana-based quantum computation.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Kubo-Martin-Schwinger relation for energy eigenstates of SU(2)-symmetric quantum many-body systems**|Jae Dong Noh, Aleksander Lasek, Jade LeSchack, Nicole Yunger Halpern|[[arxiv:2507.07249](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.07249)]|This paper extends the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) to systems with non-Abelian SU(2) symmetry to derive a Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) relation for their energy eigenstates. The method relies on a proposed non-Abelian ETH, which accounts for conflicts between non-Abelian symmetries and standard thermalization assumptions. Key results show that the finite-size corrections to the KMS relation scale inversely with system size under typical conditions, but can grow polynomially larger in other cases, as supported by numerical simulations of a Heisenberg chain. This work is significant for advancing the understanding of how non-Abelian symmetries alter conventional thermodynamics in nonequilibrium quantum systems.|
|**2026-04-27**|**Near-Term Fermionic Simulation with Subspace Noise Tailored Quantum Error Mitigation**|Miha Papi\v{c}, Manuel G. |**2026-04-27**|**Time-dependent Neural Galerkin Method for Quantum Dynamics**|Alessandro Sinibaldi, Douglas Hendry, Filippo Vicentini, Giuseppe Carleo|[[arxiv:2412.11778](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.11778)]|This paper introduces a classical computational method for quantum dynamics using a global-in-time variational principle. Applied to the Transverse-Field Ising model, the method reveals ergodicity breaking and a lack of thermalization in two dimensions over long timescales. This approach is competitive with existing variational methods and enables the study of previously inaccessible long-time dynamical regimes in strongly interacting quantum systems.|

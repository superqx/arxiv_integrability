|**2026-05-27**|**Long-range deformations in Gaussian States**|Francisco Pereira, Nandagopal Manoj, Sara Murciano|[[arxiv:2605.26932](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26932)]|This paper studies how long-range interactions in imaginary-time evolution can alter the constraints on phase transitions in one-dimensional systems. Starting from the Kitaev Majorana chain ground state, the authors apply a Gaussian deformation with power-law decaying couplings ( $1/r^{\alpha}$) and analytically compute correlations and entanglement. They discover three distinct regimes: for $\alpha > 1$, only smooth crossovers occur, while $\alpha = 1$ induces an immediate flow to the topological phase, and $\alpha < 1$ shows critical-like behavior at all deformation strengths. The key finding is that even with arbitrarily long-range interactions, no sharp phase transition emerges at non-zero deformation strength.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Are Petrov type-N and D spacetimes admitting CTCs valid in $f(R,\mathcal{L}_m,\Phi,X)$ gravity?**|Faizuddin Ahmed, Ahmad Al-Badawi, \.Izzet Sakall{\i}|[[arxiv:2605.26696](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26696)]|This paper investigates whether two known time-machine spacetimes, the Ori metric and a generalized Misner space, remain valid solutions in \(f(R,\mathcal{L}_m,\Phi,X)\) gravity, a modified theory coupling curvature, matter, and a scalar field. Using the specific model \(f = R + \mathcal{L}_m + (\lambda/2)X\) with a harmonic scalar field, the authors derive the field equations and compute curvature invariants and kinetic terms for both backgrounds. They find that both metrics satisfy the modified field equations with anisotropic matter, and the closed timelike curves (CTCs) persist without being removed by the scalar degree of freedom. The study demonstrates that this modified gravity does not impose chronology protection, providing a consistency test for scalar-extended theories in non-globally-hyperbolic spacetimes.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis with projective representation**|Soma Onoda, Osamu Fukushima, Ryusuke Hamazaki, Okuto Morikawa|[[arxiv:2509.01931](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.01931)]|This paper addresses the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (ETH) for isolated quantum systems with degenerate energy eigenstates due to symmetries, specifically focusing on 't Hooft anomalies that lead to nontrivial projective representations of Abelian symmetries. The authors propose a reformulation of the ETH for such degenerate systems, showing that the stationary values of observables are described by a generalized Gibbs ensemble rather than the standard Gibbs ensemble. Key findings reveal that the projective structure induced by anomalies necessitates this broader thermal description to account for symmetry-charged sectors. The work advances the understanding of quantum thermalization by clarifying how symmetry-induced degeneracy and projective representations influence the approach to equilibrium.|

Batchelor|[[arxiv:2605.26813](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26813)]|This paper addresses how non-Hermitian free-fermion systems behave, using the XY spin chain with complex anisotropy as a case study. The authors solve the model by constructing biorthogonal fermionic bases and using Chebyshev polynomials to represent eigenstates, showing that the quasi-energy spectrum retains its Hermitian free-fermion structure. Key results include deriving the Jordan normal form and generalized eigenvectors at exceptional points, where the system becomes defective, and demonstrating that these points act as branch points causing eigenstate permutation upon encircling them. The work provides an analytically controlled platform for studying exceptional point physics and non-Hermitian topology in many-body systems, moving beyond typical momentum-space approaches.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-05-27**|**Long-range deformations in Gaussian States**|Francisco Pereira, Nandagopal Manoj, Sara Murciano|[[arxiv:2605.26932](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26932)]|This paper investigates how long-range interactions affect phase transitions in one-dimensional quantum systems, focusing on the Kitaev Majorana chain. The authors apply imaginary-time evolution with a quadratic Hamiltonian featuring power-law couplings (1/r^α) to the ground state, analytically computing correlation functions for the resulting Gaussian states. The results reveal that the decay exponent α controls distinct regimes: crossovers for α>1, an immediate flow to a topological phase at α=1, and critical-like behavior for α<1. The study demonstrates that even with arbitrarily long-range interactions, the system does not exhibit a sharp phase transition at non-zero deformation strength, highlighting the robustness of one-dimensional constraints.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Self-Consistent Spectral Quadrature Approach to Many-Body Green Functions**|Stanislav Yu. Kruchinin|[[arxiv:2605.26887](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26887)]|This paper introduces a self-consistent spectral quadrature (sc-SQ) framework for computing many-body Green functions. The method uses Gauss-Christoffel quadrature to represent the spectral measure, ensuring positivity and exact reproduction of spectral moments, with a singular-value decomposition criterion to determine the optimal pole order. By enforcing self-consistency between the spectral function used for expectation values and the quadrature reconstruction, the approach connects to established methods like Hartree-Fock and captures non-perturbative features. Benchmarked against numerical renormalization group results for the Anderson impurity model and Hubbard model, the method successfully reproduces multi-peak spectra and Mott-gap formation, demonstrating its utility for correlated electron systems.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Exact Solution for Non-Hermitian Free Fermions: A Case Study of the XY Chain**|Yuguan Li, D. C. Liu, Murray T. Batchelor|[[arxiv:2605.26813](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26813)]|This paper studies the non-Hermitian XY spin chain, where the anisotropy parameter takes complex values. The authors derive exact solutions by exploiting the system’s free-fermion structure and using a Chebyshev-polynomial representation of eigenvectors. At exceptional points (EPs), where the Hamiltonian becomes defective, they construct generalized eigenvectors via differentiation and derive the Jordan normal form, correctly counting independent many-body states. The findings establish EPs as branch points that cause eigenstate permutations upon encirclement, providing a tractable many-body model for exploring non-Hermitian topology beyond standard momentum-space approaches.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Scar Full Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis**|Ning Sun, Yanting Cheng|[[arxiv:2605.26389](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26389)]|The paper addresses the failure of the standard eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) in systems with quantum many-body scars, which host non-thermal eigenstates. The authors formulate a "scar full ETH" to capture correlations among matrix elements involving these scar states, using typicality arguments to establish scaling forms and factorization properties. They numerically validate this framework in the PXP model. This work provides a systematic understanding of correlations in scarred systems, organizing multi-time correlation functions in terms of both thermal and scar cumulants.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Extended Bose-Hubbard Model on Small Grids: Exact Diagonalization and Monte Carlo Studies**|Gabriele Costa, Matteo Ciardi, Fabio Cinti, Santi Prestipino|[[arxiv:2605.26303](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26303)]|This paper studies the superfluid-insulator transition in small, truncated lattices, using the hard-core extended Bose-Hubbard model on square and triangular grids of up to 13 sites. The authors employ exact diagonalization to map the zero-temperature phase diagram, revealing ground-state characteristics that differ significantly from those found in the thermodynamic limit, despite some similarities. They then use path-integral Monte Carlo simulations for a few interacting bosons in a continuum with confining potentials, mimicking a 3x3 grid, to analyze superfluidity and energy. The key result is that signatures of the Bose-Hubbard behavior, such as the dichotomy between superfluid and insulating states, persist even in systems as small as five particles.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Slave-boson Formalism for Superconducting Pairing at Strong Coupling**|Sarbajit Mazumdar, Jonas Issing, Jannis Seufert, David Riegler, Peter W\"olfle, Ronny Thomale, Michael Klett|[[arxiv:2605.26233](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26233)]|This paper applies the spin-rotation-invariant Kotliar-Ruckenstein slave-boson approach to study superconductivity in the one-band Hubbard model. The method constructs an effective pairing vertex from dynamic fluctuations around the saddle point, capturing strong correlation effects. Solving the gap equation on a square lattice reveals pairing instabilities across doping, interaction, and temperature, yielding gap structures that qualitatively match cuprate observations. The significance lies in merging strong-correlation renormalizations with transparent RPA-type pairing, offering a scalable framework for modeling multi-orbital superconductivity at strong coupling.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Conserving relaxation-time approximation for electron-electron collisions**|Sayak Bhattacharjee, Tatia Kiliptari, Dmitrii L. Maslov|[[arxiv:2605.26207](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26207)]|The paper presents a **conserving relaxation-time approximation (cRTA)** that enforces conservation laws and uses an energy-resolved projection to properly capture transport phenomena near the Fermi surface. The method is benchmarked against exact solutions for various Fermi liquids, including dc and optical conductivity in both Galilean and non-Galilean invariant systems. Key results show quantitative agreement at the percent level with exact low- and high-temperature limits, as well as hydrodynamic and collisionless regimes. This work provides a simple yet highly accurate framework for simulating transport beyond standard approximations.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Exact strong zero modes are generic in integrable spin systems with large anisotropy**|Sascha Gehrmann|[[arxiv:2605.26205](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26205)]|This paper addresses the lack of a unified framework for exact strong zero modes (ESZMs) in integrable spin systems. The author shows that ESZMs arise generically from two algebraic properties of the R- and K-matrices: quasi-periodicity in the spectral parameter and tracelessness. This model-independent mechanism recovers known ESZM examples, like the XXZ chain, and predicts ESZMs in previously unrecognized models. The work provides a formal basis for understanding edge coherence in a broad class of anisotropic integrable spin systems.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Quantum Utility in Simulating the Real-time Dynamics of the Fermi-Hubbard Model using Superconducting Quantum Computers**|Talal Ahmed Chowdhury, Vladimir Korepin, Vincent R. Pascuzzi, Kwangmin Yu|[[arxiv:2509.14196](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.14196)]|The paper addresses the challenge of simulating the real-time dynamics of the Fermi-Hubbard model, a key model in condensed matter physics. The authors use IBM's superconducting quantum computers with over 100 qubits, implementing scalable first- and second-order Trotterization schemes with constant circuit depth per step. They successfully measure the N\'eel observable (staggered magnetization) over extended time scales, showcasing the system's relaxation dynamics. This demonstrates "quantum utility," showing that superconducting quantum platforms outperform classical approximation methods for large-scale, entangled quantum many-body systems.|
|**2026-05-27**|**Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis with projective representation**|Soma Onoda, Osamu Fukushima, Ryusuke Hamazaki, Okuto Morikawa|[[arxiv:2509.01931](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.01931)]|This paper addresses the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (ETH) in quantum systems with degenerate energy eigenstates caused by symmetries, specifically focusing on nontrivial projective representations of Abelian symmetries that arise from 't Hooft anomalies. The authors extend the standard ETH by formulating a "projective-representation ETH" that accounts for such degeneracies in excited states. They find that when operators carry projective charges, their stationary values are described by a generalized Gibbs ensemble rather than the standard Gibbs one. This work clarifies the role of symmetry and degeneracy in quantum thermalization, offering a framework for understanding thermalization in systems with anomalous symmetries.|

The authors present these systems as a means to explore a less-studied class of integrable equations with reversed time-like flows. The key result is the successful derivation of exact solutions for these moving boundary problems, which are shown to be governed by particular Painlevé II transcendents.

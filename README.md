|**2026-07-24**|**Entanglement asymmetry and quantum Mpemba effect for Kramers-Wannier duality**|Milo Vescovo, Pasquale Calabrese, Filiberto Ares|[[arxiv:2607.21226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.21226)]|This paper introduces the Kramers-Wannier entanglement asymmetry as a quantum-information metric to study the breaking of the non-invertible Kramers-Wannier symmetry in the transverse-field Ising chain. The authors show that in equilibrium, this asymmetry reveals a sharp crossover between ordered and disordered phases, with a distinct dip at the critical point. Dynamically, after quantum quenches to criticality, the asymmetry decays to zero, and the system exhibits a quantum Mpemba effect where states initially farther from equilibrium restore the symmetry faster. These findings demonstrate the utility of entanglement asymmetry for probing non-invertible symmetries in and out of equilibrium.|
|**2026-07-24**|**Massive Gravity @ 15**|Claudia de Rham|[[arxiv:2607.15507](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15507)]|This paper reviews the current state of massive gravity, a theory that modifies gravity at large distances, originally proposed to address the cosmological constant problem. The key development is a ghost-free, Lorentz-invariant formulation that propagates exactly five degrees of freedom, raises the strong-coupling scale, and automatically includes the Vainshtein mechanism to ensure consistency with local gravity tests. The paper highlights recent insights from effective field theory constraints, which single out the ghost-free theory as a unique infrared realization, as well as its emerging connections to black-hole entanglement and computable \(T\bar T\) deformations. Its significance lies in providing a controlled theoretical framework for studying the interplay between observational phenomenology and possible ultraviolet completions in modifications of General Relativity.|
|**2026-07-24**|**Phase-dependent kink collisions and dual critical-velocity branches in the complex sine-Gordon model**|Mohammad Mohammadi, Farnaz Eizadbaksh, Vahideh Bagheri|[[arxiv:2607.08752](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08752)]|The paper investigates how the internal phase degree of freedom in the complex sine-Gordon (CSG) model alters the collision dynamics of its solitary waves. Using numerical simulations, the authors study kink–kink collisions and map the final state as a function of initial velocity and relative phase. Key results reveal two distinct, phase-dependent branches of critical velocity: one where increasing velocity promotes capture and another where it restores scattering, alongside behaviors such as bion formation and radiative emission. This dual structure demonstrates the rich velocity–phase interplay and suggests the relative phase acts as an effective internal degree of freedom, significantly influencing complex soliton collisions.|
|**2026-07-24**|**Integrable systems from Poisson reductions of generalized Hamiltonian torus actions**|L. Feher, M. Key results show that under sufficient conditions, such systems reduce to integrable (and often superintegrable) systems on dense open subsets of M/K, providing new examples from reductions on doubles of compact Lie groups. The significance lies in solving open integrability problems for systems on cotangent bundles, Heisenberg doubles, and moduli spaces of flat connections.|

Vaidya|[[arxiv:2607.20983](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20983)]|This paper investigates Lax pairs for two-dimensional isotropic harmonic and anharmonic oscillators. The authors find that the superintegrable harmonic oscillator admits a 4x4 block-form Lax pair with a spectral parameter, yielding two commuting conserved mode energies, and also a 2x2 Lax pair whose conserved quantities are not all in involution. For the quartic anharmonic oscillator, they construct a family of su(2) Lax pairs and r-matrices, extending this to oscillators with a rotational energy. |**2026-07-24**|**Integrable systems from Poisson reductions of generalized Hamiltonian torus actions**|L. Feher, M. Fairon|[[arxiv:2507.12051](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.12051)]|This paper develops a method for reducing integrable systems with a symmetry group \(K\) to integrable systems on the quotient space \(M/K\). The approach requires the unreduced system on \(M\) to have "action variables" generating a proper, effective action of a torus \(U(1)^{\ell_1} \times \mathbb{R}^{\ell_2}\), which also descend to action variables after reduction. Key results include showing the reduced systems are generally superintegrable and applying the method to solve open integrability problems for systems derived from reductions on compact Lie group doubles. This work has broad significance for constructing and proving integrability for systems on moduli spaces of flat connections via quasi-Poisson methods.|

The method maps the altermagnetic band splitting from spin to $\eta$-pseudospin in bipartite attractive Hubbard models, defining $\eta$-altermagnetism ($\eta$-ALM) in the Bogoliubov-de Gennes framework. Key results show that anisotropic hopping breaks Kramers degeneracy, producing $\eta$-pseudospin splitting with even- and odd-parity structures, and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov computations on checkerboard and honeycomb lattices reveal $p$-, $d$-, and $f$-wave splitting patterns. In quantum quenches to criticality, the asymmetry decays to zero, revealing a quantum Mpemba effect where states initially further from equilibrium restore the symmetry faster. The authors show analytically that introducing frustrating next-nearest-neighbor hopping triggers a transition to a long-wavelength spiral state, determined by solving an effective two-body hole-magnon scattering problem. The authors provide both classical and quantum algorithms that return a Slater determinant with fidelity within ε of the maximum, running in time polynomial in the number of modes m and exponential in the number of fermions n. Key results include matching hardness lower bounds under standard complexity conjectures, a proof that quantum copies enable a polynomially efficient solution, and the discovery that fidelity above 2/3 ensures a unique global maximum while below this threshold spurious stationary points appear. The authors project a localized orbital's Hubbard interaction onto a flat band, producing a model with a momentum-space interaction scale. Key results from exact diagonalization and density matrix renormalization group methods show that adding antiferromagnetic coupling stabilizes the Mott semimetal, which hosts a spinful Dirac fermion alongside a neutral spin mode, analogous to a fractionalized Fermi liquid. The study provides the first unbiased evidence of a Mott semimetal ground state and demonstrates that this 1D model captures essential aspects of twisted bilayer graphene physics.|

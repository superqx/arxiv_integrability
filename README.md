|**2026-06-30**|**Generalized Hermite Polynomials and Spectral Degeneracies of a Singular Sextic Oscillator**|Davide Guzzetti, Dmitrii Rachenkov|[[arxiv:2606.30357](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30357)]|The paper investigates a quasi-exactly solvable singular sextic oscillator and its algebraic spectrum. The authors prove that the discriminant of the characteristic polynomial factors into three components, with one component being the square of a generalized Hermite polynomial \( H_{mn} \), whose zeros correspond to poles of a rational solution to the fourth Painlevé equation. This establishes that spectral degeneracies (level crossings) align with zeros of generalized Hermite polynomials, providing an exact Painlevé IV analogue of the Shapiro–Tater conjecture for the quartic oscillator. The results also characterize parameter values where the oscillator admits two quasi-polynomial eigenfunctions with opposite exponential behavior, and yield a new determinantal representation of \( H_{mn} \) as a resultant.|

|**2026-06-30**|**Ferromagnetic ordering in Hubbard models**|Wojciech Niedzi\'o{\l}ka, Jacek Wojtkiewicz|[[arxiv:2606.30607](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30607)]|This paper investigates whether the ground states of Hubbard models on frustrated lattices can exhibit ferromagnetic ordering. The authors apply a low-density expansion formula for ground-state energy, previously proven for the simple cubic lattice, to five frustrated lattices including the face-centered cubic one. Their key results confirm that most of these frustrated lattice models do have ferromagnetic ground states for moderate to low densities. The significance lies in showing that frustration strongly promotes ferromagnetism, even at low densities, suggesting that rigorous proof of "wealthy ferromagnetism" may be achievable in these cases.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Connecting Density Matrix Spectroscopy to Biexciton Entanglement Dynamics**|Yusuke Masaki, Takashi Otaki, Hiroaki Matsueda|[[arxiv:2606.30424](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30424)]|This paper addresses the unresolved connection between density matrix spectroscopy (DMS) measurements and the internal entanglement dynamics of a biexciton. The authors derive an analytical relationship linking the DMS-reconstructed density matrix of emitted photons to the actual biexciton state. Through numerical simulations using an extended ionic Hubbard model, they show that DMS partially captures biexciton entanglement, specifically tracking the difference between the biexciton's total entanglement entropy and its wavevector-dependent component. The findings validate DMS as a tool for probing biexciton entanglement dynamics, clarifying its interpretative power and limitations.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Trimers in the Extended Hubbard Model**|R. R. Montenegro-Filho, D. R. B. Silva, D. Cogollo, M. D. Coutinho-Filho|[[arxiv:2606.30176](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30176)]|This paper investigates the stability of the Lieb ferrimagnetic insulating phase in an extended Hubbard model on a trimer chain at half-filling. Using density matrix renormalization group calculations, the authors find that the ferrimagnetic phase remains robust against nearest-neighbor Coulomb repulsion until phase separation occurs near V ≳ U/4. Beyond this phase separation line, one of the coexisting phases is dominated by doublons on one sublattice, while the other phase is either a metallic unsaturated ferromagnet or a singlet phase depending on the local repulsion strength. The results challenge the universality of the Lieb theorem by identifying conditions where its insulating magnetic order destabilizes.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Vortex NOON states for rotation sensing**|Simon Dengis, Nathan Dupont, Peter Schlagheck, Nathan Goldman|[[arxiv:2606.29509](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29509)]|This paper proposes a method for generating NOON states using bosonic vortices in cold atoms confined within a weakly anisotropic two-dimensional harmonic trap. The authors address the problem of prohibitively slow formation times by developing two acceleration strategies: geodesic counterdiabatic driving for small particle numbers and resonance- or chaos-assisted tunneling for larger systems. They demonstrate that these states enable rotation sensing at the Heisenberg limit, the maximum precision allowed by quantum mechanics. This work is significant as it establishes a practical path toward high-precision rotation sensors using atomic NOON states in bosonic Josephson junctions.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Theory of High-Tc Superconductivity in Cuprates**|E. C. Marino|[[arxiv:2606.28906](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28906)]|The paper addresses the problem of high-temperature superconductivity in cuprates by focusing on electron dynamics within the CuO₂ planes, described by the Three Bands Hubbard Model. The author adopts the Spin-Fermion-Hubbard Model, which treats copper electrons as localized spins and oxygen holes as mobile particles with Kondo-like interactions, rather than the more common t-J Model. Key results demonstrate that this alternative approach successfully explains the mechanism of high-Tc superconductivity in hole-doped cuprates. The work is significant because it provides a validated theoretical framework, challenging mainstream simplifications and offering deeper insight into the underlying physics.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Theory of transmittance of narrow quantum wires intersection in 2D systems**|L. S. Braginsky, M. V. Entin|[[arxiv:2605.03530](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03530)]|This paper addresses the problem of electron transmission through narrow quantum wire intersections in two-dimensional systems. The method uses the fact that when wire widths are smaller than the electron wavelength, the Schrödinger equation can be approximated by the Laplace equation, allowing exact solutions via conformal mapping. Key results include the calculated transmittances for both T-shaped and X-shaped intersections, treating the wires as tunnel conductors. The significance lies in providing an exact analytical framework for understanding electron transport in bulged one-dimensional quantum wires.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Coupling-energy driven pumping through quantum dots: Role of coherences**|Lukas Litzba, Gernot Schaller, J\"urgen K\"onig, Nikodem Szpak|[[arxiv:2602.23099](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23099)]|The paper investigates electron pumping through quantum dots where low-order tunneling is suppressed and pumping is driven solely by modulations of the dot-lead coupling energy. The authors examine two specific setups: one using on-off coupling switching and another employing measurements of dot occupation. Exact solutions are derived for strong tunnel couplings without Coulomb interaction, identifying optimal regimes for pumping current and energy efficiency. The study highlights the role of quantum coherences and similarities between the two pumping mechanisms, advancing understanding of coupling-energy-driven quantum transport.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Condensate Fraction Scaling and Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless Transition of Superconductivity and Superfluidity**|Yuan-Yao He|[[arxiv:2505.17411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17411)]|This paper addresses the challenge of characterizing superfluid and superconducting transitions in two-dimensional systems. The authors establish the condensate fraction scaling as a highly accurate tool for determining Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transitions, using the 2D attractive Fermi-Hubbard model and large-scale auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo simulations. Key results show that the condensate fraction scales algebraically below the transition and exponentially above it, with significantly reduced finite-size effects compared to conventional measures. These findings provide an efficient method for precisely locating BKT transitions and uncovering specific heat anomalies, with broad applicability to 2D fermionic and bosonic superfluids and superconductors.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Thermal Entropy, Density Disorder and Antiferromagnetism of Repulsive Fermions in 3D Optical Lattice**|Yu-Feng Song, Youjin Deng, Yuan-Yao He|[[arxiv:2411.13418](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.13418)]|The paper resolves a discrepancy in recent optical lattice experiments on the 3D fermionic Hubbard model, where the antiferromagnetic structure factor peaked at a higher interaction strength than theoretically predicted. Using auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo simulations, the authors study the interplay of thermal entropy, density disorder, and antiferromagnetism. They find that the discrepancy is quantitatively explained by the entropy increase from strengthening interactions in experiments, combined with lattice density disorder. This work provides an accurate entropy phase diagram and predicts universal double occupancy behaviors for future experimental probes.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Emergent polaronic correlations in doped spin liquids**|Leyna Shackleton, Shiwei Zhang|[[arxiv:2408.02190](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.02190)]|This paper numerically investigates the spin and charge dynamics in doped Mott insulating quantum spin liquids, using correlated electronic wavefunctions that incorporate gauge fluctuations. The variational ansatz, optimized against the Hubbard model, reveals the emergence of magnetic polaron correlations. Key results show quantitative agreement between calculated spin-spin correlation functions and cold-atom simulations. The significance lies in demonstrating that polaronic correlations arise naturally from fractionalized spin liquid states upon doping.|

|**2026-06-30**|**Kinetic equations for a two-dimensional soliton gas**|Gino Biondini, Thibault Bonnemain, Benjamin Doyon, Gennady El, Giacomo Roberti|[[arxiv:2606.30582](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30582)]|This paper develops a general kinetic theory for a non-stationary two-dimensional gas of line solitons that interact elastically, applying it specifically to the Kadomtsev-Petviashvili II (KPII) equation. The theory is validated by comparing its predictions against direct numerical simulations for two test problems: the oblique collision of a KPII soliton with a 1D KdV soliton condensate, and the interaction of a trial KPII soliton with a monochromatic 2D KPII soliton gas. The simulations, which use exact large-N soliton solutions with random parameter distributions, show excellent agreement with the analytical predictions. This work establishes a foundational kinetic description for 2D non-equilibrium soliton gases, significantly extending prior 1D soliton gas theory.|
|**2026-06-30**|**Global Results on the Classification of Two-Component Integrable Evolutionary Systems**|Alexander V. Mikhailov, Vladimir Novikov, Jing Ping Wang|[[arxiv:2606.25802](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25802)]|This paper addresses the problem of classifying two-component polynomial evolutionary systems of odd order in (1+1) dimensions. The method involves deriving both necessary and sufficient integrability conditions by analyzing spectral invariants and infinite hierarchies of commuting symmetries. Key results show that there are exactly 24 possible spectral classes of integrable hierarchies, leading to a complete classification of homogeneous systems with third- and fifth-order equations. The significance lies in the clear division of these systems into C-integrable (reducible to linear-triangular form) and S-integrable (related to Drinfeld–Sokolov KdV-type systems) classes, providing a foundational framework for further study.|

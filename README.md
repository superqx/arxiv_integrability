Salazar, Alexei Andreanov, Dario Rosa|[[arxiv:2604.01320](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01320)]|This paper solves the L\'{e}vy Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (LSYK) model, a variant of the SYK model where the random couplings are drawn from a heavy-tailed L\'{e}vy distribution instead of a Gaussian one. Key results show the method can uniquely reconstruct a system's full symmetry group, including its character table and fusion rules, as demonstrated by recovering known symmetries in several model systems. Its significance lies in providing a practical, data-driven route to uncover symmetries directly from spectral observables, applicable to both chaotic and integrable systems.|

The significance is that this provides a mechanism for realizing long-range entangled conformal states through engineered dissipation in open quantum systems.|

|**2026-04-03**|**Exact Solution of Chandrasekhar's H Function For the Isotropic Case**|Fikret Anli|[[arxiv:2604.00068](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00068)]|This paper addresses the long-standing problem of finding an exact, closed-form solution for Chandrasekhar's H function in the specific case of isotropic scattering, a fundamental component in radiative transfer theory. The method transforms the governing nonlinear integral equation into a differential equation using classical techniques, which is then solved analytically. The key result is the derivation of this exact expression, and numerical comparisons show discrepancies with the classic tabulated values originally provided by Chandrasekhar. This work is significant because it provides a precise analytical benchmark for a foundational problem in astrophysics and atmospheric science, potentially clarifying inaccuracies in prior numerical solutions.|

The method applies the quantum inverse scattering framework using novel, higher-dimensional L-operators that possess algebraic, combinatorial, and geometric properties. The key result is the demonstration of a form of "weak integrability" for the 20-vertex model, characterized by a 3D Poisson structure and triangular height functions derived from these operators. The significance lies in advancing the theory of integrable systems by formalizing a notion of integrability for models outside the standard Lie-algebraic classification, with implications for statistical mechanics and quantum field theory.|

|**2026-04-03**|**Chiral skyrmionic superconductivity from doping a Chern Ferromagnet**|Miguel Gon\c{c}alves, Kun Yang, Shi-Zeng Lin|[[arxiv:2604.02298](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02298)]|This paper addresses the problem of identifying a microscopic mechanism for chiral superconductivity, a state with topological properties. The method involves numerical simulations of a doped Chern ferromagnet using exact diagonalization and density-matrix renormalization group (DMRG) techniques. The key result is the discovery of a stable Cooper pair formed from a magnon bound to two holes, which exhibits both chiral f-wave symmetry and a noncoplanar, skyrmionic spin texture. The significance is that this provides a novel mechanism for chiral superconductivity, potentially explaining its emergence in systems like doped MoTe₂ moiré superlattices.|

The authors develop a sign-free method by constructing an effective single-particle Hamiltonian from the imaginary-time propagator, allowing them to extract the superfluid stiffness. Their key results reveal a dome-shaped dependence of the d-wave superfluid stiffness on doping, with a peak magnitude significantly exceeding the threshold for superconductivity, while spin correlations remain flat. This work is significant as it provides direct, numerically exact evidence within the doped Hubbard model for a superconducting dome driven by spin fluctuations and Fermi-surface geometry, closely mirroring the phenomenology observed in cuprate high-temperature superconductors.|

Its significance is providing a practical, data-driven route to uncover complete symmetry structures directly from spectral correlations, applicable to both chaotic and integrable models.|

The key results are an exact solution showing a superconducting dome and, crucially, the identification of a many-body effect from the Fermi arcs that suppresses the transition temperature more than expected and increases the gap-to-Tc ratio. Its significance lies in providing an analytical benchmark that demonstrates how the anomalous normal state fundamentally competes with and modifies superconductivity.|

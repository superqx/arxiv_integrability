## High Energy Theory

|**2026-05-04**|**Gravitational Sommerfeld Effects: Formalism, Renormalization, and Perturbation to $O(G^{10})$**|Chih-Hao Chang, Chia-Hsien Shen, Zihan Zhou|[[arxiv:2604.14112](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14112)]|This paper develops a systematic method to compute the gravitational Sommerfeld factor, which describes universal corrections to gravitational waveforms from curved spacetime. Using the worldline effective field theory and the Mano-Suzuki-Takasugi method, the authors derive a closed-form expression for this factor and analytically solve for its magnitude and phase up to order \(O(G^{10})\) for low partial waves. They prove that the phase of the Sommerfeld factor matches the elastic Compton scattering phase shift in the absence of tidal dissipation, and they establish a new renormalization group equation for radiative multipole moments. These results provide high-precision data and exact relations that enable improved resummation of gravitational waveforms beyond standard tail logarithms.|

## Mathematical Physics

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-04**|**Almost global large deviations principle for the KdV equation**|Riccardo Berforini D'Aquino, Ricardo Grande|[[arxiv:2605.00484](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00484)]|This paper addresses the problem of extreme wave formation in the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation with random initial data of small average size. The authors use a large deviations principle to characterize the probability of observing unusually large amplitudes over long polynomial timescales. Key results show that, due to the integrable nature of the KdV equation, extreme waves arise primarily from dispersive focusing—the quasi-synchronization of many phases—rather than resonant energy exchange. The significance lies in providing rigorous asymptotics for rare, high-amplitude events in a weakly nonlinear, integrable setting by combining Birkhoff normal form analysis with probabilistic methods.|
|**2026-05-04**|**Random matrix ensembles and integrable differential identities**|Costanza Benassi, Marta Dell'Atti, Antonio Moro|[[arxiv:2504.11296](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.11296)]|This paper develops an integrable differential identity approach to characterize observables and state functions in random matrix theory, specifically for unitary and orthogonal ensembles with even interaction Hamiltonians. The method shows that order parameters for the unitary ensemble, derived from the Volterra lattice, solve the modified KP equation, while the analogous reduction for the orthogonal ensemble yields a new integrable chain related to the Pfaff lattice. A key step involves calculating initial conditions for the orthogonal ensemble through a map from orthogonal to skew-orthogonal polynomials. The work is significant because it unifies discrete and continuum descriptions, revealing that the same semi-discrete dynamical chain solves both systems, and extends integrable structure theory to orthogonal ensembles.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-04**|**Superconducting diode effect in correlated electron systems by nonreciprocal magnetism**|Kyohei Nakamura, Youichi Yanase|[[arxiv:2605.00601](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00601)]|This paper investigates the superconducting diode effect (SDE) in correlated electron systems, specifically using the Rashba-Zeeman-Hubbard model to study d-wave superconductivity near antiferromagnetic quantum criticality. The method applies the Dyson-Gor'kov equation with fluctuation exchange approximation, revealing that electron correlations suppress the conventional SDE from depairing currents. Key results show that a supercurrent nonreciprocally induces antiferromagnetic order, which controls the critical current and enables perfect diode efficiency. The significance lies in identifying a previously unrecognized correlation-driven SDE mechanism, establishing strongly correlated superconductors as a platform for diode physics.|
|**2026-05-04**|**Quantum corrections to the Josephson dynamics: a population-imbalance approach**|Oliver Hideg, Sofia Salvatore, Luca Salasnich|[[arxiv:2605.00571](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00571)]|The paper addresses quantum corrections to the Josephson dynamics of two weakly coupled Bose-Einstein condensates by using population imbalance as the sole dynamical variable. The method involves deriving an imbalance-only Lagrangian with a position-dependent mass, quantizing it via symmetric operator ordering, and computing one-loop quantum corrections using a covariant background-field method. Key results include explicit expressions for the effective potential and mass, leading to a quantum-corrected Josephson frequency, with numerical comparisons showing the imbalance approach outperforms the phase-only method in strong interaction regimes. This work is significant because it provides a more accurate description of quantum effects in strongly interacting bosonic systems, extending the applicability of Josephson junction models beyond the weak-coupling limit.|

|**2026-05-04**|**Random matrix ensembles and integrable differential identities**|Costanza Benassi, Marta Dell'Atti, Antonio Moro|[[arxiv:2504.11296](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.11296)]|This paper introduces a method using integrable differential identities and specific initial conditions to analyze random matrix ensembles, focusing on the unitary and orthogonal cases. For the unitary ensemble, the authors demonstrate that the order parameters solve the modified KP equation, while for the orthogonal ensemble, a new integrable chain is derived by evaluating initial conditions through a map from orthogonal to skew-orthogonal polynomials. Key results include the thermodynamic limit yielding a hydrodynamic-type integrable system for the orthogonal ensemble, with the same semi-discrete dynamical chain solving both the discrete and continuum initial value problems. This work provides an effective framework for characterizing observables in random matrix theory and uncovers new integrable structures, notably linking the orthogonal ensemble to a novel chain.|

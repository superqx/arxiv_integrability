|**2026-04-23**|**On non-relativistic integrable models and 4d SCFTs**|Rotem Ben Zeev, Anirudh Deb, Hee-Cheol Kim, Shlomo S. Razamat|[[arxiv:2604.19885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19885)]|This paper investigates the connection between specialized observables in four-dimensional supersymmetric quantum field theories (SCFTs) and certain solvable quantum mechanical systems. The authors demonstrate that a specific limit (the "generalized Schur index") of 4d N=2 theories can be systematically expressed using the eigenfunctions of non-relativistic, elliptic integrable models like the Calogero-Moser and Ruijsenaars-Schneider systems. A key finding is that this framework reveals unexpected mathematical identities and mappings between indices of different theories, such as those within the Deligne-Cvitanović series. The work's significance lies in establishing a broader paradigm, suggesting that similar "Schur-like" limits in more general 4d N=1 SCFTs are fundamentally linked to the free-fermion regimes of various non-relativistic integrable models.|
|**2026-04-23**|**Einstein-Maxwell fields as solutions of Einstein gravity coupled to conformally invariant non-linear electrodynamics**|Marcello Ortaggio|[[arxiv:2511.13665](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.13665)]|This paper investigates which standard Einstein-Maxwell solutions can be generalized to theories using conformally invariant non-linear electrodynamics (CINLE). The authors develop a mathematical criterion to identify such "extendable" solutions, showing that all static configurations and those with a specific geometric property (a twist-free Killing vector) qualify. This finding is significant because it allows physicists to directly convert many known black hole and cosmological solutions into new, more complex models within CINLE theories, as demonstrated with several explicit examples.|

## Mathematical Physics

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-23**|**Superintegrable 2D systems in magnetic fields with a parabolic type integral**|Tatiana Ekelchik, Antonella Marchesiello|[[arxiv:2604.20465](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20465)]|This paper investigates two-dimensional superintegrable systems in a magnetic field, focusing on the case where one integral of motion is of parabolic type. The method involves classifying systems by assuming the existence of two quadratic integrals beyond the Hamiltonian, specifically pairing a parabolic integral with either an elliptic or a non-standard parabolic one. The key result confirms that, under these conditions, the only possible system on the Euclidean plane requires both a constant magnetic field and a constant electrostatic potential. This finding is significant as it extends prior classification work and reinforces the uniqueness of this simple system within the studied class of quadratic superintegrability.|
|**2026-04-23**|**On non-relativistic integrable models and 4d SCFTs**|Rotem Ben Zeev, Anirudh Deb, Hee-Cheol Kim, Shlomo S. Razamat|[[arxiv:2604.19885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19885)]|This paper explores connections between certain four-dimensional quantum field theories and non-relativistic integrable systems. The method involves expressing a specific observable (the generalized Schur index) of 4D superconformal field theories (SCFTs) in terms of mathematical objects from integrable models, such as elliptic Jack functions and eigenfunctions of the Lamé equation. A key result is demonstrating a mapping between indices of different theories, which implies non-trivial mathematical identities for sums of eigenfunctions across different root systems. The significance is establishing a broader framework where limits of integrable models naturally describe specialized observables in a wide class of 4D SCFTs, extending the connection beyond previously known examples.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-23**|**Melting temperature shifts from quantum fluctuations in generalized Wigner crystals**|Aman Kumar, Sogoud Sherif, Veit Elser, Hitesh J. Changlani|[[arxiv:2604.19870](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19870)]|This paper challenges the common assumption that quantum fluctuations always lower the melting temperature of ordered phases by collaborating with thermal fluctuations. The authors use finite-temperature Lanczos calculations on an extended Hubbard model to study the melting of generalized Wigner crystals in moiré systems. Their key result is that quantum effects can competitively *increase* the melting temperature relative to classical estimates, sometimes by more than 50%, which they explain using a perturbation theory framework. This work is significant because it provides a new qualitative understanding of fluctuation interplay and makes testable predictions for experiments where material parameters like bandwidth are tunable.|
|**2026-04-23**|**Asymmetric and chiral dynamics of two-component anyons with synthetic gauge flux**|Rui-Jie Chen, Ying-Xin Huang, Guo-Qing Zhang, Dan-Wei Zhang|[[arxiv:2512.19139](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.19139)]|This paper studies how two-component anyons behave in a one-dimensional lattice, focusing on how their unique quantum statistics and an applied magnetic-like flux affect their motion. The authors use numerical simulations of a two-particle model to analyze expansion dynamics, revealing that transport can become asymmetric and exhibit distinct chiral or anti-chiral motion. A key result is that both the anyonic phase and the synthetic gauge flux can dynamically suppress expansion or reverse the direction of chirality, depending on their signs and the interaction strength. This work is significant for understanding and controlling exotic quantum transport in engineered systems, linking anyonic statistics directly to measurable dynamical symmetries and chiral phenomena.|
|**2026-04-23**|**Optimal Work Extraction from Finite-Time Closed Quantum Dynamics**|Shoki Sugimoto, Takahiro Sagawa, Ryusuke Hamazaki|[[arxiv:2508.20512](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.20512)]|This paper addresses the challenge of maximizing work extraction from quantum systems within a limited time, where a fundamental trade-off exists between power and efficiency. The method focuses on a specific class of problems where the control Hamiltonian is optimized over a Lie algebra compatible with the system's natural dynamics, encompassing complex many-body models. A key result is that the optimal protocol simplifies to a time-independent control Hamiltonian, found by solving a nonlinear self-consistent equation, which avoids computationally expensive searches. This work is significant as it provides an exact and computationally efficient framework for designing optimal finite-time work extraction in many-body quantum systems, crucial for advancing quantum thermodynamics and engineering.|

## Nonlinear Sciences

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-23**|**The gauge action on semi-discrete Lax representations and its invariants**|Sergei Igonin|[[arxiv:2604.20830](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20830)]|This paper addresses the problem of classifying semi-discrete Lax pairs (matrix Lax representations) for integrable differential-difference equations, which are considered gauge equivalent if related by a local matrix transformation. The method involves constructing explicit algebraic invariants under this gauge action. A key result is that if any of these invariants depends nontrivially on a spectral parameter, that parameter is essential and cannot be removed by gauge transformation. The significance lies in providing a practical, computable tool to verify the genuineness of a spectral parameter and to test whether two given Lax pairs are gauge equivalent, advancing the structural theory of integrable semi-discrete systems.|
|**2026-04-23**|**An explicit operator explains end-to-end computation in the modern neural networks used for sequence and language modeling**|Anif N. Muller|[[arxiv:2604.20595](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20595)]|This paper addresses the challenge of interpreting the complex, end-to-end computations performed by modern state space models (SSMs) used in sequence modeling. The authors' method establishes a precise mathematical correspondence between these models and a physically interpretable system of nonlinear oscillators arranged in a ring network. A key result is the derivation of an exact operator that fully describes the model's input-output map, revealing that classification emerges from wave interactions within the network. The significance of this work is that it provides a novel, mathematically rigorous framework for understanding and interpreting these powerful but opaque neural network architectures.|
|**2026-04-23**|**Lax Pairs: Integrable, Less Integrable and Nonintegrable Systems**|D. C. Antonopoulou, S. Kamvissis|[[arxiv:2603.09224](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09224)]|This paper examines the limitations of Lax pair formulations for solving initial-boundary value problems in integrable systems, contrasting them with the well-established success for initial value problems. The method involves reviewing and analyzing specific cases where Lax pairs are applied to boundary value problems. A key result is that while some boundary problems retain integrability and predictable long-term behavior, others exhibit irregular, chaotic dynamics. This work is significant because it highlights a critical gap in integrable systems theory, showing that the presence of a Lax pair does not guarantee tame solutions when boundaries are involved.|

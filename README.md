The authors employ the covariant phase space formalism as a natural and efficient method to overcome this difficulty. They demonstrate their approach using an exactly solvable two-dimensional model, where they construct a perturbative solution, compute the energy spectrum and unequal-time commutators, and find results consistent with the exact theory. The significance lies in providing a systematic framework for quantizing more complex higher-derivative theories.|

This work is significant because it demonstrates a mechanism for generating cosmological horizons solely from electromagnetic fields, potentially offering new insights into black hole physics and late-time cosmology.|

The work provides a controlled integrable realization of ETH-violating scar-like states, demonstrating ordered critical matter deep within a many-body spectrum.|

|**2026-06-24**|**On Degeneracies of Density, Magnetic, and Pairing Responses: How Competing Orders Echo Underlying Symmetries in the Hubbard Model**|Michael Meixner, Herbert E{\ss}l, Matthias Reitner, Alessandro Toschi, Thomas Sch\"afer|[[arxiv:2606.24755](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24755)]|The paper investigates the degeneracies of charge, spin, and pairing susceptibilities in the bipartite Hubbard model, using pseudospin symmetry to analytically derive relations between these response functions. The method involves combining analytical theory with two-particle numerical simulations, revealing a simultaneous d-wave pairing and d-density wave (loop current) instability near the metal-insulator transition driven by short-ranged spin fluctuations. Key results show that geometric frustration gradually lifts this degeneracy, favoring superconductivity over competing orders. The significance lies in providing a general tool for uncovering symmetry relations in correlated systems and offering a unified perspective on intertwined charge, pairing, and spin orderings.|
|**2026-06-24**|**Out-of-equilibrium scaling of the particle density in quantum fermionic wires after a critical quenching of the chemical potential**|H. Panagopoulos, E. Vicari|[[arxiv:2606.24395](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24395)]|This paper investigates the post-quench scaling of particle density in Kitaev quantum wires after an instantaneous change of the chemical potential to its critical value. The authors analytically demonstrate that the difference between the particle density and its critical value exhibits an out-of-equilibrium scaling behavior dependent on a dynamic variable θ, which involves time, the initial state's length scale, and the dynamic critical exponent. The scaling function displays a singular limit as θ approaches zero, which is linked to the anomalous equilibrium scaling of the density at the quench's starting point. This finding confirms earlier conjectures that post-quench dynamics can reveal universal scaling behaviors that are typically obscured in equilibrium.|
|**2026-06-24**|**Fluctuation exchange study on the electron-hole asymmetry of the superconductivity across 1/3 filling in the trilayer Hubbard model**|Yushi Yamada, Masataka Kakoi, Masayuki Ochi, Kazuhiko Kuroki|[[arxiv:2606.24126](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24126)]|The study uses the fluctuation exchange approximation to investigate superconductivity in a trilayer Hubbard model with large interlayer hoppings, focusing on band fillings near 1/3. The researchers find that for small onsite Coulomb interaction \(U\), superconductivity shows electron-hole symmetry, aligning with expectations for a nearly half-filled two-band system. In contrast, for large \(U\), an electron-hole asymmetry emerges, with superconductivity favored in the hole-doped regime (removing electrons from 1/3 filling). This asymmetry is linked to the asymmetric renormalization of the bonding, nonbonding, and antibonding bands under strong correlation, highlighting the role of band structure effects in doped multilayer systems.|
|**2026-06-24**|**Infinite-Level Hierarchy of Solvable Quantum Circuits**|Michael A. Rampp, Suhail A. Rather, Pieter W. Claeys|[[arxiv:2606.23803](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23803)]|The paper addresses the limitation of the generalized dual-unitary hierarchy, which loses solvability across all spacetime beyond the second level. The authors propose a new infinite hierarchy of solvability conditions that, when combined with the existing hierarchy, enables exact analysis of correlation functions and entanglement dynamics throughout spacetime. They prove that this hierarchy has non-trivial solutions at every level. This work extends the concept of dual unitarity while preserving solvability, offering a pathway to study more general exactly solvable non-integrable quantum systems.|
|**2026-06-24**|**Three-dimensional spin-orbital liquids**|Anna Sandberg, Lukas R{\o}dland, Maria Hermanns|[[arxiv:2602.06628](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06628)]|The paper addresses the problem of realizing three-dimensional quantum spin liquids beyond the two-dimensional Kitaev model. The method involves constructing exact spin-orbital Hamiltonians using higher-dimensional Clifford algebras on three- and four-coordinated lattices. Key results show that these models produce gapless Majorana metals with topological Fermi surfaces, nodal lines, and Weyl semimetal phases, whose stability and splitting patterns under perturbations are analyzed. This provides a unified framework for understanding three-dimensional Majorana metals in spin liquids, significantly extending the known landscape of fractionalized phases.|
|**2026-06-24**|**Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking in Chiral Current-Carrying High-Energy Eigenstates**|Tigran A. Sedrakyan, Jun-Jun Pang, Chenan Wei, Baigeng Wang|[[arxiv:2512.09107](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09107)]|This paper identifies an exactly solvable exception to the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) in the integrable spin-½ XXX Heisenberg chain. By biasing conserved charges and using a selector Hamiltonian, the authors construct rare, atypical eigenstates of the undeformed chain that are chiral, current-carrying, and zero-entropy Bethe states. These states exhibit nonthermal local observables and gapless Luttinger-liquid correlations, existing outside the typical finite-entropy manifold of generalized Gibbs ensembles. The findings demonstrate a controlled way to create ETH-violating, scar-like states and ordered critical matter deep within a many-body spectrum, with signatures persisting under weak integrability breaking over prethermal timescales.|
|**2026-06-24**|**Integrable 3-Site, Tilted, Extended Bose-Hubbard Model with Nearest-Neighbour Interactions**|Jon Links|[[arxiv:2506.21789](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.21789)]|This paper identifies a specific choice of coupling parameters that makes a 3-site, tilted, extended Bose-Hubbard model with nearest-neighbour interactions integrable. The authors develop a Bethe ansatz method to solve this model, obtaining analytical expressions for its energy spectrum and eigenstates. The key result is the existence of an exact solution for this extended system, which goes beyond the standard three-site Bose-Hubbard model. This work is significant as it provides a rare, exactly solvable example in the study of interacting cold-atom systems with dipolar interactions.|

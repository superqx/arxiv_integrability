## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-06-11**|**Integrable Massless and Massive Fermions**|Zhao Zhang|[[arxiv:2603.11172](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11172)]|This paper introduces a new classification for one-dimensional integrable fermions based on the simultaneous satisfaction of the Yang-Baxter equation and Shastry’s decorated Yang-Baxter equation, which is more general than prior free-fermion algebra definitions. The author identifies two mechanisms for generating massive fermions from massless ones: breaking time-reversal symmetry via an external field or introducing time-reversal symmetric interactions. These mechanisms are exemplified by the XY chain in a longitudinal field and the Hubbard model, both featuring non-relativistic, bivariate $R$-matrices. The work establishes integrability conditions for such Hamiltonians and outlines procedures for uniquely determining their $R$ -matrices, advancing the theoretical framework for integrable fermionic systems.|
|**2026-06-11**|**Random matrices**|Bertrand Eynard, Taro Kimura, Sylvain Ribault|[[arxiv:1510.04430](https://arxiv.org/abs/1510.04430)]|This paper presents a self-contained introduction to random matrix theory, focusing on three main methodological approaches: the Coulomb gas method, loop equations with topological recursion, and orthogonal polynomials linked to integrable systems. A key contribution is the emphasis on the "spectral curve" as a unifying geometric object that each method defines to encode a model's properties. The text demonstrates how these approaches interconnect, using examples like counting polygonal surfaces and angular integrals to illustrate applications. Its significance lies in offering a cohesive framework that connects diverse analytical techniques within the field.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-06-11**|**Estimation of conserved charges for a one dimensional system with inhomogeneous hopping**|Triparna Mondal|[[arxiv:2606.11991](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11991)]|This paper addresses the challenge of identifying conserved charges in quantum systems, which are typically difficult to find. The authors apply integrable matrix theory to a one-dimensional single-particle system with inhomogeneous nearest-neighbor hopping, a model that can be tuned between chaotic and integrable regimes. By analyzing the eigenspectrum, they estimate the number of conserved charges across this transition and find it is nearly equal to the system size in the integrable limit. The key result suggests that the number of conserved charges derived from integrable matrix theory can serve as a quantitative measure of quantum integrability.|
|**2026-06-11**|**Superconductivity near quarter- and half-filling of a strongly correlated triangular Hubbard band in twisted trilayer WSe2**|Yungi Jeong, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Joonho Jang|[[arxiv:2606.11495](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11495)]|The paper presents experimental evidence of superconductivity in twisted trilayer WSe2, using alternating twist angles to realize a strongly correlated triangular lattice Hubbard model. The key method involves applying displacement fields to tune carrier density and layer polarization. The results show superconductivity emerging near quarter- and half-filling, with a robust correlated insulating state at half filling and correlated metallic phases bordering the superconducting regions. These findings establish twisted trilayer WSe2 as a valuable platform for exploring exotic quantum phases arising from geometric frustration and strong correlations.|
|**2026-06-11**|**Physically Constrained Ensemble Gaussian Process Modelling for Expensive Quantum Systems with Heteroskedastic Noise**|Arpan Biswas, Surtirtha Paul, Joseph Agada, Matthias Thamm, Adrian Del Maestro|[[arxiv:2606.11240](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11240)]|The paper addresses the challenge of accurately modeling expensive quantum simulations, which suffer from heteroskedastic noise and require physically meaningful predictions despite scarce data. The authors propose a Physically Constrained Ensemble Gaussian Process (pc-EGP) framework that enforces physical constraints through a weighted penalty in the loss function and trains an ensemble of GPs integrated via numerical quadrature. Applied to DMRG simulations of the Bose-Hubbard model and QMC simulations of a confined quantum liquid, pc-EGP achieves a better balance of accuracy and physical consistency compared to conventional GP. This work provides a robust, domain-informed approach for surrogate modeling of computationally expensive quantum systems, enabling more reliable autonomous exploration.|
|**2026-06-11**|**Integrable Massless and Massive Fermions**|Zhao Zhang|[[arxiv:2603.11172](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11172)]|This paper introduces a unified framework for classifying integrable fermionic models into massless and massive regimes based on the \( R \)-matrix satisfying both the Yang-Baxter equation and Shastry’s decorated Yang-Baxter equation. The author identifies two mechanisms for generating a spectral gap: breaking time-reversal symmetry via an external field, or introducing time-reversal symmetric interactions. Key results include the realization of these mechanisms in the XY chain and Hubbard model, along with derived integrability conditions and procedures for determining their \( R \)-matrices. The work provides a more general classification than previous free-fermion approaches and offers systematic tools for analyzing integrable fermionic systems.|
|**2026-06-11**|**Tomographic collective modes in a magnetic field**|Jeff Maki, Johannes Hofmann|[[arxiv:2603.06518](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06518)]|This paper investigates the odd-even effect in two-dimensional Fermi liquids, where different parity deformations of the Fermi surface decay at vastly different rates. The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation to study how a magnetic field disrupts the tomographic transport regime that arises from this effect. They find that at a critical magnetic field, one of two tomographic collective modes in the transverse conductivity disappears, with the surviving mode determined by the Landau parameters. The work is significant as it predicts observable changes in current response damping in magnetic fields, offering a route to experimentally verify the tomographic regime.|
|**2026-06-11**|**Random matrices**|Bertrand Eynard, Taro Kimura, Sylvain Ribault|[[arxiv:1510.04430](https://arxiv.org/abs/1510.04430)]|This paper offers a self-contained introduction to random matrix theory, concentrating on three fundamental analytical approaches: the Coulomb gas method with an algebraic geometry perspective, loop equations solved via topological recursion, and orthogonal polynomials linked to integrable systems. A key result is the definition of the spectral curve, a geometric object central to each approach that encodes all model properties. The text also covers the related topics of enumerating polygonal surfaces and evaluating angular integrals. Its significance lies in providing a unified, pedagogical overview of these core techniques, making the field more accessible.|

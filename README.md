The significance lies in presenting a novel classification of solvable potentials and demonstrating how parameters can fundamentally alter a system's energy spectrum.|

|**2026-05-28**|**Avoided Stoner instability at a single ordinary Van Hove point**|I. S. Tupitsyn, B. Currie, A. V. Chubukov, B. V. Svistunov, E. Kozik, N. V. Prokof'ev|[[arxiv:2605.28761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28761)]|The paper addresses why the predicted ferromagnetic (Stoner) instability at a Van Hove point in the two-dimensional Hubbard model is not observed experimentally, despite mean-field theory expecting it to occur at any coupling strength. Using two diagrammatic Monte Carlo methods, the authors uncover that the instability is avoided because the effective coupling is renormalized downward and the density of states is suppressed due to reduced quasiparticle weight. Key results show that the system remains stable down to temperatures an order of magnitude below the mean-field prediction. This work provides numerically exact evidence explaining the failure of the mean-field Stoner criterion, resolving a long-standing theoretical puzzle.|
|**2026-05-28**|**Conductivity of a Non-Galilean--Invariant Fermi Liquid: Exact Solution of the Kinetic Equation**|Tatia Kiliptari, Vladimir I. Yudson, Dmitrii L. Maslov|[[arxiv:2605.21774](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21774)]|This paper solves the kinetic equation for a disordered, non-Galilean-invariant Fermi liquid to obtain an exact expression for its conductivity. The key finding is that electron-electron interactions contribute to conductivity only through the quasiparticle scattering time. The crossover between collisionless and hydrodynamic regimes is determined by comparing this scattering time with the impurity scattering rate or probe frequency. This work is significant because it provides a complete solution valid in the hydrodynamic regime, inaccessible to perturbation theory, and demonstrates the necessity of mass renormalization to achieve consistency with Kubo approaches near a quantum critical point.|
|**2026-05-28**|**Interlayer Coupling Driven Correlated and Charge-Ordered Electronic States in a Transition Metal Dichalcogenide Superlattice**|Yiwei Li, Lixuan Xu, Shihao Zhang, Lanxin Liu, Yifan Zhou, Qiang Wan, Shiwei Chen, Shiheng Liang, Yulin Chen, Yi-feng Yang, Xuan Luo, Yuping Sun, Nan Xu, Zhongkai Liu|[[arxiv:2511.14570](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.14570)]|This paper investigates how interlayer coupling in the natural van der Waals superlattice 4Hb-TaS₂ gives rise to correlated and charge-ordered electronic states. Using area-selective angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy, the authors directly probe the electronic structures of both the 1T- and 1H-terminated surfaces. The key results reveal that the surface 1T-layer’s modulation folds the metallic states of subsurface 1H-layers to the Brillouin zone center, producing chiral Fermi surfaces, and that hybridization with the incipient flat band creates a Kondo-like peak at the Fermi level. Additionally, interlayer charge transfer drives distinct 3×3 and 2×2 charge orders on the 1T and 1H layers, respectively. These findings are significant because they reconcile competing theoretical models and highlight the interplay between flat bands, charge order, and superconductivity in correlated superlattices.|
|**2026-05-28**|**Emergent charge-2 $e$ carriers well hidden from electronic band structures**|Muhammad Gaffar, Wei Ku|[[arxiv:2511.01742](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.01742)]|This paper addresses the problem of why non-Fermi liquid behaviors are observed in materials where electronic band structures appear conventional. Using the attractive Hubbard model and determinant quantum Monte Carlo calculations, the authors demonstrate the emergence of charge-2e bosonic carriers that coexist with electronic carriers. Key results show that these 2e carriers, despite dominating in density, remain hidden from the electronic spectral function, which displays good quasiparticles with minimal mass enhancement, though a pseudogap reveals their presence above the superfluid temperature. The findings caution against dismissing strong correlations based on clean band structures and provide a microscopic basis for boson-fermion mixed liquid models in correlated materials.|
|**2026-05-28**|**Eigenstate Thermalization in 1+1-Dimensional SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory Coupled with Dynamical Fermions**|Diptarka Das, Lukas Ebner, Saurabh V. The authors use exact diagonalization and the loop-string-hadron framework to compute matrix elements for local and non-local operators, analyzing chaos indicators and the behavior of diagonal and off-diagonal matrix elements. Key results include the extraction of a spectral function from off-diagonal elements, revealing a spectral gap and memory peak for non-local operators, along with evidence for subsystem ETH and gauge-theory-specific features in reduced density matrices. The study advances understanding of thermalization in gauge theories, highlighting the interplay between quantum chaos, random matrix behavior, and unique gauge constraints.|

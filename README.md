|**2026-06-05**|**Black Bounce Solutions from a Self-Interacting 3-Form Field in General Relativity**|Francisco S. N. Lobo, Manuel E. Rodrigues|[[arxiv:2606.05508](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05508)]|This paper presents a new class of black-bounce solutions in general relativity, constructed using a self-interacting 3-form field alongside a scalar field. The method involves deriving exact solutions directly from the field equations, without imposing a predefined metric ansatz, by exploiting the Hodge duality between 3-form and 1-form fields. Key results include two solution families: one with a globally phantom scalar field, and another where the scalar field transitions from phantom near the bounce to canonical outside the event horizon—a feature usually seen in modified gravity. This work is significant because it shows that regular, black-bounce geometries can arise within pure general relativity, offering a mathematically consistent and observationally viable framework.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Regularized Master-Field Approximation for Large- $N$ Reduced Matrix Models**|Reishi Maeta|[[arxiv:2605.10720](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10720)]|The paper proposes a numerical method for large-\(N\) reduced matrix models based on a regularized master field, which is approximated by a finite-dimensional matrix that must satisfy loop equations as closely as possible. This method avoids the sign problem, making it applicable to both Euclidean and Minkowski-type models. Numerical tests on one- and two-matrix models show that the method reproduces exact solutions for Euclidean cases and perturbative results for Minkowski cases. These results demonstrate the method's effectiveness and suggest that such matrix models can be described by a regularized master field.|

## Mathematical Physics

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-06-05**|**Isospectrality and Operator Complexity**|Pradip Kattel, Yicheng Tang, Natan Andrei|[[arxiv:2606.05294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05294)]|This paper investigates two isospectral fermion chains—one strongly interacting, the other quadratic—that share the same energy spectrum but differ dramatically in phase structure and operator dynamics. The authors use a nonlocal unitary transformation to map one model onto the other, which converts local fermion operators into extended many-body strings. In the quadratic model, operators remain within a closed linear subspace, whereas in the interacting model, they generate increasingly higher-body terms and exhibit asymptotic Lanczos growth proportional to the square root of the iteration step. The significance lies in demonstrating that identical free spectra can coexist with interacting operator complexity, altering the conventional understanding of how operator dynamics relate to spectral properties.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Exact solution of the Gaunt-modified Landau-Lifshitz equation in a plane wave**|S. A. Shekhanov, C. P. Ridgers|[[arxiv:2606.05205](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05205)]|This paper solves the Landau-Lifshitz equation modified by a Gaunt factor, which models quantum radiation reaction. The key is that in a plane wave, the quantum parameter depends only on lightfront momentum, preserving the classical integrability. The authors derive exact solutions for energy and four-velocity, which match known classical results in the limit. This provides an analytic, deterministic description of semiclassical radiation reaction in plane waves.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-06-05**|**Nonreversible Gauge Fields in Fokker--Planck Dynamics: Supersymmetric Hamiltonians and Learned Finite Forces**|Masayuki Ohzeki|[[arxiv:2606.06412](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06412)]|This paper formulates nonreversible perturbations of Fokker-Planck dynamics as gauge fields that preserve the stationary density but modify the excited spectrum. The authors represent admissible gauge currents using antisymmetric tensor fields and introduce a finite-time regularized objective to select an optimal gauge strength, which is learned via an actor-critic procedure. Key results include recovering the Lyapunov-equation optimum in an anisotropic Gaussian Ornstein-Uhlenbeck benchmark and demonstrating constrained selection in a double-well metastable landscape. The significance lies in unifying concepts like relaxation gaps, hypocoercive acceleration, and nonequilibrium currents, while linking stochastic gradient methods (e.g., Adam) to physically relevant Fokker-Planck systems with learned nonreversible forces.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Superconductivity beyond band geometry: emergence of pair quantum geometry**|Mehmet Akif Keskiner, Menderes I\c{s}k{\i}n|[[arxiv:2606.06017](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06017)]|This paper extends the concept of quantum geometry from single-particle to paired states in superconductors. The authors derive an effective-mass theorem for two-body bound states and Cooper pairs near the critical temperature using a generic multiband Hubbard model. They find that the inverse effective mass separates into a conventional band-structure part and a new geometric contribution, "pair quantum geometry," which becomes significant when pairing is non-uniform across sublattices and can be complex due to Landau damping. Exact calculations on various lattice models confirm that this pair quantum geometry quantitatively impacts the effective mass, establishing it as a fundamental aspect of superconductivity beyond conventional band geometry.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Odd-parity magnons in the Haldane-Hubbard model from topological exciton condensation**|Rintaro Eto, Johannes Knolle|[[arxiv:2606.05837](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05837)]|This paper investigates collective excitations in the Haldane-Hubbard model, identifying topological excitons in the paramagnetic phase whose condensation drives a transition to a collinear Néel state. The resulting magnetic phase is an odd-wave magnet characterized by odd-parity magnons with distinctive \( f \)-wave splitting. A key finding is that a closing of the electron bandgap induces a corresponding closure in the magnon bandgap, altering the magnon topology and greatly enhancing spin splitting. The results establish a novel connection between topological excitons and odd-parity magnons, with potential applications in Floquet-driven materials and cold atomic gases.|
|**2026-06-05**|**A universal and efficient hybrid digital-analog fermionic quantum simulator**|Hao-Tian Wei, Kaden R. A. Hazzard|[[arxiv:2606.05517](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05517)]|This paper presents a universal hybrid digital-analog framework for quantum simulation using fermionic ultracold atom platforms. By employing variational algorithms, the method can simulate the ground-state properties of target Hamiltonians—such as the repulsive Hubbard model, pairing models, and gauge field models—in a time that grows polynomially with error tolerance, offering an exponential quantum speedup over classical algorithms. Numerical and theoretical evidence shows this efficiency holds for local observables like energy density and correlations across these distinct models. The significance lies in enabling current fermionic hardware to simulate systems far beyond its native capabilities without requiring full digital control.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Isospectrality and Operator Complexity**|Pradip Kattel, Yicheng Tang, Natan Andrei|[[arxiv:2606.05294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05294)]|This paper investigates two isospectral fermion chains—one strongly interacting and one quadratic—that share identical energy spectra but exhibit fundamentally different operator dynamics. The authors use a nonlocal nonlinear unitary transformation to map between the models, converting local fermion operators into extended many-body strings. In the quadratic model, operators evolve within a closed linear subspace, while in the interacting model, they generate increasingly higher-body terms and show asymptotic Lanczos growth ( $b_n \propto \sqrt{n}$ ). The results demonstrate that free spectra and interacting operator complexity can coexist, challenging traditional assumptions about phase structure and complexity.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Breakdown of Thermalization from Real-Time Dynamics in the Two-Dimensional Hubbard Model**|Alessandro Sinibaldi, Luciano Loris Viteritti, Riccardo Rende, Fakher F. Assaad, Giuseppe Carleo|[[arxiv:2606.05293](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05293)]|The paper addresses the problem of thermalization in strongly correlated fermionic systems, specifically the two-dimensional Hubbard model on a half-filled square lattice. The authors use time-dependent variational Monte Carlo with transformer-based neural-network quantum states to simulate the real-time dynamics after a quench in the interaction strength U. They find two distinct regimes: for weak to intermediate U, double occupancy converges to thermal predictions, indicating ergodic relaxation; beyond a critical U, the dynamics deviate, showing clear signs of thermalization breaking. This work demonstrates the power of advanced numerical methods to access previously unreachable regimes, shedding light on nonequilibrium quantum phenomena relevant to high-temperature superconductivity and ultracold atom experiments.|
|**2026-06-05**|**The Two Orbital, Interacting Hatano-Nelson Model**|Jonah Huang, Rubem Mondaini, Nancy Aggarwal, Richard Scalettar|[[arxiv:2604.14533](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14533)]|This paper studies the interplay of non-Hermiticity, interactions, and multiple orbitals in the Hatano-Nelson model. The authors extend the model to a two-chain, spinful system with interchain hopping and map the "phase" diagrams showing where the energy spectrum becomes purely real as a function of interaction strength, hopping asymmetry, and interchain coupling. They analyze the system's spectral sensitivity to boundary conditions and the relationship between periodic boundary condition doublons and open boundary condition skin modes. The study demonstrates that the non-Hermitian framework qualitatively describes the dynamics of such non-equilibrium systems under Lindbladian evolution, confirming its stability and relevance.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Phase Diagram and Spectral Function of the Two-Dimensional Disordered Bose-Hubbard Model: A Real-Space Dynamical Mean-Field Theory Analysis**|Bastian Schindler, Renan da Silva Souza, Walter Hofstetter|[[arxiv:2509.01230](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.01230)]|This study investigates the two-dimensional Bose-Hubbard model with local disorder, where competition between disorder and interactions creates a Bose glass phase. Using real-space bosonic dynamical mean-field theory, the authors distinguish phases via the Edwards-Anderson order parameter, compressibility, and percolation analysis of the condensate. Key results show an intermediate Bose glass between superfluid and Mott insulator phases in agreement with theory, but significant finite-size effects on the phase boundary. The method also uncovers damped localized modes in the spectral function, providing novel insights into strong-coupling disordered bosonic systems.|
|**2026-06-05**|**Transformer-based operator learning framework for self-energy in strongly correlated systems**|Yuanran Zhu, Peter Rosenberg, Zhen Huang, Hardeep Bassi, Chao Yang, Shiwei Zhang|[[arxiv:2504.14483](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.14483)]|This paper introduces the Σ-Attention framework, a Transformer-based model designed to efficiently compute the self-energy operator in strongly correlated electronic systems, a key computational bottleneck in the field. The method uses an Encoder-Only Transformer trained on a hybrid dataset generated from many-body perturbation theory, strong-coupling expansion, and exact diagonalization to learn a universal approximation valid across different interaction regimes. By applying the model to the 1D Hubbard model, the authors demonstrate that it accurately predicts the Matsubara Green's function and captures the Mott transition at finite temperature. The significance lies in the Transformer’s scalability, enabling the extension of accurate self-energy calculations to much larger systems than previously possible.|

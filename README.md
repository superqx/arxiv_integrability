|**2026-04-13**|**Heat and thermal travelling wave solutions of a nonlinear Maxwell-Cattaneo-Vernotte equation**|Munaf\`o Carmelo Filippo, Rogolino Patrizia, Sciacca Michele|[[arxiv:2604.09432](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09432)]|This paper addresses the challenge of finding exact traveling wave solutions for a nonlinear version of the Maxwell-Cattaneo-Vernotte equation, which models heat propagation with finite speed. The method involves expressing the material's thermal conductivity and relaxation time as specific polynomial functions of temperature to introduce nonlinearity. The key result is the derivation of exact soliton solutions, achieved by identifying the precise degrees of these polynomial functions that permit such wave structures. This work is significant as it provides explicit analytical solutions that describe self-reinforcing thermal waves, offering deeper insight into nonlinear heat transport phenomena beyond standard diffusion models.|
|**2026-04-13**|**Symmetry-driven thermalization via finite de Finetti theorems**|Uttam Singh, Nicolas J. Cerf|[[arxiv:2604.09410](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09410)]|This paper challenges the conventional statistical explanations for thermalization in quantum systems by proposing a deterministic alternative based on symmetry. The method involves proving a finite de Finetti theorem, which mathematically shows that the reduced states of a large system invariant under energy-preserving unitaries are nearly thermal product states. A key result is that the error in this approximation vanishes as the system size increases, and the authors provide a dynamical model that realizes this symmetry class. The significance is that it establishes symmetry, rather than statistics or chaos, as a fundamental and sufficient principle for the emergence of thermal behavior.|
|**2026-04-13**|**On a non-commutative sixth $q$ -Painlev\'e system: from discrete system to surface theory**|Irina Bobrova|[[arxiv:2507.22466](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.22466)]|This paper addresses the need for a non-commutative geometric framework for discrete integrable systems, using a non-commutative version of the sixth *q*-Painlevé equation (*q*-P(A₃)) as its primary example. The method constructs the system by defining a birational representation of an affine Weyl group and then develops a corresponding non-commutative surface theory to derive the same representation from first principles. A key result is the successful recovery of a known cascade of related discrete Painlevé equations from this framework and the establishment of a link to other non-commutative *d*-Painlevé systems. The significance lies in extending Sakai's geometric classification of Painlevé equations to a non-commutative setting, providing a formal geometric foundation for non-Abelian discrete integrable systems.|
|**2026-04-13**|**Complete classification of integrability and non-integrability of S=1/2 spin chains with symmetric next-nearest-neighbor interaction**|Naoto Shiraishi|[[arxiv:2501.15506](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.15506)]|This paper addresses the problem of determining which quantum spin chains with specific next-nearest-neighbor interactions are integrable, a fundamental question in statistical mechanics. The method involves a complete analytical classification of models within this symmetric, shift-invariant class. The key result is a theorem proving there are only two integrable models in this entire class: one classical and one solvable by the Bethe ansatz, with all others being non-integrable. This finding is significant because it provides a definitive classification, confirming no other integrable models exist in this category and ruling out the possibility of models with intermediate integrability.|

|**2026-04-13**|**Eigenstate entanglement entropy in Bose-Hubbard models**|G. Medo\v{s}, L. Vidmar|[[arxiv:2604.09180](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09180)]|This paper investigates the entanglement properties of bosonic many-body systems, a topic less explored than its fermionic counterparts. The authors apply a generalized mean-field approach to calculate the leading volume-law coefficient of entanglement entropy in Bose-Hubbard models, finding it is robust against the introduction of on-site disorder. Their key numerical results reveal that the subleading constant contribution depends nontrivially on system parameters when particle number is conserved and may become universal when it is not. This work is significant for advancing the understanding of quantum thermalization and entanglement structure in bosonic matter.|
|**2026-04-13**|**Resonant dynamics of dipole-conserving Bose-Hubbard model with time-dependent tensor electric fields**|Jiali Zhang, Shaoliang Zhang|[[arxiv:2506.13159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13159)]|This paper addresses the challenge of controlling excitations in dipole-conserving quantum systems, which are relevant for studying fracton phases. The method involves theoretically coupling a dipole-conserving Bose-Hubbard model to a time-dependent rank-2 tensor electric field, engineered via a periodically driven quadratic potential. The key result is that at resonance, the drive controllably induces two distinct dynamical processes: the photon-assisted splitting of large dipoles and the movement of small dipoles. This work is significant as it proposes a practical scheme for engineering and manipulating exotic excitations using tensor gauge fields, advancing the simulation of fracton physics.|
|**2026-04-13**|**Complete classification of integrability and non-integrability of S=1/2 spin chains with symmetric next-nearest-neighbor interaction**|Naoto Shiraishi|[[arxiv:2501.15506](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.15506)]|This paper addresses the problem of determining which S=1/2 quantum spin chains with symmetric next-nearest-neighbor interactions are integrable. The method involves a complete analytical classification of this specific class of models, known as zigzag spin chains. The key result is a theorem proving that only two integrable models exist within this class: one classical and one solvable by the Bethe ansatz, with all others being non-integrable. The significance of this work is that it provides a definitive classification, confirming no other integrable models are missing and ruling out the existence of models with only a finite set of conserved quantities.|
|**2026-04-13**|**Generalized Dynamical Keldysh Model**|E. Z. Kuchinskii, M. V. Sadovskii|[[arxiv:2401.08118](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.08118)]|This paper addresses the challenge of modeling the spectral properties of an electron in a time-dependent random field, extending beyond the limitations of the standard dynamical Keldysh model. The method involves generalizing the model to account for fields with finite correlation times and finite transfer frequencies, enabling the exact summation of all Feynman diagrams in the perturbation series for the electron's Green's function. A key result is the prediction of novel modulation effects in the spectral density and density of states for fields with finite transferred frequency. The significance lies in providing a broader class of exactly solvable models that capture more realistic field statistics, offering new analytical tools for studying disordered and driven quantum systems.|

The key result is the successful definition and computation of this invariant, which is explicitly plotted for a series of progressively more intricate examples. The significance of this work is that it provides a powerful new tool for distinguishing and understanding the global structure of hypersemitoric systems, advancing the classification program in symplectic geometry.|

## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-06-15**|**Heavenly equations in de Sitter space**|Maciej Dunajski, Timothy Moy|[[arxiv:2606.14572](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14572)]|This paper addresses the problem of simplifying the description of anti-self-dual Einstein metrics in the presence of a non-zero cosmological constant. The authors prove that all such metrics can be locally derived from a single second-order partial differential equation previously introduced by Lipstein and Nagy. They connect this equation to the established hyper-heavenly formalism and derive a Lax pair for it, then demonstrate that Plebański's second heavenly equation is recovered in the limit of a vanishing cosmological constant. The work's significance lies in unifying and simplifying the mathematical framework for these gravitational solutions and clarifying their relationship to known formalisms.|
|**2026-06-15**|**Quantum Entanglement of Bethe States**|Yu Hao, Yunfeng Jiang, Bi-Quan Yang, De-liang Zhong|[[arxiv:2606.14140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14140)]|This paper examines the bipartite entanglement entropy of Bethe states in integrable spin chains, including the XXX½ model and its higher-spin and non-compact generalizations. By scanning the entire spectrum of finite chains, the authors identify Bethe solutions that minimize and maximize entanglement, revealing that the lowest-entropy state corresponds to the ground state in the antiferromagnetic XXX½ case but not in higher-spin models. The study also shows how singular and strange Bethe root configurations affect entanglement and uncovers unique features in the non-compact SL(2,ℝ) chain. Additionally, for off-shell states, an optimization algorithm is developed to explore maximum entanglement without requiring the Bethe ansatz equations.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-06-15**|**Quantum Entanglement of Bethe States**|Yu Hao, Yunfeng Jiang, Bi-Quan Yang, De-liang Zhong|[[arxiv:2606.14140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14140)]|This paper investigates quantum entanglement in Bethe eigenstates of integrable spin chains like the XXX $_{\frac{1}{2}}$ and higher-spin XXX$_s$ models. The method involves a systematic scan of bipartite entanglement entropy across the entire spectrum to find extremal states. Key results show that while the minimal-entropy state in the XXX$_{\frac{1}{2}}$ antiferromagnetic chain is always the ground state, this is not true for higher-spin models, and maximal-entropy states show highly complex root structures. The significance lies in revealing how special Bethe root configurations affect entanglement and in identifying unique entanglement features in non-compact chains, with a new algorithm for off-shell state optimization.|
|**2026-06-15**|**Superconductivity near quarter and half filling of a strongly correlated triangular Hubbard band in twisted trilayer WSe2**|Yungi Jeong, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Joonho Jang|[[arxiv:2606.11495](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11495)]|The paper reports the discovery of superconductivity in alternating twisted trilayer WSe2, a strongly correlated triangular-lattice Hubbard system. Using this platform, the authors observe a robust correlated insulating state at half-filling and superconductivity upon doping, as well as an additional superconducting state near quarter-filling flanked by correlated metals. These findings demonstrate that the system realizes strong correlations due to mirror symmetry, enabling the study of frustrated quantum phases. The results establish trilayer WSe2 as a key platform for exploring Hubbard model physics on a triangular lattice.|

|**2026-06-15**|**Benjamin-Ono dynamics of internal waves with currents**|Lyudmila Ivanova|[[arxiv:2606.14563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14563)]|This paper derives integrable models for internal waves at the interface between two fluid layers, specifically using the Benjamin-Ono (BO) equation. The method incorporates the effects of sheared currents in both layers, which are often present in oceanographic settings. Key results show that the characteristics of BO solitons are significantly altered by the shear current parameters. The significance lies in providing a more realistic model for internal wave propagation in the ocean, where currents are ubiquitous.|

By analytically continuing the scattering data of unitary interfaces, the authors obtain an SL(2,ℂ)-parametrized family that breaks current conservation, and they derive the corresponding conformal boundary states using a dual biorthogonal basis to address the non-Hermitian Hilbert space. The authors model these backgrounds as quantum quenches in the matrix model with non-linear potential terms, going beyond the usual double-scaling limit. Working in a Thomas-Fermi approximation, they find that while the early-time behavior matches the double-scaled theory, the phase-space density eventually relaxes to a time-independent equilibrium with a power law, showing that the singularity is an artifact of the double scaling limit. The significance lies in demystifying the singularity's nature and describing its eventual relaxation, with implications for interpreting the final string theory state.|

Ragoucy|[[arxiv:2606.31624](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31624)]|This paper addresses the problem of formulating Serre relations for Yangian doubles. The authors propose a method based on Enriquez's approach, analyzing the analytical properties of current products within highest weight representations. Their key result demonstrates that for Yangian doubles associated with classical Lie algebras, the Serre relations for simple root currents can be reformulated as simpler, quadratic commutation relations between composed currents. The significance lies in providing a potentially more tractable description of these algebras, which are important in mathematical physics.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Generalized Hermite Polynomials and Spectral Degeneracies of a Singular Sextic Oscillator**|Davide Guzzetti, Dmitrii Rachenkov|[[arxiv:2606.30357](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30357)]|This paper studies a quasi-exactly solvable singular sextic oscillator, focusing on its algebraic spectrum for a specific parameter range. The authors prove that the discriminant of the characteristic polynomial factorizes, with one factor being the square of a generalized Hermite polynomial \(H_{m,n}\). They show that spectral degeneracies, or level crossing points, correspond exactly to the zeros of this polynomial, establishing a direct analogue of the Shapiro–Tater conjecture for Painlevé IV. Additionally, the work reveals a new determinantal representation of \(H_{m,n}\) as a resultant, highlighting the polynomial's role in governing simultaneous quasi-polynomial eigenfunctions.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Multivariable Painleve'-II equation: connection formulas for asymptotic solutions**|N. A. Sinitsyn|[[arxiv:2603.22470](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22470)]|This paper applies an asymptotically exact WKB analysis to an integrable generalization of the Painlevé-II equation, which is a system of coupled equations with symmetry breaking terms. The method connects the asymptotic behaviors of solutions at different infinities by leveraging an exact solution of the quantum mechanical Demkov-Osherov model. Key results include precise scaling formulas for the number of excitations in unstable vacuum decay during a second-order phase transition, capturing subdominant contributions. The work’s significance lies in revealing a deeper link between classical integrable systems and solvable multistate Landau-Zener models.|

|**2026-07-01**|**Exactly solvable non-unitary conformal interfaces in unitary CFTs**|Qicheng Tang, Zixia Wei, Xueda Wen|[[arxiv:2606.32035](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.32035)]|This paper addresses the problem of constructing exactly solvable non-unitary conformal interfaces in unitary conformal field theories (CFTs). The method involves analytically continuing the scattering data of known unitary interfaces on the lattice, yielding a family parametrized by \(SL(2,\mathbb C)\) that breaks probability-current conservation. Key results include deriving the corresponding boundary states using dual biorthogonal bases and a non-unitary generalization of Cardy's condition, as well as demonstrating logarithmic entanglement scaling with a generally complex effective central charge. The significance lies in providing a consistent CFT framework for non-unitary boundaries and interfaces, with applications to global quantum quenches where effective temperature is linked to boundary-condition-changing operators.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Fate of "Space-like singularities" in $c=1$ Matrix Model**|Sumit R. Das, Shaun D. Hampton, Sinong Liu, Gautam Mandal|[[arxiv:2606.31925](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31925)]|This paper studies time-dependent backgrounds in two-dimensional string theory that appear to produce singularities. The authors use a matrix model with non-linear terms, departing from the usual double-scaling limit, and model these backgrounds as quantum quenches. They find that singularities only appear in the strict limit; at large but finite matrix size, phase space density evolves toward a smooth, equilibrium-like state, with relaxation following a universal power law. The key result is that the seemingly singular behavior is an artifact, and the final relaxed state suggests a reinterpretation of these backgrounds in string theory.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Design Principles for Quasi-Isotropic Exchange in Rare-Earth Quantum Magnets**|Kotaro Shimizu, Esteban Agustin Ghioldi, Filip Ronning, Cristian D. Batista|[[arxiv:2606.31558](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31558)]|This paper addresses the problem of strongly anisotropic exchange interactions in rare-earth magnets, which hinder the realization of quasi-isotropic Heisenberg magnetism. The authors use degenerate perturbation theory on a multiorbital Hubbard model to reveal that isotropic exchange arises from virtual hopping within the ground-state doublet, while anisotropic interactions involve excited multiplets. They identify an orbital design principle: quasi-isotropic exchange is promoted when the ground-state doublet has strong maximal-angular-momentum character perpendicular to the superexchange plane. This framework, validated against experimental Yb-based insulators, provides a practical guide for engineering quasi-isotropic interactions in rare-earth quantum materials.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Athermality of generalized Gibbs ensembles**|Riccardo Senese, Bruno Bertini, Katja Klobas, Pasquale Calabrese|[[arxiv:2606.30762](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30762)]|This paper quantifies the deviation of generalized Gibbs ensembles (GGEs) from standard thermal equilibrium in integrable quantum systems. The authors introduce "athermality" as a resource-theoretic measure based on relative entropy to the closest thermal state. Computing this measure for several models, including the free XY and interacting XXZ spin chains, they find that athermality becomes anomalously small near quantum critical points, despite the system being at finite energy density. The results demonstrate that GGEs can closely mimic thermal states at criticality, revealing a singularity in their entropy that provides a clear signature of quantum phase transitions.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Partial Reversibility and Counterdiabatic Driving in Nearly Integrable Systems**|Rohan Banerjee, Shahyad Khamnei, Anatoli Polkovnikov, Stewart Morawetz|[[arxiv:2602.22317](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22317)]|This paper investigates the limits of reversibility in nearly integrable systems, using simple toy models to understand when entropy-preserving processes are possible outside idealized limits. The authors apply approximate counterdiabatic driving to mitigate dissipative losses from rapid driving. Key results show that this technique can partially restore reversibility. The significance lies in demonstrating that similar behavior likely occurs in quantum many-body systems with integrability-breaking perturbations, offering a practical approach to controlling near-integrable systems.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Thermodynamic Constraints on Perfect Equilibrium Superconducting Diodes**|Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2512.21384](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21384)]|The paper investigates why equilibrium superconducting diodes have limited efficiency, proposing that thermodynamic constraints fundamentally prevent a perfect diode state where current flows in only one direction. The authors show that achieving an efficiency of zero, where reverse critical current is completely suppressed, is impossible due to free energy continuity. Using an exactly solvable Ising superconductor model, they demonstrate that near-perfect behavior would require free-energy singularities, while standard theories impose finite lower bounds on efficiency. This work provides a unified thermodynamic explanation for diode performance across various superconducting platforms, clarifying that high efficiencies reported in Josephson systems typically rely on non-equilibrium conditions.|
|**2026-07-01**|**A Quantum Algorithm with Polylogarithmic Depth per Trotter Step for the Extended Hubbard Model**|Yu Wang, Martina Nibbi, Maxine Luo, Isabel Nha Minh Le, Yanbin Chen, J. The authors propose Q2FMM, a quantum algorithm inspired by the fast multipole method, which replaces direct site-site interactions with hierarchical coarse-grained box interactions. By reusing multipole expansions for parent boxes and designing reversible circuits to uncompute garbage data, the algorithm achieves a circuit depth for each Trotter step that scales polylogarithmically with system size. This result marks a significant efficiency improvement over conventional approaches, enabling more practical quantum simulations of complex condensed-matter systems.|
|**2026-07-01**|**Dipolar optimal control of entangled current states**|H\'ector Briongos-Merino, Felipe Isaule, Bruno Juli\'a-D\'iaz, Montserrat Guilleumas|[[arxiv:2507.22822](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.22822)]|This paper addresses the challenge of generating specific quantum states with entangled circulation in a lattice ring. The authors propose using quantum optimal control to manipulate the dipolar interaction of ultracold atoms by time-dependently orienting a magnetic field. Through numerical simulations with the extended Bose-Hubbard model, they demonstrate that this control scheme can engineer entangled current states with perfect fidelity in many systems and achieves theoretical fidelity limits in others. The significance lies in demonstrating a feasible, low-dimensional control method for creating complex entangled states, which is important for advancing quantum technologies.|

Key results show that while perfect reversibility is lost, this technique can effectively reduce unwanted heating. The findings are significant because they provide numerical evidence that these principles extend to quantum many-body systems with integrability-breaking perturbations, offering a practical strategy for controlling such complex systems.|

|**2026-05-08**|**Systematic Extraction of Exact Yang-Mills Solutions via Algebraic Tensor Ring Decomposition**|Yu-Xuan Zhang, Jing-Ling Chen|[[arxiv:2605.06379](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06379)]|This paper introduces an algebraic tensor ring decomposition framework to systematically derive exact classical solutions from the non-linear PDEs of Yang-Mills theory. The method promotes static pure-gauge backgrounds to dynamical variables, uses differential-algebraic quotient rings to resolve the resulting equations, and organizes solutions via algebraic bifurcation analysis. |**2026-05-08**|**Challenges to Understanding Celestial Holography from the Bottom Up**|Adam Tropper|[[arxiv:2605.05350](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05350)]|This paper tests a common bottom-up method in celestial holography—defining celestial amplitudes by transforming momentum-space amplitudes term-by-term in perturbation theory—using the exactly solvable 2D Sinh-Gordon model as a case study. Comparing the non-perturbative S-matrix with its perturbative expansion, the authors find that the order of transforming and expanding does not commute, leading to disagreement even at leading order. This result shows that naive term-by-term celestial transforms are not generally valid in quantum field theories with weak-coupling expansions, implying that mismatches in perturbative tests of CCFT duals do not necessarily rule out the holographic proposal. The significance lies in demonstrating that bottom-up tests of celestial holography are more subtle and unreliable than previously assumed.|

|**2026-05-08**|**Systematic Extraction of Exact Yang-Mills Solutions via Algebraic Tensor Ring Decomposition**|Yu-Xuan Zhang, Jing-Ling Chen|[[arxiv:2605.06379](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06379)]|This paper presents an algebraic tensor ring decomposition framework to systematically extract exact solutions from the nonlinear partial differential equations of Yang-Mills theory. The method uses pure-gauge backgrounds as dynamical templates, with Maurer-Cartan forms generating algebraic terms that stabilize the nonlinear self-interactions, and employs differential-algebraic quotient rings and bifurcation analysis to organize the solution space. Key results include three classes of exact solutions: relativistic SU(2) color waves with mass gap generation in coupled branches, dynamical dyonic flux tubes with Bessel-type screening in a Meissner branch, and SU(3) configurations exhibiting four phases via kinetic cancellation mapped to a chaotic oscillator. |**2026-05-08**|**Galois Solvability of Finite-Size Bethe Solutions in the Heisenberg Chain**|Oliver R. Bellwood, William J. This reveals that algebraic complexity grows at different rates for these quantities, limiting explicit analytic tractability. Using a Riemann-Hilbert formulation and direct/inverse scattering analysis, the authors establish the solution's existence, uniqueness, and its connection to the initial-value problem. Applying Deift-Zhou nonlinear steepest descent, they derive explicit asymptotic expressions for the solution in the dispersive wave region. These results rigorously confirm and extend Newell’s original findings, supported by numerical simulations that validate the asymptotic formulas.|

|**2026-05-08**|**The General Structure of Trilinear Equations**|Takeshi Fukuyama|[[arxiv:2605.05624](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05624)]|This paper extends the Hirota bilinear formalism to trilinear structures for integrable systems. By expressing the Ernst potential in tau-ratio form, the authors show that the stationary axisymmetric Einstein equations decompose into a cubic sector, identified with a YTSF-type trilinear kernel, and a quartic gradient envelope. Applying a general trilinear kernel criterion to Tomimatsu–Sato solutions, they find that the δ=3 case shares the same kernel structure as the δ=2 case, with universal normalization. These findings indicate that the trilinear kernel may represent a universal underlying structure governing the highest-derivative sector of the Ernst system, offering new insight into integrability beyond bilinear hierarchies.|
|**2026-05-08**|**A Vector Bilinear Framework for Soliton Dynamics in Coupled Modified KdV Systems**|Laurent Delisle, Amine Jaouadi|[[arxiv:2604.10474](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10474)]|This paper addresses the problem of analyzing soliton dynamics in coupled modified Korteweg-de Vries (cmKdV) systems by developing a vector bilinear formalism. The method reformulates Hirota’s bilinear method at the vector level, allowing for direct construction of one-, two-, and three-soliton solutions in closed vector form without component-wise calculations. Key results include the recovery of the three-soliton condition at the vector level, confirming integrability, and the discovery of nontrivial vector ground states in indefinite coupling regimes that yield solitons on non-zero backgrounds. This work provides a compact, structure-preserving framework for multi-component nonlinear wave dynamics, enabling unified treatment of diverse coupling regimes and opening avenues for studying more complex excitations in integrable systems.|

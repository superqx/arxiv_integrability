|**2026-06-25**|**Spin modulations in the Rashba-Hubbard chain -- a tensor network study**|Jozef Genzor, Roman Kr\v{c}m\'ar, Andrej Gendiar, Chia-Min Chung, Denis Kochan|[[arxiv:2606.26014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26014)]|This paper uses density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) calculations to study the one-dimensional Rashba-Hubbard chain, where spin-orbit coupling is an exactly removable gauge field at the Hamiltonian level but not for spin correlations. The method maps the model to an ordinary Hubbard chain with renormalized hopping, revealing that charge properties only respond quadratically to weak spin-orbit coupling, while spin correlations respond linearly. Key results show that spin correlations develop sidebands at wave vectors \(k_0 \pm k_{\rm so}\), producing distinct spin spirals at half filling and beating patterns away from it. The significance lies in providing a controlled tensor-network benchmark for this exactly removable limit, establishing a reference for systems where spin-orbit coupling cannot be gauged away.|
|**2026-06-25**|**Lattice non-invertible symmetry from non-commuting transfer matrices**|Eric Vernier, Yuan Miao, Masahito Yamazaki|[[arxiv:2606.25660](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25660)]|This paper solves a long-standing problem by directly linking Onsager symmetry to quantum integrability in the XXZ spin chain at special roots of unity. The authors use a non-commuting transfer matrix algebra to construct a lattice realization of the Onsager algebra and its duality automorphism. They show that the resulting duality operator obeys \(\mathbb{Z}_N\) Tambara-Yamagami fusion rules, explicitly realizing topological defect lines from conformal field theory on the lattice. The work establishes non-Abelian integrability as a natural framework for understanding Onsager symmetry and categorical dualities in lattice models.|
|**2026-06-25**|**The SU(N) Holstein Model**|Chunhan Feng, Linh Pham, George Batrouni, Richard Scalettar|[[arxiv:2606.25240](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25240)]|This paper studies the SU(N) Holstein model, where multiple fermionic species (N>2) interact with a single phonon mode, a scenario relevant to cold atom experiments. Using determinant Quantum Monte Carlo simulations, the authors show that at half-filling, the system enters an insulating charge density wave phase at low temperatures. For N=3, the critical transition temperature can be up to twice that of the standard N=2 case. The findings are significant because they extend our understanding of electron-phonon systems beyond the conventional two-spin species case, revealing enhanced ordering temperatures in higher-symmetry settings.|
|**2026-06-25**|**Majorana-Pauli stabilizer codes and duality webs of fermionic topological phases**|Meng Sun, Zongyuan Wang, Nathanan Tantivasadakarn, Yu-An Chen|[[arxiv:2606.25048](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25048)]|This paper introduces Majorana-Pauli stabilizer codes, a new class of exactly solvable fermionic lattice models that combine Pauli and Majorana operators to describe intrinsically fermionic topological phases. As a key example, the authors construct a stabilizer realization of the fermionic toric code, an intrinsically fermionic topological order, and demonstrate that its anyon properties and braiding statistics emerge directly from the stabilizer structure. They further show this fermionic toric code is part of a larger duality web connecting various bosonic and fermionic topological and symmetry-protected phases, and generalize the construction to all Abelian fermionic topological orders and supercohomology phases. This work significantly extends the stabilizer-code paradigm to encompass a wide range of fermionic phases, bridging fermionic many-body physics with quantum error correction.|

The authors construct exactly solvable PDW models using a generalized quantum geometric nesting framework, leveraging non-commuting magnetic translation symmetries and time-reversal symmetry. Key results are demonstrated with a time-reversal invariant Kapit-Mueller model featuring two flat Chern bands of opposite Chern number. This work provides a concrete platform for studying the interplay of band topology and quantum geometry in PDW superconductors.|
|**2026-06-25**|**Quantum magic of strongly correlated fermions $-$ the Hubbard dimer**|Edoardo Zavatti, Gabriele Bellomia, Massimo Capone|[[arxiv:2605.18494](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18494)]|This paper investigates the quantum magic (non-stabilizerness) of the Hubbard dimer, an analytically solvable model for strongly correlated fermions. The authors compute both local and nonlocal non-stabilizerness using robustness of magic and stabilizer Rényi entropy, showing that the latter often fails for mixed states common in these systems. Key results reveal that non-stabilizerness captures unique aspects of quantum complexity not accessible via fermionic non-Gaussianity or superselected entanglement. The findings establish non-stabilizerness as a fundamental resource, offering new insights into fermionic systems with tunable interactions.|
|**2026-06-25**|**Schwinger boson theory for $S=1$ Kitaev quantum spin liquids**|Daiki Sasamoto, Joji Nasu|[[arxiv:2509.25761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.25761)]|This paper investigates the $S=1$ Kitaev model, which, unlike the exactly solvable $S=1/2$ case, remains poorly understood for spin excitations. The authors employ Schwinger boson mean-field theory with bond operators to treat anisotropic interactions and identify mean-field parameters that realize a quantum spin liquid. A key finding is that the standard Wick decoupling scheme for calculating spin correlations yields momentum dependence inconsistent with the exchange interaction's sign, a problem resolved by using decoupling based on bond operators. The study further reveals that the zero-temperature dynamical spin structure factor splits into two structures at higher temperatures due to bandwidth narrowing of spinons, clarifying the role of different decoupling approaches.|
|**2026-06-25**|**Correlation-Driven $d$ -Wave Superconducting Dome from Pseudogap Spectral Reconstruction**|Yue Yuan, Ziwen Pan, F. Using an exactly solvable correlated model, the authors perform self-consistent calculations to reveal that pseudogap correlations suppress spectral weight locally, while superconductivity reorganizes quasiparticle excitations coherently. Key results show that the interplay between these two effects naturally produces a superconducting dome in the temperature-doping phase diagram, with optimal doping near the pseudogap-to-metal quantum critical point. The findings establish a direct causal link between pseudogap correlations and the superconducting dome observed in cuprates, highlighting the robustness of d-wave pairing across a wide doping range.|

|**2026-06-25**|**Exact Solution of Granovetter's Threshold Model for a Finite Population**|Jos\'e F. Fontanari|[[arxiv:2606.25921](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25921)]|This paper presents an exact analytical solution to Granovetter's threshold model for collective behavior in a finite population. The method uses a Beta distribution to parameterize individual threshold heterogeneity and derives the precise probability that a cascade stops with a given number of active agents, starting from a single instigator. Key results reveal two distinct phase transitions: a discontinuous, first-order transition for power-law thresholds (beta=1), with a critical window shrinking as N^{-1/2}, and an infinite-order, continuous transition when high-threshold agents persist (beta<1), where the critical region contracts much more slowly as (ln N)^{-1}. The significance lies in establishing an exact mathematical benchmark for finite-size effects, which is crucial for understanding micro-to-macro behavioral aggregation and critical phenomena in social cascades.|
|**2026-06-25**|**Global Results on the Classification of Two-Component Integrable Evolutionary Systems**|Alexander V. Mikhailov, Vladimir Novikov, Jing Ping Wang|[[arxiv:2606.25802](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25802)]|The paper presents necessary and sufficient conditions for integrability in two-component polynomial evolutionary systems of odd order. The authors prove there are exactly 24 distinct spectral classes of integrable hierarchies. A complete classification of integrable homogeneous hierarchies with third and fifth-order equations is achieved, dividing them into C-integrable systems (reducible to linear-triangular form) and S-integrable systems (linked to canonical Drinfeld–Sokolov KdV-type systems). This work provides a foundational taxonomy for these integrable systems, clarifying their underlying algebraic structures and potential applications.|
|**2026-06-25**|**A novel 2+1-dimensional extended Dym equation: moving boundary problems solvable via Painlev\'e II symmetry reduction**|Colin Rogers, Pablo Amster|[[arxiv:2606.25739](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25739)]|This paper introduces a new 2+1-dimensional extension of the Dym equation, a soliton equation. The key finding is that this system can be reduced through symmetry analysis to the Painlevé II equation, which is integrable. This reduction enables the authors to solve a specific class of Stefan-type moving boundary problems exactly. The work is significant as it provides a new integrable model and a direct method for obtaining analytic solutions to complex free-boundary problems.|

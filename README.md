## Mathematical Physics

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-10**|**Quantum recurrences and the arithmetic of Floquet dynamics**|Amit Anand, Dinesh Valluri, Jack Davis, Shohini Ghose|[[arxiv:2508.09933](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09933)]|This paper investigates the precise conditions for when a periodically driven quantum system will exactly return to its initial state, a phenomenon known as quantum recurrence. The authors develop a method based on algebraic number theory to analyze the spectrum of the system's time-evolution operator, enabling them to computationally identify all possible exact recurrence times. A key result is that even simple, rational system parameters do not generally guarantee such exact returns, challenging intuitive expectations. This work is significant for providing a rigorous mathematical framework to distinguish between regular and chaotic quantum dynamics, with implications for designing controlled quantum systems.|
|**2026-04-10**|**Spectral moments of complex and symplectic non-Hermitian random matrices**|Gernot Akemann, Sung-Soo Byun, Seungjoon Oh|[[arxiv:2505.12271](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.12271)]|This paper addresses the challenge of calculating mixed spectral moments for non-Hermitian random matrices in the complex and symplectic symmetry classes. The authors develop a systematic framework that yields explicit formulas for these moments in terms of orthogonal polynomial norms, applicable to models like the elliptic Ginibre ensemble. A key finding is that the moments for the complex ensemble relate simply to their Hermitian counterparts, while those for the symplectic ensemble decompose into a complex part plus a specific correction. This work is significant for unifying and extending the analytical toolkit for non-Hermitian random matrix theory, enabling detailed asymptotic analysis and revealing universal structures in spectral statistics.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-10**|**Hubbard vs. Emery model: spectra, transport and relevance for cuprates**|Jak\v{s}a Vu\v{c}i\v{c}evi\'c, Rok \v{Z}itko|[[arxiv:2604.08085](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08085)]|This paper addresses the challenge of identifying the minimal theoretical model needed to accurately describe the physics of cuprate superconductors. It directly compares the single-orbital Hubbard model and the three-orbital Emery model by analyzing their spectral and transport properties. The key finding is that while both models present a similar qualitative picture, they show strong quantitative differences in properties like dc resistivity and effective mass, which can be tested against experiments. The significance is that these comparisons with experimental data on La₂CuO₄-based cuprates suggest the effective coupling in the Hubbard model is larger than typically assumed, providing a pathway to refine model parameters and discriminate between competing theories.|
|**2026-04-10**|**d-Wave pair density wave superconductivity in a two-orbital model**|Samuel Vadnais, Arun Paramekanti|[[arxiv:2604.07511](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07511)]|This paper investigates the origin of unconventional superconductivity in materials with multiple electronic orbitals. Using a two-orbital model, the authors apply theoretical methods to analyze competing quantum phases. Their key finding is that specific interorbital interactions can robustly stabilize a $d$ -wave pair density wave, an exotic superconducting state where electron pairs form a spatially modulating pattern. This result is significant because it identifies orbital physics as a distinct mechanism for generating pair density waves, which are candidate states for explaining mysterious phenomena in various quantum materials.|
|**2026-04-10**|**Robustness of Kardar-Parisi-Zhang-like transport in long-range interacting quantum spin chains**|Sajant Anand, Jack Kemp, Julia Wei, Christopher David White, Michael P. Zaletel, Norman Y. Yao|[[arxiv:2602.15933](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15933)]|This paper investigates whether the superdiffusive spin transport observed in integrable quantum spin chains, which resembles Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) physics, persists in more generic, non-integrable models relevant for experiments. Using tensor network simulations, the authors study long-range Heisenberg models and find that KPZ-like superdiffusive transport with a characteristic dynamical exponent of \( z = 3/2 \) survives for remarkably long times despite the non-integrability. A key result is that this behavior is linked to the models' proximity to the integrable Inozemtsev family. The work is significant because it demonstrates that exotic KPZ-like transport, previously thought to require fine-tuned integrability, can be robust and observable in realistic quantum simulator platforms like Rydberg atom arrays.|

## Nonlinear Sciences

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-10**|**On the Connection Between Chaos Assisted Tunneling and Coherent Destruction of Tunneling**|Sumita Datta|[[arxiv:2604.07841](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07841)]|This paper investigates how classical chaos influences quantum tunneling in driven nonlinear systems. The method connects tunneling rates to effective classical barriers shaped by external driving and stochastic effects. Key results reveal that chaos-assisted tunneling and coherent destruction of tunneling both stem from the same phase space restructuring and quantum interference. This unified framework is significant for controlling tunneling in complex quantum systems and understanding transport under decoherence.|
|**2026-04-10**|**Quantum recurrences and the arithmetic of Floquet dynamics**|Amit Anand, Dinesh Valluri, Jack Davis, Shohini Ghose|[[arxiv:2508.09933](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09933)]|This paper investigates the precise conditions for when a finite-dimensional, periodically driven quantum system will exactly return to its initial state after a fixed number of cycles, a phenomenon known as exact quantum recurrence. The method employs algebraic number theory to analyze the spectrum of the system's Floquet operator, enabling a complete classification of all possible recurrence times based on the arithmetic properties of its eigenvalues. A key result is that rational system parameters do not universally ensure exact recurrence, and the framework can both enumerate all possible recurrence times and definitively prove their absence for specific parameters. This work is significant for providing a rigorous, computable theory of quantum recurrences, clarifying their connection to quantum chaos, and identifying precise parameter regimes relevant for quantum control and metrology.|

## Mathematical Physics

|**2026-05-12**|**Two-parameter classes of exactly solvable quantum systems**|A. D. Alhaidari|[[arxiv:2605.09812](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09812)]|This paper introduces a method for constructing exactly solvable quantum systems using two-parameter classes of tridiagonal Hamiltonians. The wavefunction is expressed as a convergent series, where the expansion coefficients are orthogonal polynomials determined by a recursion relation with two free parameters. A key finding is that altering these parameters from a reference value induces an effective interaction potential, which can be studied numerically, and can even create bound states or resonances in systems with a purely continuous spectrum, like a free particle. The significance lies in providing a flexible framework for generating and analyzing novel quantum systems without needing to explicitly define their potential functions.|
|**2026-05-12**|**Impact of the non-canonical approach to the exact solution of the ideal one-dimensional electron gas confined with an anisotropic quantum wire of oscillator-shaped profile**|E. I. Jafarov, S. M. Nagiyev, J. Van der Jeugt|[[arxiv:2605.08986](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08986)]|This paper presents an exactly solvable model of an ideal one-dimensional electron gas confined in an anisotropic quantum wire with an oscillator-shaped potential, where homogeneity is broken by a radially varying effective electron mass. The authors solve the problem using both canonical and non-canonical approaches. Key results include analytical wavefunctions expressed via Laguerre polynomials for the radial part and, uniquely in the non-canonical approach, angular wavefunctions for even and odd states given by Gegenbauer polynomials. The work's significance lies in providing exact solutions and studying limit relations, offering a more complete description of quantum confinement effects in systems with position-dependent mass.|
|**2026-05-12**|**Second-order superintegrable systems from semi-simple and nilpotent Frobenius structures**|Andreas Vollmer|[[arxiv:2601.01978](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01978)]|This paper addresses the challenge of constructing second-order maximally superintegrable systems in arbitrary dimensions. The authors leverage a previously established connection between such systems and Hesse-Frobenius structures, which arise from commutative algebras, to reduce the classification problem to algebra. By applying conification and direct product constructions to basic semi-simple and nilpotent Frobenius algebras, they explicitly generate new, higher-dimensional superintegrable systems. Significantly, all non-degenerate second-order maximally superintegrable systems in three dimensions emerge from these elementary algebraic examples, validating the algebraic approach.|
|**2026-05-12**|**Exact Current Fluctuations in a Tight-Binding Chain with Dephasing Noise**|Taiki Ishiyama, Kazuya Fujimoto, Tomohiro Sasamoto|[[arxiv:2504.06989](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06989)]|This paper solves the long-standing problem of deriving the exact full counting statistics (FCS) of current in a diffusive quantum many-body system. The authors consider a tight-binding chain with dephasing noise and use the system's SU(2) symmetry along with a mapping to the one-dimensional Hubbard model to obtain an exact Fredholm determinant representation for the moment generating function. Their long-time asymptotic analysis reveals that the cumulant generating function follows diffusive scaling for any nonzero dephasing, with a crossover from ballistic to diffusive behavior in the weak-dephasing limit. The results provide the first exact microscopic derivation of current FCS in a diffusive quantum system and show consistent diffusive scaling with experimental measurements.|

|**2026-05-12**|**Bose-Fermi Mapping in Hubbard Models at Imaginary Chemical Potential and Phase-Induced Fermionization**|Evangelos G. Filothodoros|[[arxiv:2605.10418](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10418)]|The problem addressed is establishing a connection between the attractive Fermi-Hubbard model and the repulsive Bose-Hubbard model at finite temperature, specifically using an imaginary chemical potential to enable a correspondence. The method involves a large \(N\)-expansion, revealing that the partition functions of the two models are related by a shift of \(\theta \to \theta + \pi\), which maps the BCS-BEC crossover of fermions to a Bose-Fermi crossover with fermion-like occupation for bosons. Key results include the identification of a universal thermal window at specific angles and the demonstration that fermionization can occur at finite interaction strength through a thermodynamic effect driven by the imaginary chemical potential, rather than requiring infinite repulsion or modified statistics. This work is significant because it offers a novel thermodynamic mechanism for inducing fermion-like behavior in bosonic systems, providing a unified framework for understanding crossovers in interacting lattice systems beyond traditional approaches like the Tonks-Girardeau limit.|
|**2026-05-12**|**Layer-antisymmetric pair-phase resonance at the bonding-antibonding splitting in the AA-stacked bilayer attractive Hubbard model**|Yogeshwar Prasad|[[arxiv:2605.10387](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10387)]|The paper investigates the collective pair-phase mode in an AA-stacked bilayer s-wave superconductor, focusing on the layer-antisymmetric phase channel. Using Gaussian fluctuation theory for the attractive Hubbard model, the authors analytically demonstrate that this channel hosts an in-gap resonance at twice the interlayer hopping, which arises from an algebraic mechanism connecting the antisymmetric phase bubble to the symmetric Goldstone mode. The key result is that this resonance is fixed by single-particle hybridization rather than interaction-driven Josephson coupling, as confirmed by numerical Bogoliubov-de Gennes calculations. This work is significant because it identifies a new collective excitation with a predictable energy scale, detectable via layer-odd probes like cold-atom layer-bias experiments.|

By imposing a maximum site occupation of two and including nearest-neighbor repulsion, the authors identify the $l$ SS phase in the strong-moiré regime. This phase is distinguished from conventional supersolids by coexisting local order and global disorder, including exponentially decaying global correlations and a vanishing global structure factor. The results provide a microscopic framework and experimental observables for detecting local supersolid states in moiré optical lattices.|

Key results show that superconducting correlations shift from $d_{z^2}$- to $d_{x^2-y^2}$-dominated interlayer spin-singlet pairing while maintaining an $s_{\pm}$ structure. A key result is that the non-canonical method yields additional angular wavefunctions, given by Gegenbauer polynomials, for even and odd states. This work provides a more complete analytical framework for understanding position-dependent mass systems, which is significant for modeling realistic quantum wires.|

By tracking how variance condenses into leading PCA components, they delineate regimes dominated by charge, spin, and pairing correlations as on-site and nearest-neighbor interactions are tuned. This establishes PCA as a transparent, unsupervised framework for diagnosing correlation competition in finite strongly correlated systems.|
|**2026-05-12**|**Scaling of free cumulants in closed system-bath setups**|Merlin F\"ullgraf, Jochen Gemmer, Jiaozi Wang|[[arxiv:2511.11333](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.11333)]|This paper extends the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis framework to analyze open system-bath setups. They discover a universal scaling of microcanonical free cumulants with interaction strength for observables related to the system Hamiltonian. |**2026-05-12**|**Diagnosing phase transitions through time-scale entanglement**|Stefan Rohshap, Hirone Ishida, Frederic Bippus, Leonard M.

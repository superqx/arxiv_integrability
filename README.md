The method introduces a long-range swap model where each species has a parameter that interpolates its dynamics between two known types (TASEP and drop-push), and analyzes it using the coordinate Bethe ansatz. The key result is the proof of integrability for binary parameters and the identification of new, nontrivial integrable compositions in the continuous parameter regime. Its significance lies in expanding the known universe of exactly solvable models to include heterogeneous interaction mechanisms, paving the way for studying novel nonequilibrium phenomena.|

A key result is the explicit construction of Bäcklund transformations, which describe the system's discrete time evolution. The significance lies in providing a concrete framework that connects integrable systems to quantum K-theory, facilitating further study of the K-theoretic Peterson isomorphism.|

The significance is that this foundational result simplifies the study of \(p\)-adic dynamical systems and integrable systems by allowing a focus on equations rather than space, and it leads to a global classification of such manifolds by \(p\)-adic volume.|

The method employs the exact Bethe ansatz solution for a one-dimensional Fermi polaron with an attractive contact interaction, utilizing determinant representations of wave function overlaps and leveraging the mathematical properties of Cauchy matrices. The key result is a fully analytical derivation showing that the quasiparticle residue decays algebraically as \( Z = W N^{-\theta} \), with the exponent \( \theta \) confirmed to be \( 2\delta_F^2/\pi^2 \), where \( \delta_F \) is the phase shift at the Fermi edge. The significance lies in providing an exact, non-perturbative confirmation of this fundamental many-body physics concept within a solvable model, offering a benchmark for understanding impurity effects in quantum gases.|

The method uses a sudden quench to create an analog black hole horizon and analyzes the resulting radiation through both theoretical wave packets and a practical sensor qubit. Key results show the radiation spectrum is not perfectly thermal and that a weakly-coupled sensor can accurately measure its temperature, while strong coupling obscures the signal. This work is significant for providing concrete experimental protocols to identify true analog Hawking radiation in quantum simulators, separating it from background noise.|
|**2026-04-15**|**Exact solution of a two-dimensional (2D) Ising model with the next nearest interactions**|Zhidong Zhang|[[arxiv:2601.10749](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10749)]|This paper addresses the long-standing problem of finding an exact solution for the two-dimensional Ising model that includes interactions between next-nearest-neighbor spins. The key results are the exact derivation of the partition function and spontaneous magnetization, revealing that an increased number of interactions or topological contributions raises the critical temperature. This solution is significant for understanding the physical properties of two-dimensional magnetic materials and advances the theoretical framework for complex spin systems.|
|**2026-04-15**|**Electrostatics in semiconducting devices II: Solving the Helmholtz equation**|Antonio Lacerda-Santos, Xavier Waintal|[[arxiv:2507.03131](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.03131)]|This paper tackles the challenge of achieving reliable convergence in self-consistent quantum-electrostatic simulations, which is especially problematic under strong non-linear conditions like carrier depletion. The key result is that this approach robustly converges to the exact solution of the initial problem, typically requiring only one or two update iterations in practice. This work is significant as it provides a fast and robust numerical framework for simulating electrostatics in quantum nanoelectronic devices, overcoming a longstanding computational bottleneck.|

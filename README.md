## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-25**|**Beyond descendants: integrable observables for cohomological field theories**|Xavier Blot, Danilo Lewa\'nski, Sergey Shadrin|[[arxiv:2605.22236](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22236)]|This paper introduces a new framework for cohomological field theories by replacing standard psi classes with "integrable observables," which preserve integrability properties. The authors demonstrate this method using three examples, showing it recovers known Dubrovin-Zhang and double ramification hierarchies and also extends a recently discovered "Π-class." A key result proves that the new Π-hierarchies are Miura equivalent to both existing hierarchies, revealing deep structural connections. The significance of this work includes providing a new, concise proof of Witten's conjecture and unifying several approaches within the theory of integrable systems.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Long-time Freeness in the Kicked Top**|Elisa Vallini, Silvia Pappalardi|[[arxiv:2411.12050](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.12050)]|This paper investigates the emergence of "freeness," a statistical independence property from free probability theory, in the chaotic quantum dynamics of the kicked top model. The authors use higher-order out-of-time-order correlators to study this property and find that, in the fully chaotic regime, freeness is approached exponentially fast over long times. They introduce a large deviation theory for freeness to define and analyze the associated time scales, revealing a hierarchy of different rates that indicates a multifractal approach. The work offers new insights into the long-time thermalization of chaotic quantum systems and connects free probability to the study of complex quantum dynamics.|

## Mathematical Physics

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-05-25**|**Quantum Quenches that Resemble Operator Growth**|Xiangyu Cao|[[arxiv:2605.23874](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23874)]|This paper investigates "growth quenches," local perturbations that progressively destabilize false vacuums in constrained quantum systems, and identifies a formal analogy to operator growth dynamics. Using the Krylov recursion method, the authors extend an operator growth hypothesis to predict linear growth of Lanczos coefficients and derive a bound for a Lyapunov exponent analogue, revealing localization in Krylov and Fock spaces under certain conditions. They validate these predictions in solvable Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK)-inspired models and the 1D East-West model, observing Fock localization, ballistic transport from conserved charges, and partial localization from flat bands. The work establishes growth quenches as a valuable framework for exploring non-equilibrium coherent phenomena in many-body systems.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Interaction-Split Edge Spectral Flow and Neutral Triplet Boundary Modes in a C = 2 Hubbard Pump**|Yong-Feng Yang, Zhao-Rui Tian, Chen Cheng, Hong-Gang Luo|[[arxiv:2605.23769](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23769)]|This paper proposes a many-body Thouless pump using a spatially modulated Hubbard interaction, rather than conventional single-particle potentials. Using DMRG simulations on a period-three chain at filling 2/3, the authors find a correlated insulator with a many-body Chern number of C=+2, indicating two charges pumped per cycle. A key result is that interactions split the edge spectral flow into two distinct events, suppressing doublon transport and localizing a neutral spin-triplet excitation at the edge. This work demonstrates that electronic correlations can fundamentally restructure topological pumping, revealing interaction-driven boundary modes beyond band theory.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Exact solution of generalized gauge-invariant Ising chains with multi-spin interactions**|Pavel Khrapov, Stepan Shchurenkov|[[arxiv:2605.23228](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23228)]|This paper presents exact solutions for generalized gauge-invariant Ising chains with multi-spin interactions on strip lattices of width up to four. The authors develop a transfer-matrix method involving transformations to eliminate gauge redundancy and map the problem to an effective Ising model, enabling explicit calculation of the partition function and correlation functions. Key results include general formulas for Wilson loops and the identification of confinement-like and deconfinement-like phases based on area-law versus perimeter-law behavior. This work significantly extends classical results by providing exact solutions for a broader range of gauge-invariant models and deriving explicit string tensions and phase diagrams.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Surface States in Strain-Induced Nodal-Line Topological Semiconductors**|Vitaly N. Golovach, Alexander Khaetskii|[[arxiv:2605.23000](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23000)]|This paper investigates the topological phases of inverted-band-gap semiconductors when subjected to strain and spin-orbit coupling. Using a Luttinger Hamiltonian model, the authors analytically derive surface states across high-symmetry directions, mapping transitions between topological insulator, Dirac, nodal-line, and Weyl semimetal phases. A key result is the continuous evolution of these surface states across phase boundaries, revealing a non-analyticity in their dispersion at the projected nodal line due to distinct surface-state patches with unique spin textures. The study provides a unified framework that synthesizes previous findings and establishes energy-scale criteria for realizing these topological phases.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Fractionalization, emergent SU( $N$ ) symmetries, and fragmentation in layered quantum spin-orbital models**|Pedro M. C\^onsoli, Aayush Vijayvargia, Onur Erten|[[arxiv:2605.22908](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22908)]|This paper proposes a family of layered quantum spin-orbital models to study fractionalization and symmetry-breaking. The method involves stacking square-lattice layers with Kitaev-type interactions and coupling them via Ising spins, mapping the system to an emergent SU(N) Fermi-Hubbard model using a parton construction. Key results include the discovery of magnetic fragmentation for N>2, where orbitals form a quantum liquid state while spins exhibit either conventional or nonlocal order. This work provides a microscopic framework for exploring fractionalized phases and quantum critical points, extending the understanding of intertwined orders in strongly correlated systems.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Correlated dynamics of three-particle bound states induced by emergent impurities in Bose-Hubbard model**|Wenduo Zhao, Boning Huang, Yongguan Ke, Chaohong Lee|[[arxiv:2601.20042](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20042)]|The paper investigates the dynamics of three-particle bound states in a Bose-Hubbard model, specifically focusing on how interaction-induced impurities (created by the bound pair) lead to two distinct types: dimer-monomer bound states and bound edge states. Using theoretical analysis, the authors find that in quantum walks, the spread velocity of the dimer-monomer state is governed by the maximum group velocity of its energy band, which is much slower than for a single particle. For Bloch oscillations, the period of the dimer-monomer state is one-third that of a single particle, and bound edge states require interaction-induced defects to exceed the effective tunneling strength. This work clarifies the basic mechanics and collective behavior of three-particle bound states, revealing novel transport properties distinct from single-particle or dimer systems.|
|**2026-05-25**|**Long-time Freeness in the Kicked Top**|Elisa Vallini, Silvia Pappalardi|[[arxiv:2411.12050](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.12050)]|The paper investigates higher-order quantum correlations and the concept of "freeness" (free independence) in chaotic dynamics, using the kicked top model which transitions from integrability to chaos. Numerically studying \(2n\)-point out-of-time-order correlators, the authors find that in the fully chaotic regime, freeness is achieved exponentially fast over time. They introduce a large deviation theory to define and analyze the associated time scales, revealing a hierarchy of multifractal behavior in the approach to freeness. The results offer deeper insights into long-time chaotic quantum dynamics and have potential implications for understanding many-body quantum systems.|

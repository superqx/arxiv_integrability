|**2026-07-13**|**Cherednik integrable system: eigenfunctions at generic eigenvalues**|A. Mironov, A. Morozov, A. Popolitov|[[arxiv:2607.09203](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09203)]|The paper addresses the problem of constructing eigenfunctions with generic eigenvalues for the Cherednik integrable system, whereas existing polynomial eigenfunctions (non-symmetric Macdonald polynomials) are limited to specific eigenvalues. The method adapts a recursion and analytic continuation technique previously used by Noumi and Shiraishi for the Ruijsenaars-Schneider system to the Cherednik case. The key result is that generic eigenfunctions are not simple power series but consist of \( N! \) branches, each with a more complex structure. This finding is significant as it reveals a fundamental structural difference between related integrable systems and provides explicit formulas for special cases.|
|**2026-07-13**|**A diagrammatic field theory of quantum error correction**|Steven Rayan|[[arxiv:2607.08911](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08911)]|This paper proposes a field-theoretic framework for quantum error correction using fusion-space codes from unitary fusion categories. The method distinguishes between diagnostic footprint algebras, which reveal local error data, and syndrome-admissible commuting algebras, which can be measured without leaking logical information. A key result is that exact correctability reduces to fibrewise Knill-Laflamme conditions, with a concrete demonstration in the Ising theory showing how four and six sigma punctures realize complementary diagnostics and explicit error recovery. The work establishes a topological criterion for code correctability and a Peierls-type threshold theorem, advancing a rigorous categorical approach to fault-tolerant quantum memory.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Exact Lindbladian Dynamics from Conformal Embeddings and Topological Defects in Conformal Field Theory**|Chen Bai|[[arxiv:2607.08827](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08827)]|This paper addresses the challenge of solving the dynamics of open quantum systems in conformal field theory (CFT). The authors identify intrinsic conformal structures—conformal embeddings and topological defects—that make the Lindbladian dynamics exactly solvable. Key results include the triangular structure of the adjoint Lindbladian for Majorana fermions, exact evolution of currents in Wess-Zumino-Witten models, and diagonalizable dynamics in rational CFTs via Verlinde lines. The significance lies in demonstrating that these conformal structures yield exact, recursive, or algebraic solutions for open quantum dynamics in (1+1)D.|
|**2026-07-13**|**A Self-Consistent Exact Solution from Einstein Gravity: Black Hole in King $\left(2,3,0\right)$ Dark Matter Halos**|David Senjaya|[[arxiv:2607.08792](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08792)]|This paper presents an exact solution for a static, spherically symmetric black hole embedded in a Dehnen (2,3,0) dark matter halo, derived directly from the full Einstein field equations. The study shows that the halo significantly alters photon trajectories, shifting circular photon orbits and deforming gravitational lensing, while also modifying the quasinormal mode spectrum and the black hole's thermodynamic properties. Key results include an enlarged photon sphere and shadow radius, expanded thermodynamic stability, and nontrivial phase structures absent in the vacuum Schwarzschild case. The findings demonstrate that realistic dark matter environments produce observable gravitational and thermodynamic deviations from isolated black holes, offering potential signatures for detecting halo-induced effects.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Lattice Field Theory Analysis of the Chiral Heisenberg Model**|Simon Hands, Johann Ostmeyer|[[arxiv:2603.29461](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29461)]|This paper addresses the universal behavior of the Hubbard model near the semi-metal to Mott insulator transition by formulating and simulating the 3D chiral Heisenberg model as a lattice field theory. Using domain wall fermions and the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm, the authors located the phase transition for spontaneous SU(2) symmetry breaking and extracted critical exponents. The obtained exponent estimates differ significantly from previous (2+1)D simulations but align more closely with 3D covariant analytic predictions, resolving a theoretical discrepancy. The study also demonstrates a method to extract the fermion correlator signal by rotating the source in isospace, providing foundational results for future determinations of the fermion anomalous dimension.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Exactly Solvable 1+1d Chiral Lattice Gauge Theories**|Sahand Seifnashri|[[arxiv:2601.14359](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.14359)]|This paper presents a method for constructing exactly solvable chiral lattice gauge theories in two spacetime dimensions using a modified Villain Hamiltonian formulation. A specific quadratic Hamiltonian is derived for a model chiral gauge theory (the "34-50" model) to serve as a concrete example. By showing that multiple copies of this theory realize O(N,N;ℤ) T-duality transformations, the authors provide a tool to exactly solve and analyze these models. The work offers a rigorous lattice construction for a class of chiral theories, which are typically difficult to simulate in non-abelian or higher-dimensional settings.|
|**2026-07-13**|**2d BF Theory Coupled to 1d Quantum Mechanics: The Phase Space and Its Quantization**|Seyed Faroogh Moosavian, Yehao Zhou|[[arxiv:2111.06876](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.06876)]|This paper analyzes the classical and quantum phase space of 2D BF theory coupled to 1D quantum mechanics with specific gauge symmetries. The authors first construct the classical phase space and describe its ring of gauge-invariant functions, including its large-\(N\) limit, and compute the Hilbert series for finite and infinite \(N\). They then quantize this phase space, linking the resulting algebra to the Yangian and defining its coproduct. A key result identifies the quantized algebras with the Coulomb branch algebras of certain 3d \(\mathcal{N}=4\) quiver gauge theories, establishing a significant connection between these different physical systems.|

The method involves performing Lie reductions based on this classification and constructing exact solutions. Key results show that all equations in the class admit mass and \(L^2\)-norm conservation laws, while energy-type conservation laws only exist for specific coefficient cases that align with the symmetry classification. The method involves generalizing the Noumi-Shiraishi approach—a recursion and analytic continuation of Macdonald polynomials—to the non-symmetric polynomial eigenfunctions of this system. The key result shows that the generic eigenfunction is more complex than in the symmetric case, consisting of \(N!\) branches, each expressed as a Noumi-Shiraishi-type power series. The work is significant as it extends the construction of universal eigenfunctions to a broader class of integrable systems beyond the symmetric Ruijsenaars-Schneider model.|
|**2026-07-13**|**A diagrammatic field theory of quantum error correction**|Steven Rayan|[[arxiv:2607.08911](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08911)]|This paper introduces a field-theoretic framework for quantum error correction using fusion-space codes in unitary fusion categories. The work provides a categorical approach to quantum error correction, with potential connections to representation theory and algebraic geometry.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Superintegrable 2D systems in magnetic fields with a parabolic type integral**|Tatiana Ekelchik, Antonella Marchesiello|[[arxiv:2604.20465](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20465)]|This paper investigates two-dimensional superintegrable systems in Euclidean space with a magnetic field, focusing on cases where integrals of motion are quadratic polynomials in momenta. The authors extend prior work by assuming one integral is of parabolic type and the other is elliptic or a "non-standard" parabolic type. Their analysis confirms that under these conditions, the only possible quadratic superintegrable system is one with a constant magnetic field and constant electrostatic potential. This result reinforces the limited number of superintegrable systems on the Euclidean plane, consolidating earlier classifications.|

At 1/3 filling, the authors discover a continuous topological quantum phase transition driven by electron interactions, moving from a \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) topological insulator to a strongly correlated insulator with a spin Chern number \(C_s = 2\). This work is significant for establishing a unified solvable framework for both topological and Mott transitions in strongly correlated systems.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Exact Lindbladian Dynamics from Conformal Embeddings and Topological Defects in Conformal Field Theory**|Chen Bai|[[arxiv:2607.08827](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08827)]|This paper proposes using intrinsic conformal structures, specifically conformal embeddings and topological defects, to solve the dynamics of open quantum systems in (1+1)D conformal field theories exactly. The method involves analyzing the adjoint Lindbladian for \(N\) Majorana fermions, revealing a triangular structure that leads to recursive exact solutions, and extending this to Wess-Zumino-Witten models and diagonal rational CFTs. Key results show that conformal embeddings enable exact evolution of affine-current products, while Verlinde topological defect lines yield exactly diagonal dynamics for primary sectors, with dephasing rates determined by the modular \(S\) matrix. This work is significant as it identifies mechanisms for exact solvability in open conformal dynamics, offering a rare exact framework for analyzing quantum many-body systems.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Lattice Field Theory Analysis of the Chiral Heisenberg Model**|Simon Hands, Johann Ostmeyer|[[arxiv:2603.29461](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29461)]|This paper investigates the universal behavior of the Hubbard model at the semi-metal to Mott insulator transition by formulating the 3D chiral Heisenberg model as a lattice field theory with domain wall fermions. Using numerical simulations, the authors locate the phase transition corresponding to spontaneous SU(2) symmetry breaking and extract critical exponents. The key results show that the exponents differ significantly from (2+1)D simulations but align more closely with analytic 3D covariant field theory predictions. The study also presents first results for the fermion correlator, introducing a method to rotate the fermion source in isospace to obtain a signal, advancing understanding of the relevant universality class.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Exactly Solvable 1+1d Chiral Lattice Gauge Theories**|Sahand Seifnashri|[[arxiv:2601.14359](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.14359)]|This paper solves the problem of constructing exactly solvable abelian chiral lattice gauge theories in two spacetime dimensions. The method uses the modified Villain formulation of the compact boson to build an explicit quadratic lattice Hamiltonian, using a "34-50" chiral gauge theory as an example. Key results include the realization of O(N,N;ℤ) T-duality transformations for multiple copies, which allows for exact analysis of the models. The significance lies in providing a rigorous lattice framework for chiral gauge theories that can be solved and studied analytically.|
|**2026-07-13**|**Amplitude mode in two-dimensional coherent spectroscopy of weak-coupling antiferromagnets**|Jiyu Chen, Naoto Tsuji, Philipp Werner|[[arxiv:2504.21351](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.21351)]|This paper uses nonequilibrium dynamical mean-field theory to simulate two-dimensional coherent spectroscopy (2DCS) for the Hubbard model, focusing on weak-coupling antiferromagnets. The method compares static and dynamic Hartree terms to isolate the collective amplitude mode's contribution to the 2DCS signal. Key results show that with broadband pulses, both rephasing and nonrephasing peaks at the gap energy are dominated by this amplitude mode. The significance lies in demonstrating that narrow-band pulses at half the gap frequency can strongly enhance these amplitude mode signals, offering a clear spectroscopic signature.|

|**2026-07-13**|**Field observation of soliton gases in the deep open ocean**|Yu-Chen Lee, Sander Wahls|[[arxiv:2510.04662](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04662)]|This paper presents the first field evidence of soliton gases in the deep open ocean, using buoy measurements from Taiwanese waters. The authors applied nonlinear Fourier transforms to quantify the soliton energy ratio in wave records, identifying sea states dominated by solitons rather than linear dispersive waves. After filtering out directional interference, three wave records from the Eluanbi station confirmed genuine soliton gas conditions, characterized by short-period, relatively small waves with extreme steepness. These findings demonstrate that soliton gases occur naturally in the open ocean, expanding their observation beyond shallow lagoons and advancing understanding of nonlinear ocean wave dynamics.|

|**2026-06-17**|**Hamiltonian formalism for Bose excitations in a plasma with a non-Abelian interaction: plasmon bremsstrahlung**|Yu. A. Markov, M. A. Markova, N. Yu. Markov|[[arxiv:2606.17670](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17670)]|This paper develops a classical Hamiltonian framework to describe plasmon bremsstrahlung radiation from high-energy color-charged particles in a hot quark-gluon plasma. The method generalizes the Lie-Poisson bracket to QCD, introducing a two-component bosonic field variable and two non-Abelian color charges, with canonical transformations expressed as integro-power series up to sixth order. Key results include a self-consistent system of four Boltzmann-type kinetic equations for the plasmon number density matrix and an exact solution for the dynamics of its colorless and color components under fixed color charges. The work is significant for providing a systematic Hamiltonian formalism to study non-Abelian collective excitations and their kinetic evolution in hot QCD matter.|
|**2026-06-17**|**The Helical SYK Model and Emergent Infrared Integrability**|Gustavo Valdivia-Mera, Bhavay Tyagi, Eric R. Bittner, Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2606.17161](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17161)]|The paper introduces a helical version of the SYK model in 1+1 dimensions using left- and right-moving Majorana fermions. The authors methodically organize quartic interactions into chirality sectors, showing that symmetry can enforce an exactly solvable, integrable density-density form. Key results demonstrate that while the full interaction space breaks this integrability, the large-N infrared limit remains tractable, with all interactions becoming marginally irrelevant. This reveals that the theory flows to a free, integrable fixed point in the infrared, highlighting emergent integrability driven by disorder and symmetry.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Spinning test particles in the spacetime of a global monopole**|Morteza Mohseni, Saurya Das|[[arxiv:2606.14509](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14509)]|This paper investigates the motion of spinning test particles in a global monopole spacetime using the Mathisson-Papapetrou-Dixon (MPD) equations. The authors derive a general exact solution to these equations by exploiting the spacetime symmetries, showing that trajectories, momenta, and spin can be expressed in terms of three specific functions of the angular coordinates. Key results include demonstrating that the system is completely integrable and obtaining both general non-geodesic trajectories and particular cases like radial and planar motion. The significance lies in presenting the first general exact solution to the MPD equations in a non-maximally symmetric curved spacetime, advancing the understanding of spin-curvature coupling effects in topological defect backgrounds.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Universal Theory of Decaying Turbulence**|Alexander Migdal|[[arxiv:2604.12207](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12207)]|This paper presents an exact solution for the statistics of freely decaying turbulence in any spatial dimension \(d>1\). The authors use loop equations and the Mandelstam identity to show that the nonlinear advection term vanishes, reducing the momentum-loop equation to a purely diffusive form that describes a random walk on a circle. Key results include a universal energy scaling function independent of dimension \(d\), an infinite cascade of finite-time transitions linked to the Riemann zeta function, and the absence of genuine finite-time singularities. The significance lies in providing an exact analytic alternative to classical turbulence theories and offering predictions that closely match numerical simulations.|

Perel|[[arxiv:2606.17689](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17689)]|The paper addresses the problem of charge carrier tunneling in graphene through an n-p-n junction under smoothly varying, nonuniform electrostatic and magnetic fields. The authors develop a semiclassical method that reduces the scattering problem to a connection problem at degeneracy points, going beyond prior work limited to constant magnetic fields. Key results include explicit formulas for reflection and transmission coefficients, including phase information, along with semiclassical conditions for Fabry-Pérot resonances and "magic" angles. This work is significant for advancing the theoretical understanding of transport in graphene under realistic, spatially varying field configurations.|

The key method involves showing that the probability current can be decomposed into a superposition of two currents, each linked to a specific spinor component and its corresponding probability flux. This analysis yields a new system of Hamilton-Jacobi equations and equations of motion for charged particles in electromagnetic fields that account for spin-field interaction. The significance is demonstrated by applying these equations to an exact solution of the Pauli equation under a uniform magnetic field and an asymmetric quadratic potential.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Universal Theory of Decaying Turbulence**|Alexander Migdal|[[arxiv:2604.12207](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12207)]|This paper presents an exact analytic solution to the problem of freely decaying turbulence in any dimension, derived using loop equations and the Mandelstam identity. The method shows that the nonlinear advection term vanishes, reducing the momentum-loop equation to a purely diffusive process described by a random walk on a circle. A key result is that the energy spectrum is governed by a universal Mellin transform, which replaces the classical Kraichnan–Batchelor–Leith \(k^{-3.5}\) scaling with a continuous effective index, while the Riemann hypothesis links an infinite cascade of finite-time transitions to a singular behavior at infinite time. The significance lies in providing an exact, dimension-independent theoretical framework that matches 3D direct numerical simulations and unifies previously reported "multifractal" transients under a single scaling function.|

|**2026-06-17**|**Coherent effects in quantum transport models and their classical counterparts**|N. S. Maslova, V. N. Mantsevich, I. M. Sokolov, P. I. Arseyev|[[arxiv:2606.18224](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18224)]|The paper investigates how quasiparticles, locally excited at a specific initial moment, transport in exactly solvable quantum models. The authors find that the time-dependent probability distribution function (PDF) in these quantum systems closely resembles that of classical continuous-time random walks (CTRW), including Lévy walks and diffusing diffusivity. This reveals a coherent effect linking quantum transport to classical stochastic processes. The results are significant because they establish a formal connection between quantum and classical transport, offering a pathway to interpret quantum dynamics through classical frameworks.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Three-dimensional repulsive Hubbard model**|Alexei Sherman|[[arxiv:2606.17933](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17933)]|This study uses the strong-coupling diagram technique to analyze the three-dimensional repulsive Hubbard model. For half-filling, the researchers mapped the boundary between paramagnetic and antiferromagnetic states for Hubbard repulsion values between \(4t\) and \(12t\), identifying a Mott transition at \(U \approx 9t\) via density of states calculations. The work further reveals that the Néel temperature's dependence on electron concentration differs significantly at weak (\(U=4t\)) versus strong (\(U=12t\)) coupling, with the latter exhibiting a plateau linked to a restructuring of the density of states. The findings provide new insights into magnetic phase transitions and electron correlation effects in three dimensions.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Semiclassical Analysis of Tunneling in Graphene under Nonuniform Electrostatic and Magnetic Fields**|Maria V. Perel|[[arxiv:2606.17689](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17689)]|This paper develops a semiclassical theory for electron tunneling in monolayer graphene through an n-p-n junction under a perpendicular magnetic field, extending beyond prior work that only considered constant fields. The method reduces the scattering problem to a connection between degeneracy points using a semiclassical approximation, yielding analytic formulas for reflection and transmission coefficients. Key results include the derivation of transmission phases, Fabry-Pérot resonance conditions, and "magic" angles, with numerical simulations showing the magnetic field’s influence on angular transmission dependence. This work provides a general framework for analyzing tunneling in non-uniform fields, with significance for graphene-based quantum devices and interference effects.|
|**2026-06-17**|**The Helical SYK Model and Emergent Infrared Integrability**|Gustavo Valdivia-Mera, Bhavay Tyagi, Eric R. Bittner, Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2606.17161](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17161)]|The paper introduces the Helical SYK model in 1+1 dimensions with quartic interactions among Majorana fermions, arranged by chirality into a hierarchical structure. The most constrained symmetry sector reduces to a solvable density-density form via bosonization, granting integrability, while broader sectors break this structure. Using conformal perturbation theory and disorder averaging, the authors show that all interactions are marginally irrelevant in the large-\(N\) infrared limit. This demonstrates that the theory becomes free and integrable in the deep IR, revealing emergent integrability from a disordered, non-integrable UV theory.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Universal Design and Physical Applications of Non-Uniform Cellular Automata on Translationally Invariant Lattices**|Xiang-You Huang, Jie-Yu Zhang, Peng Ye|[[arxiv:2605.13379](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13379)]|This paper introduces higher-order non-uniform cellular automata (NUCA) as a universal framework for studying physical systems on translationally invariant regular lattices, including hyperbolic ones. The method deforms hyperbolic lattices to embed them into Euclidean square lattices, preserving locality while enabling geometry-dependent update rules. Key results include the construction of exactly solvable subsystem symmetry-protected topological models and non-uniform Clifford quantum cellular automata for hyperbolic quantum states, as well as a probabilistic NUCA for directed percolation on hyperbolic lattices. The work’s significance lies in unifying the study of geometry-driven physics across Euclidean and hyperbolic lattices, opening systematic routes for quantum and statistical mechanics in curved spaces.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Stability of current-carrying states in hard-core bosons with long-range hopping on a square lattice**|Yoshihiro Yabuuchi, Ippei Danshita|[[arxiv:2511.14260](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.14260)]|This paper investigates the stability of current-carrying states in a Bose-condensed system of hard-core bosons on a square lattice with algebraically decaying long-range hopping. Using mean-field theory, the authors analyze the excitation spectrum to identify critical quasi-momenta where Landau and dynamical instabilities occur. They find that long-range hopping suppresses these critical quasi-momenta, causing them to vanish at the exponent \(\alpha = 3\), with a distinct scaling behavior near this point. The results highlight how long-range interactions fundamentally alter the stability of superfluid currents, with implications for quantum transport in systems like trapped ions or ultracold atoms.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Stochastic dynamics of quasiparticles in the hard rod gas**|Seema Chahal, Indranil Mukherjee, Abhishek Dhar, Herbert Spohn, Anupam Kundu|[[arxiv:2510.19693](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.19693)]|This paper studies the stochastic motion of quasiparticles in a one-dimensional gas of hard rods, an integrable model. The authors compute exact mean, variance, and two-time correlations of individual quasiparticles under various background conditions, revealing they behave as drifting Brownian particles. A key result shows that two tagged quasiparticles in a homogeneous background move like a rigid rod at late times due to strong correlations. The work provides both a microscopic derivation and a fluctuating hydrodynamic theory, enhancing the understanding of transport in integrable systems.|
|**2026-06-17**|**Enhanced Superconducting Diode Effect in the Asymmetric Hatsugai-Kohmoto Model**|Kai Chen, Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2510.17452](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.17452)]|This paper investigates the superconducting diode effect (SDE) in strongly correlated electron systems, a gap in existing research that has mainly focused on weak correlations. The authors use the exactly solvable Hatsugai-Kohmoto (HK) interaction model in asymmetric band metals, combining low-energy analysis with numerical self-consistent calculations. The key result is that the HK interaction can significantly enhance the quality factor of the SDE. This finding provides new insight into how strong electron-electron correlations influence nonreciprocal supercurrents.|

Bezerra de Mello, Herondy Mota|[[arxiv:2607.15070](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15070)]|This paper addresses the Casimir effect for a massive scalar field between parallel plates with a spatially varying effective mass. The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation to find exact normal modes, revealing a Landau-like energy spectrum without an external magnetic field, and compute the vacuum energy using zeta-function regularization. Key results show that the vacuum energy splits into two contributions, both exponentially suppressed in the strong-coupling regime, with the Landau-like term reproducing the standard Casimir energy in the weak-coupling limit. This work establishes a new exactly solvable model connecting position-dependent masses to boundary-induced quantum vacuum phenomena, offering insights for spatially inhomogeneous relativistic systems.|
|**2026-07-17**|**Birman-Schwinger Formulation of the Faddeev--Popov Zero-Mode Problem**|Daniel G. Tedesco|[[arxiv:2607.14425](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14425)]|This paper presents a new, direct formulation of the Gribov horizon problem in Yang-Mills theory by constructing a bounded operator from the Faddeev-Popov operator. The key result is that the horizon condition is exactly defined by a spectral threshold where an eigenvalue of this operator crosses -1, making the formulation more precise than previous indirect methods. The method also shows that Gribov's no-pole condition is simply the leading approximation of the ghost dressing function, clarifying both its validity and its limitations. The paper's significance is demonstrated with an exact solution for a periodic background field, providing a solvable reference for understanding the horizon mechanism, though its extension to general configurations remains an open challenge.|
|**2026-07-17**|**Ising the way into de Sitter**|Giovanni Galati, Stathis Vitouladitis|[[arxiv:2607.14219](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14219)]|This paper studies the two-dimensional Ising model deformed by a thermal operator on de Sitter (dS) spacetime. Using fermionisation, the authors compute exact two-point correlation functions for various operators and analytically continue them to Lorentzian dS. Key results show that late-time behavior aligns with dS representation theory and includes principal-series oscillations, while perturbative methods fail due to divergent secular terms. The work demonstrates how non-perturbative effects resolve these pathologies, offering a solvable model for quantum field theory in de Sitter space.|
|**2026-07-17**|**Exact general solutions for cosmological scalar field evolution in a vacuum-energy dominated expansion**|Patrick Hu, Robert J. The method involves identifying linear differential equation cases for constant, linear, and quadratic potentials, and deriving parametric solutions for nonlinear potentials like \( V = V_0 \ln \phi \). Key results show these parametric solutions are less practical than those for barotropic fluids, but the authors find a crucial distinction: the slow-roll approximation universally applies to flat potentials during vacuum-dominated expansion, unlike during matter or radiation domination. The significance lies in clarifying which approximation schemes are valid for scalar field dynamics in dark-energy-dominated phases.|

This transformation generates new solutions for integrable systems such as the Novikov-Veselov equation and the two-dimensional sine-Gordon equation. The work provides a new tool for constructing explicit solutions to these physically important equations.|
|**2026-07-17**|**Twisted Yangians of types BI, CI, DI and Drinfeld type current relations**|Vidas Regelskis|[[arxiv:2607.14692](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14692)]|This paper develops new presentations for twisted Yangians associated with symmetric pairs of types BI, CI, and DI. The authors introduce a "transposed presentation" governed by a modified reflection equation and derive Drinfeld-type current relations with closed-form Serre formulas. Key results include establishing the conjectured isomorphism between the R-matrix and Drinfeld presentations and decomposing the extended twisted Yangian as a tensor product with a polynomial ring. The work provides explicit Poincaré-Birkhoff-Witt bases and coideal coproducts, significantly advancing the structural understanding of these quantum algebras.|
|**2026-07-17**|**Exact general solutions for cosmological scalar field evolution in a vacuum-energy dominated expansion**|Patrick Hu, Robert J. The method solves the field equations for several potentials, including constant, linear, quadratic, and specific nonlinear forms such as logarithmic and power-law potentials, yielding parametric solutions and a first integral for the logarithmic case. Key results show that these nonlinear solutions are less practical than those for matter- or radiation-dominated universes, but the authors generalize the slow-roll approximation, proving it applies to all sufficiently flat potentials during vacuum-dominated expansion—unlike for \(w_B > -1\) fluids. The significance lies in providing exact benchmarks for inflationary dynamics and clarifying when slow-roll approximations are valid in accelerating universes.|
|**2026-07-17**|**Discrete-time maximally superintegrable systems and deformed symmetry algebras: the Calogero-Moser case**|Pavel Drozdov, Giorgio Gubbiotti, Danilo Latini|[[arxiv:2601.10625](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10625)]|This paper investigates the symmetry algebra of the discrete-time Calogero-Moser system, a discretized version of the integrable N-body model. The authors show that the discretization process naturally deforms the original continuous symmetry algebra, with the discretization parameter acting as a deformation parameter. They find that this deformation leads to novel polynomial algebraic structures and unexpectedly connects the symmetry algebras to Bell polynomials through trace properties. The work establishes discrete superintegrable systems as a source of deformed algebraic structures and reveals surprising links to combinatorial mathematics.|
|**2026-07-17**|**An exactly solvable asymmetric simple inclusion process**|Arvind Ayyer, Samarth Misra|[[arxiv:2510.09191](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.09191)]|This paper introduces a generalized asymmetric simple inclusion process (ASIP) on a periodic lattice, called the (q, t, θ)-ASIP, where hopping rates are deformed using t-analogues. The authors show the process belongs to the misanthrope class, making its steady state independent of the asymmetry parameter q. Key results include exact computation of the steady state, where single-site occupation probabilities follow a beta-binomial distribution at t=1, and identification of palindromic symmetries in the weights for t ≠ 1. The work is significant for providing an exactly solvable model that reveals rich phase diagrams and establishes links to processes with uniform steady states, offering new tools for analyzing non-equilibrium systems.|

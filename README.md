|**2026-05-18**|**Non-combinatorial involutive braidings: the quantum algebra $\mathfrak{gl}_{k,m}$**|Anastasia Doikou|[[arxiv:2605.16121](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16121)]|This paper investigates involutive, non-combinatorial solutions to the braid equation as deformations of the permutation map. The authors identify the associated quantum algebra as the $\mathfrak{gl}_{k,m}$ Yangian, a subalgebra of the standard Yangian. They construct highest-weight modules of this algebra, which provide eigenstates for quantum spin-chain Hamiltonians, with the $\mathfrak{gl}_{1,1}$ case reducing to a variant of the Heisenberg XX model. A key result is a comprehensive analysis of combinatorial bases for $\mathfrak{gl}_{1,1}$ representations, explicitly linking them to Young tableau shapes.|

|**2026-05-18**|**Staggering domino-like blast front motion in a one-dimensional cold gas**|Taras Holovatch, Yuri Kozitsky, Krzysztof Pilorz, Yurij Holovatch|[[arxiv:2605.16125](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16125)]|This paper studies a one-dimensional gas model of alternating masses (\(m, \mu\)) where particles collide elastically after an initial impulse. The authors discover that for specific mass ratios \(\mathcal{M}_k\), the system transitions from typical hydrodynamic blast wave behavior to a ballistic, "staggering domino-like" motion. In this exact solution, only a single triplet of particles moves at any time while all others remain at rest, causing the shock front to travel at constant initial velocity. The significance lies in identifying a new, analytically solvable regime that diverges sharply from standard hydrodynamic predictions.|
|**2026-05-18**|**Spherically symmetric approaches in the theoretical study of low-dimensional magnets**|A. F. Barabanov, V. E. Valiulin, A. V. Mikheyenkov, P. S. Savchenkov|[[arxiv:2605.16059](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16059)]|This paper presents the spherically symmetric self-consistent approach (SSCA), also known as the rotation-invariant Green’s function method (RGM), as a theoretical framework for studying low-dimensional Heisenberg-type spin models, including frustrated ones. The method addresses challenges in traditional analyses by carefully incorporating key theoretical constraints, such as the Mermin-Wagner theorem and the site spin constraint, to accurately describe systems where average spin may be zero. Key results demonstrate the method's ability to generate spin structures and phase diagrams for various lattice models, including the J₁-J₂ and J₁-J₂-J₃ models on a square lattice, while also being extendable to spin-pseudospin models and more complex systems like the Hubbard and t-J models. The significance of this work lies in providing a robust, rotation-invariant tool for exploring quantum fluctuations and frustration in low-dimensional magnetic systems, areas where conventional approaches often fail.|
|**2026-05-18**|**Born-rule statistical dynamical quantum phase transitions under measurement**|Guan-Hua Chen, Guo-Yi Zhu|[[arxiv:2605.16029](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16029)]|This paper addresses the problem of characterizing dynamical quantum phase transitions (DQPTs) in a statistical framework based on Born's rule. The authors sample all possible post-measurement states of a one-dimensional quantum Ising chain, mapping the resulting distribution to a statistical model to define an average dynamical free energy. Key results include the recovery of DQPT signals under high-moment averaging and the observation of delocalized level distributions near critical times, alongside the vanishing of Yang-Lee-Fisher zeros through complex time analysis. The work establishes a framework for statistical DQPTs and proposes a measurement-based quantum computation protocol, offering a practical route for experimental investigation in quantum devices.|
|**2026-05-18**|**Solving Classical and Quantum Spin Glasses with Deep Boltzmann Quantum States**|Luca Leone, Arka Dutta, Markus Heyl, Enrico Prati, Pietro Torta|[[arxiv:2605.15899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15899)]|This paper addresses the challenge of solving classical and quantum spin glasses, which are difficult for standard methods due to numerous local energy minima. The authors propose Deep Boltzmann Quantum States, a neural network model that uses efficient block Gibbs sampling, combined with natural-gradient updates and a training algorithm that gradually increases problem hardness. Key results show that the method matches exact solutions or best estimates for spin-glass models with hundreds of spins and solves instances of the NP-hard Job Shop Scheduling Problem. The significance lies in demonstrating that deep neural architectures with global updates and an annealing-like scheme offer a powerful tool for hard combinatorial optimization and the study of disordered quantum systems.|
|**2026-05-18**|**Revealing Hidden Correlations in a Fermi-Hubbard system via Interaction Ramps**|Botond Oreg, Carter Turnbaugh, Jens Hertkorn, Ningyuan Jia, Martin Zwierlein|[[arxiv:2605.14909](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14909)]|The paper addresses the challenge of detecting hidden charge-density-wave correlations in the attractive Fermi-Hubbard model. The authors use a rapid boost of interaction strength to convert nonlocal pairs into doublons, which enhances the visibility of these correlations. Key results show that the enhancement is strongest in the strongly correlated regime and allows distinction between a Fermi liquid and a pseudogap phase. This technique is significant for potentially revealing exotic pair orders in spin-imbalanced systems and stripe order in the repulsive Hubbard model.|
|**2026-05-18**|**Transition between 2D Symmetry Protected Topological Phases on a Klein Bottle**|Vibhu Ravindran, Bowen Yang, Xie Chen|[[arxiv:2510.00587](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.00587)]|This paper investigates the behavior of a 2+1D Z₂ symmetry-protected topological (SPT) phase on a Klein bottle, a non-orientable manifold. The authors find that inserting a symmetry defect along the orientation-reversing cycle induces an extra charge in the ground state. Crucially, this response persists across the phase transition into the trivial phase, causing an exact two-fold ground state degeneracy independent of system size. The results are demonstrated with exactly solvable models and linked to the modular transformation of 3+1D Z₂ gauge theory, highlighting the emergent parity symmetry in the SPT phase.|
|**2026-05-18**|**Exactly Solvable Phase Transition in a Cavity-Coupled 1D Ising Chain**|Shuntaro Otake, Motoaki Bamba|[[arxiv:2507.01486](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.01486)]|This paper addresses the problem of whether phase transitions can occur in one-dimensional Ising chains, which normally lack them. The authors model a 1D Ising chain coupled to a cavity photon mode and solve it exactly. They find that the system exhibits a finite-temperature superradiant phase transition, driven by long-range interactions mediated by photons. This work provides the simplest solvable example of such a transition, highlighting the role of cavity-mediated interactions in enabling phase changes in low-dimensional systems.|

|**2026-05-18**|**Staggering domino-like blast front motion in a one-dimensional cold gas**|Taras Holovatch, Yuri Kozitsky, Krzysztof Pilorz, Yurij Holovatch|[[arxiv:2605.16125](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16125)]|This paper investigates a one-dimensional model of alternating mass particles ( $m, \mu$) that mimics blast wave hydrodynamics via elastic collisions, initialized by pushing the leftmost particle. The authors discover that for specific mass ratios $\mathcal{M}_k$ , the typical shock front dynamics change drastically: instead of a slowing front and particle recoil, exactly one moving triplet of particles propagates ballistically while all others remain stationary. This "staggering domino-like" behavior is an exact solution, yielding explicit formulas for these critical mass ratios, particle positions, and velocities. The findings reveal a sharp transition from hydrodynamic-like to purely ballistic propagation, significantly expanding the understanding of mass-dependent dynamics in such systems.|

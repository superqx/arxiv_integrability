|**2026-06-29**|**Multi--black holes in Bertotti--Robinson spacetime**|Hideo Furugori, Jun-ichi Sakamoto, Shinya Tomizawa|[[arxiv:2606.28232](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28232)]|The paper addresses the problem of constructing multi-black hole solutions in a Bertotti-Robinson spacetime background. The method employs a monodromy-matrix formalism from integrable sigma models, using nilpotent algebraic structures to factorize and reconstruct gravitational solutions from an extremal Reissner-Nordström seed. Key results include the explicit construction of multi-center configurations with regular, extremal black holes, each possessing an AdS₂ × S² near-horizon geometry, and generalization to stationary and Israel-Wilson-Perjés-type solutions. The significance lies in demonstrating a systematic framework for building multi-black hole solutions in nontrivial backgrounds, with potential for further extensions.|

The work also demonstrates that key properties, including monodromy action formulas, recurrence relations, and the coproduct formula, follow directly from this definition. This establishes a foundational framework for analyzing quantum integrable systems with these symmetry groups, clarifying the structure of Bethe vectors.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-06-29**|**A universal and efficient hybrid digital-analog fermionic quantum simulator**|Hao-Tian Wei, Kaden R. A. Hazzard|[[arxiv:2606.05517](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05517)]|This paper proposes a universal framework for using fermionic ultracold atom platforms as hybrid digital-analog quantum simulators. The method employs variational algorithms to simulate the ground-state properties of target Hamiltonians that are not natively implemented in the hardware. Key results show that the quantum evolution time scales polynomially with inverse error, offering exponential speedups over classical methods, with numerical evidence provided for the Hubbard, pairing, and Hofstadter-Hubbard models. The work is significant for demonstrating that current fermionic quantum hardware can simulate a much broader class of many-body systems than their native interactions allow.|
|**2026-06-29**|**Study of the triangular-lattice Hubbard model with constrained-path quantum Monte Carlo**|Shu Fay Ung, Ankit Mahajan, David R. Reichman|[[arxiv:2603.14808](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14808)]|This study benchmarks the constrained-path quantum Monte Carlo (CPMC) method on the triangular-lattice Hubbard model, a strongly correlated and frustrated system. Using symmetry-adapted trial wave functions, the method achieves high accuracy, with energy deviations under 1% from exact results at most fillings. However, at half-filling with strong frustration, simple trial wave functions perform poorly, necessitating computationally efficient symmetry-projected trials. The results demonstrate that CPMC with symmetry projection is a scalable and practical tool for investigating competing ground states in frustrated quantum systems.|
|**2026-06-29**|**Identification and optimization of accurate spin models for Fermi-Hubbard ladders using matrix product states**|Andoni Agirre, Thomas Frederiksen, Geza Giedke, Tobias Grass|[[arxiv:2512.18695](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.18695)]|This paper addresses the challenge of modeling spin interactions in non-bipartite nanographene ladders. The authors use density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) simulations of the Fermi-Hubbard model on oligo(indenoindene) structures. They identify optimized delocalized fermionic modes that act as emergent spin-1/2 degrees of freedom and show that the low-energy physics maps accurately onto a frustrated J1-J2 Heisenberg chain. This work provides a compact, accurate spin model for correlated behavior in open-shell nanographenes, clarifying how spin interactions emerge in such systems.|
|**2026-06-29**|**Kondo breakdown induced by non-Hermitian complex hybridization**|Kazuki Yamamoto, Masaya Nakagawa, Norio Kawakami|[[arxiv:2510.20186](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.20186)]|The paper studies the mechanism of Kondo breakdown in non-Hermitian systems, focusing on a model with complex hybridization rather than explicit loss terms. Using slave-boson mean-field theory and exact Bethe ansatz solutions, the authors show that this complex hybridization alone drives the Kondo breakdown. The key result is that a single complex parameter governs the transition, capturing the essential physics of more complex dissipative models. This work provides a simpler, unified framework for understanding Kondo breakdown in non-Hermitian systems.|
|**2026-06-29**|**Optimal Work Extraction from Finite-Time Closed Quantum Dynamics**|Shoki Sugimoto, Takahiro Sagawa, Ryusuke Hamazaki|[[arxiv:2508.20512](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.20512)]|This paper investigates the problem of optimally extracting work from closed quantum systems within a finite time, subject to a constraint on control Hamiltonian magnitude. The authors derive a fundamental trade-off between power and work output, demonstrating they cannot be maximized simultaneously. They identify a solvable class of problems where the control Hamiltonian is optimized over a Lie algebra preserved by the system's dynamics, reducing the problem to a time-independent nonlinear self-consistent equation. This approach provides a tractable method for many-body systems like the Heisenberg and Hubbard models, showing that maximum power necessitates rapid protocols.|

## High Energy Theory

|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-07-14**|**Multi-rotating black holes with non-aligned angular momenta in 5D Kaluza-Klein theory**|Shinya Tomizawa, Hideo Furugori, Jun-ichi Sakamoto, Ryotaku Suzuki|[[arxiv:2607.11609](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11609)]|This paper presents an exact solution for multi-centered, rotating black holes in four-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton theory, derived from a five-dimensional Kaluza-Klein framework. The key advancement is generalizing prior work to allow each black hole to have a non-aligned spin axis. The resulting spacetimes avoid curvature singularities and closed timelike curves as long as each black hole's spin stays below a specific threshold, encompassing several well-known solutions as special cases. This work significantly broadens the theoretical understanding of multi-black hole systems with independent rotations, which is relevant for testing cosmic censorship and black hole dynamics.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Norms, overlaps and Yangian descendants for the Haldane-Shastry spin chain**|Yunfeng Jiang, Jules Lamers, Yuan Miao|[[arxiv:2606.20168](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20168)]|The Haldane-Shastry spin chain is a key integrable model with long-range interactions and Yangian symmetry, but a systematic construction of its descendant states was lacking. This paper develops a detailed method to construct these descendants using the algebraic Bethe ansatz, connecting them to the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis under extreme twist. The authors derive product and determinant formulas for the norms and overlaps of these states. These results enable the computation of various physical quantities and deepen the understanding of the model's symmetry structure.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Teleparallel $F(T)$ electromagnetic static spherically symmetric spacetime solutions**|Alexandre Landry|[[arxiv:2605.25752](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25752)]|This paper addresses the problem of finding static, spherically symmetric solutions in teleparallel \(F(T)\) gravity, which is a modified theory of gravity using torsion instead of curvature, now coupled with electromagnetic fields. The authors develop a general reconstruction method to systematically build \(F(T)\) models for any chosen spacetime ansatz, deriving the field equations and conservation laws from a covariant formalism. Key results include several new exact solutions, such as black-hole-like and wormhole-like branches, which generalize the Reissner-Nordström spacetime and exhibit modified horizon structures and torsion singularities. The significance lies in offering a unified, covariant framework for constructing compact object and strong-field solutions, enabling tests of gravity beyond General Relativity.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Higher-Order Corrections to Scrambling Dynamics in Brownian Spin SYK Models**|Tingfei Li, Miao Wang, Jianghui Yu|[[arxiv:2602.07952](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07952)]|This paper addresses the challenge of analyzing operator growth in Brownian spin SYK models, extending beyond the standard focus on the scrambling time. The authors develop a method using a master equation and generating functions to compute the full operator-size distribution, which is diagonalizable at leading order in the large-\(N\) limit. Key results include exact solutions for arbitrary initial operators, identification of crucial higher-order \(1/N\) corrections to late-time dynamics, and a demonstration that the full distribution offers deeper insights into quantum chaos than scrambling time alone. The significance lies in providing a more complete and systematic framework for studying quantum chaos and decoherence effects in high-dimensional, strongly interacting systems.|

This work provides a unified framework for constructing compact-object solutions in nonlinear teleparallel gravity, with implications for testing gravity beyond General Relativity in strong-field regimes.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-07-14**|**Thermal phase transitions in a mixed-spin Ising model on the Lieb lattice: Exact results beyond zero magnetic field**|Jozef Strecka, Katarina Karlova|[[arxiv:2607.11661](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11661)]|This paper studies a mixed-spin Ising model on the Lieb lattice with an applied magnetic field and single-ion anisotropy. The method uses a decoration-iteration transformation to map the model onto a simpler exactly solvable effective spin-1/2 Ising model in a regime where the effective field vanishes. Key results reveal three ground-state phases (ferrimagnetic, disordered, ferromagnetic) and a dome-shaped boundary of discontinuous transitions ending in a line of Ising critical points, along with thermally-driven reentrant phase transitions. The significance lies in obtaining exact solutions for finite-field thermal phase transitions, with results verified by Monte Carlo simulations.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Stripe-Ordered Altermagnetism Emerging from Correlation-Driven Spin-Density-Wave Instability**|Zenghui Fan, Jingyao Meng, Tianxing Ma|[[arxiv:2607.11532](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11532)]|This paper investigates a new form of altermagnetism arising from a spin-density wave (SDW) instability rather than conventional antiferromagnetism. Using Hartree-Fock and determinant quantum Monte Carlo methods on a Hubbard model for iron pnictides, the authors discover a correlation-driven stripe-ordered altermagnetic (SOAM) insulating phase. A key result is that applying a uniaxial staggered potential breaks specific symmetries, enabling pronounced nonrelativistic spin splitting, with simulations confirming the phase's stability at finite temperatures. This work expands the concept of altermagnetism beyond its traditional antiferromagnetic context into strongly correlated electron systems.|
|**2026-07-14**|**SlaKoNet-VQD: A universal Slater-Koster tight-binding Hamiltonian for variational quantum band-structure calculations on near-term hardware**|Akshaya Ajith, Jaehyung Lee, Charles Rhys Campbell, Kamal Choudhary|[[arxiv:2607.09761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09761)]|This paper introduces SlaKoNet-VQD, which combines a deep-learning-based Slater-Koster tight-binding model with a variational quantum deflation algorithm to enable band-structure calculations on near-term quantum hardware. The method replaces the costly Hamiltonian construction typically required for quantum simulations with a universal neural network generator trained on data from 65 elements. The approach achieves high accuracy, recovering silicon’s band structure with a mean deviation of 1.78 meV on a simulator and executing ground-state calculations on IBM quantum hardware for aluminum. This work provides a scalable, high-throughput framework for benchmarking variational quantum algorithms and optimizing materials discovery across the periodic table.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Norms, overlaps and Yangian descendants for the Haldane-Shastry spin chain**|Yunfeng Jiang, Jules Lamers, Yuan Miao|[[arxiv:2606.20168](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20168)]|The paper addresses the incomplete treatment of descendant states in the Haldane-Shastry spin chain, which are essential for computing physical quantities. The authors construct these descendants systematically using the algebraic Bethe ansatz, building on recent work by Ferrando et al. Key results include product and determinant formulas for the norms and overlaps of these states, which in the extreme twist limit connect to the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis. This work advances the understanding of Yangian symmetry in an integrable model, enabling more comprehensive calculations for long-range spin systems.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Study of the triangular-lattice Hubbard model with constrained-path quantum Monte Carlo**|Shu Fay Ung, Ankit Mahajan, David R. Reichman|[[arxiv:2603.14808](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14808)]|The paper benchmarks the constrained-path Monte Carlo (CPMC) method on the triangular-lattice Hubbard model. It finds that using symmetry-adapted trial wave functions reduces energy errors to less than 1% away from half-filling, but at half-filling, simple trials introduce significant bias due to strong frustration. To achieve similar accuracy in that regime, symmetry-projected trials are required. The key result is that CPMC with symmetry projection offers a computationally practical, polynomial-scaling method for studying strongly correlated and frustrated systems.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Higher-Order Corrections to Scrambling Dynamics in Brownian Spin SYK Models**|Tingfei Li, Miao Wang, Jianghui Yu|[[arxiv:2602.07952](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07952)]|This paper develops a new method for analyzing operator growth in a Brownian spin SYK model with arbitrary higher-order interactions. The authors derive a master equation and a generating-function formulation to exactly solve the leading-order dynamics of the full operator-size distribution. They then extend this with a systematic \(1/N\) expansion to compute higher-order corrections, revealing their significant impact on scrambling and late-time behavior. The work shows that the full operator-size distribution is a more detailed probe of quantum chaos than simpler measures, particularly for open systems.|
|**2026-07-14**|**Emergent $\PT$ Symmetry and Exceptional Points in a Driven Dirac Impurity**|Vinayak M. Kulkarni|[[arxiv:2505.17811](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17811)]|This paper investigates nonequilibrium steady states in a periodically driven Dirac impurity. Using slave-boson mean-field theory, the authors show that coarse-graining the driven system produces a PT-symmetric kernel with spin-selective gain and loss. They identify exceptional points emerging in the impurity sector, which generate strong nonnormality and reorganize low-energy spectral features, distinguishing between impurity-localized and bath-induced exceptional points. The study reveals that impurity-localized exceptional points can enhance the estimated Kondo screening scale, offering insights into controlling correlated quantum systems through periodic driving.|

The method uses a non-monotonic parametrization of the spatial coordinate in exact solutions, rather than traditional approaches like variable separation.

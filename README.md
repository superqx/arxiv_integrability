|**2026-07-08**|**Unitary matrix models, quantized symmetric functions and spin chain**|Gao-fu Ren|[[arxiv:2607.06384](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06384)]|This paper presents a new correspondence linking unitary matrix models to correlation functions in quantum spin chains. The method involves quantizing symmetric functions into operators that act on the magnon sector of the spin chain, preserving their algebraic structure. A key result shows that the Schur orthogonality in the matrix integral translates directly into the inner product of quantized Schur states, which is demonstrated for several well-known models. This work provides a unified algebraic framework connecting matrix integrals, quantum integrability, and symmetric function theory.|
|**2026-07-08**|**Localized zero modes enhance massive Casimir interactions**|Jose M. Mu\~noz-Casta\~neda, In\'es Cavero-Pel\'aez, Gonzalo Sancho-Garrido|[[arxiv:2607.05530](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05530)]|The paper addresses the problem of exponentially screened Casimir interactions in massive quantum fields. The authors propose using an isolated normalizable zero mode in a classical background to create a low-frequency collective channel that avoids this screening. Key results show that this interaction is universal, determined solely by the zero-mode profile, with kinks and planar domain walls as robust examples, including the sine-Gordon kink. This work establishes a generic infrared mechanism for significantly modifying massive vacuum forces through localized zero modes.|
|**2026-07-08**|**New Rotating Black Hole in Electromagnetic Fields: Cosmological Horizon without Cosmological Constant**|Liang Ma, H. Lu|[[arxiv:2606.23782](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23782)]|This paper presents a new exact solution in Einstein-Maxwell gravity for a rotating black hole that possesses a cosmological horizon without requiring a positive cosmological constant or dark energy. The method involves constructing a background spacetime from the Kundt class and embedding a Kerr black hole within it, followed by an analysis of the global structure and thermodynamics. Key results include the derivation of the first law of black hole thermodynamics and the demonstration that the cosmological horizon is sustained solely by the electromagnetic field. This finding is significant as it offers a novel framework for studying black hole physics and late-time cosmological phenomena without invoking dark energy.|
|**2026-07-08**|**Constrained integrability and anyonic chains**|Matthew Blakeney, Luke Corcoran, Marius de Leeuw|[[arxiv:2605.28946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28946)]|This paper addresses the problem of identifying integrable spin chains with constrained Hilbert spaces, specifically anyonic chains governed by fusion rules. The authors use Yang-Baxter integrability and a modified boost operator formalism to search for integrable points. They extend known integrability results to several fusion categories up to rank 7, discovering new integrable anyonic chains, including spin-3/2 models, Tambara-Yamagami models, and product categories like Fib×Ising. The significance lies in expanding the landscape of integrable models in constrained systems, offering new examples and numerical insights into models based on the Haagerup-Izumi fusion category.|

|**2026-07-08**|**Asymptotic analysis of N-elliptic localized solutions for the Fokas--Lenells equation**|Wang Tang, Guo-Fu Yu|[[arxiv:2607.06409](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06409)]|This paper studies N-elliptic localized solutions of the Fokas–Lenells equation, using Weierstrass elliptic functions and the N-fold Darboux-Backlund transformation for their construction. The key method involves asymptotic analysis via a Cauchy determinant expressed with sigma functions to examine solution behavior along and between propagation directions. Results reveal the asymptotic patterns and symmetry properties of these solutions. The work advances understanding of nonlinear wave structures in integrable systems with elliptic backgrounds.|
|**2026-07-08**|**Attractor Flow Versus Hesse Flow in Wall-Crossing Structures**|Qiang Wang|[[arxiv:2607.05433](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05433)]|This paper reinterprets a previous physics study on BPS states within the mathematical framework of wall-crossing structures. The authors compare two types of flows on the base of a complex integrable system: the Hesse flow and the attractor flow, demonstrating that both are compatible with the wall-crossing formalism. They then introduce the concepts of dual Hesse flow and dual attractor flow, showing that a rotation of the underlying affine structure can transform the Hesse flow into the dual attractor flow and vice versa. This duality suggests a potential application of these flows in the context of Mirror Symmetry.|
|**2026-07-08**|**Constrained integrability and anyonic chains**|Matthew Blakeney, Luke Corcoran, Marius de Leeuw|[[arxiv:2605.28946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28946)]|This paper reviews and extends the study of Yang-Baxter integrable spin chains that operate within constrained Hilbert spaces, specifically focusing on anyonic chains where constraints come from fusion rules. The authors present a new result classifying which anyonic chains admit a Temperley-Lieb algebra and employ a modified boost operator formalism to discover several new integrable models. Key findings include integrable anyonic chains for $\mathfrak{su}(2)_k$ spin-3/2 models, Tambara-Yamagami categories, and product categories like Fib$\times$ Ising, alongside numerical results for a Haagerup-Izumi model. The work significantly broadens the known landscape of integrable anyonic systems, providing a systematic method for constructing their Hamiltonians and analyzing their properties.|

|**2026-07-08**|**Weak-coupling altermagnetism and chiral magnetic excitations in a checkerboard lattice**|Abhigya Rangari, Manna Paul, Sayandip Ghosh|[[arxiv:2607.06106](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06106)]|This paper investigates altermagnetism, a new class of magnetism with spin-split electronic bands but compensated magnetic moments, using a minimal one-band Hubbard model on a checkerboard lattice. The study identifies that the checkerboard lattice naturally hosts altermagnetism, demonstrated by diverging altermagnetic susceptibility at weak coupling, and maps phase transitions from a nonmagnetic semimetal to an altermagnetic semimetal and then insulator. Key results show well-defined magnon excitations that decay into single-particle states, with the magnetic excitations exhibiting a strong, alternating chirality splitting dependent on direction. This discovery provides a simple electronic model for altermagnetism and highlights chiral, directional magnetic excitations as a distinctive signature of this class of materials.|

The authors extend the boost operator formalism to discover new integrable anyonic chains across various fusion categories, including spin-3/2 models and Tambara-Yamagami categories. They find several new integrable chains and provide numerical results for a Haagerup-Izumi model. |**2026-07-08**|**Superconducting order parameter in aperiodic binary systems**|Sougata Biswas, Debika Debnath, Paramita Dutta|[[arxiv:2507.14671](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.14671)]|This paper theoretically investigates the superconducting order parameter (OP) in aperiodic binary systems using the attractive Hubbard model, extending the analysis from one-dimensional toy models to two-dimensional systems. The key results show that the qualitative features of the OP are largely preserved in higher dimensions, and the study identifies specific aperiodic configurations and parameter regimes that enhance onsite pairing amplitudes. By analyzing the evolution of the OP with temperature, interaction strength, and hopping amplitude, the research provides a comparative analysis of superconducting transition temperatures across different aperiodic structures. The work is significant because it helps determine which aperiodic systems are most conducive to Cooper pair formation, advancing the understanding of superconductivity in non-periodic materials.|
|**2026-07-08**|**Electrons interacting with Goldstone modes and the rotating frame**|Konstantinos Vasiliou, Yuchi He, Nick Bultinck|[[arxiv:2307.05699](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.05699)]|This paper examines how electrons interact with Goldstone modes in systems with spontaneously broken continuous symmetry. Using the random phase approximation, the authors calculate the electron-Goldstone mode scattering vertex across the entire Brillouin zone. They find that electrons couple to both the Goldstone field and its time derivative, and that quasiparticles must be defined in a rotating frame that follows order parameter fluctuations. The results impact electron spectral functions in symmetry-broken and thermally disordered systems, as demonstrated for antiferromagnetism in the Hubbard model and spin spiral order in the three-band model.|
|**2026-07-08**|**The topological Kondo model out of equilibrium**|Matteo M. Using a matrix-product-state method for superconductor dynamics, the authors simulate the relaxation of Majorana magnetization to determine the Kondo temperature and analyze electric transport after a voltage quench. Key results show a crossover from weak-coupling states to a strongly correlated topological Kondo regime at charge degeneracy points, exhibiting universal fractional zero-bias conductance. The findings provide nonperturbative evidence for the topological Kondo effect in double nanowire devices, highlighting its distinct signatures out of equilibrium.|

The method involves constructing a fundamental solution matrix using Weierstrass elliptic functions and deriving the localized solutions via an N-fold Darboux–Backlund transformation. |**2026-07-08**|**Wave Kinetics and Thermalization in Kadomtsev-Petviashvili-I System**|Kiran Venkata Kolluru, Giorgio Krstulovic, Alan C Newell, Sergey Nazarenko|[[arxiv:2607.06119](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06119)]|This paper investigates wave kinetics in the Kadomtsev-Petviashvili-I system, an integrable model that nonetheless permits resonant wave interactions. Using numerical simulations, the authors show that these interactions preserve compact spectral supports and allow the system to thermalize into Rayleigh-Jeans equilibrium states, challenging the common assumption that integrable systems cannot thermalize. The thermalization proceeds through non-local spectral transfers, creating strong low-wavenumber peaks in a process analogous to Bose-Einstein condensation. The findings reveal novel thermalization dynamics in integrable systems and deepen the understanding of weak wave turbulence.|
|**2026-07-08**|**The Euler Ensemble as a Turbulent Attractor: Parity Sectors, Zero Modes, and a Zeta Edge**|Alexander Migdal|[[arxiv:2607.05745](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05745)]|The paper studies the Lyapunov spectrum of finite Euler ensembles, which are compact arithmetic fixed points of the momentum-loop equation for decaying Navier-Stokes turbulence. The method reduces the stability problem to an arithmetic spectral problem based on parity sectors and winding numbers, finding that the zero-winding sector in even ensembles forms a singular discrete zero mode with positive Lyapunov exponents and instability. In contrast, odd and punctured even ensembles are marginal fixed-mode limits where the normalized eigenvalue law converges weakly to zero, with finite eigenvalues surviving only as a vanishing arithmetic edge involving coprime sums and zeta functions. This work reveals a deep connection between turbulence attractors and number theory, showing that stability depends sensitively on parity and winding, and that transverse perturbations are zero modes with no quadratic spectral shift in the marginal sectors.|
|**2026-07-08**|**Constrained integrability and anyonic chains**|Matthew Blakeney, Luke Corcoran, Marius de Leeuw|[[arxiv:2605.28946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28946)]|This paper addresses the problem of identifying integrable spin chains whose Hilbert spaces are subject to constraints, like those arising from anyonic fusion rules. The authors review Temperley-Lieb algebra emergence in anyonic chains and extend integrability results to several fusion categories up to rank 7. Using a modified boost operator formalism, they discover new integrable chains, including spin-3/2 models for $\mathfrak{su}(2)_k$ and models based on Tambara-Yamagami and product fusion categories. This work broadens the known landscape of integrable anyonic models, with implications for constrained quantum systems like Rydberg arrays.|

The method involves studying small, bounded deformations of the complex structure in twistor space and analyzing the space of holomorphic planes that go through the origin. The key result shows this space is an infinite-dimensional complex manifold projecting onto a four-dimensional self-dual spacetime, with solutions obtained by embedding spacetime into the higher-spin space. This work is significant because it provides a geometric framework that makes the integrability of the theory explicit through a Lax pair, while also connecting to chiral higher-spin gravity.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Holographic Dual of PT Symmetric BCFT**|Ryota Maeda, Nanami Nakamura, Tadashi Takayanagi|[[arxiv:2606.18629](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18629)]|This paper constructs a holographic dual for a 2D conformal field theory with Parity-Time (PT) symmetric, non-hermitian boundary conditions using the AdS/BCFT correspondence, achieved by introducing an imaginary scalar field on an end-of-the-world brane. The key result is that increasing the strength of the non-hermitian interactions induces a spontaneous PT symmetry breaking in the system. Additionally, by Wick rotating the setup to model a quantum quench, the authors find that the resulting entanglement entropy growth exceeds the standard rate associated with conventional Cardy states. The work provides a new holographic framework for studying non-hermitian physics and demonstrates its potential to generate novel, faster entanglement dynamics.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Probing weak chaos in $\mathcal N=4$ super Yang-Mills and long-range spin chains**|Pawel Caputa, Brian Creed, Rathindra Nath Das, Saskia Demulder, Tristan McLoughlin|[[arxiv:2606.18351](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18351)]|This paper investigates how chaotic behavior emerges in finite-loop truncations of the planar dilatation operator in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory, which models long-range spin chains between integrable limits. Using spectral statistics, eigenvector diagnostics, and spread complexity, the authors find that two- and four-loop truncations exhibit GOE-like level statistics at strong coupling, though with features of weak integrability breaking, while three-loop truncations remain non-chaotic. Key results show the four-loop system requires a larger coupling threshold for chaos and that eigenstates are less random than GOE vectors, indicating weak ergodicity and multifractality. The findings suggest these finite-loop operators are not generic chaotic Hamiltonians but instead display patterns consistent with the restoration of integrability in the all-loop planar theory.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Ultraviolet completion of the inflationary paradigm**|Leonardo Modesto, Lorenzo Orlando|[[arxiv:2603.01722](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01722)]|This paper proposes a new nonlocal quantum completion of general \( f(R) \) theories of gravity, such as the Starobinsky model, to address the incompatibility of renormalizability and stability in previous proposals. The method involves constructing a nonlocal theory that preserves the same background solutions and perturbation equations as the local \( f(R) \) theory, ensuring classical observables remain unchanged. Key results show that the new model is at least super-renormalizable and can be made finite, overcoming prior theoretical issues. The significance lies in demonstrating that the inflationary paradigm is consistent with a well-defined quantum theory of gravity, implying that classical inflationary predictions are robust against ultraviolet corrections.|

The authors map the theory onto a symplectic Lie algebra evolution and generalize the Stroh formalism and Barnett-Lothe invariants from elasticity to quantum field theory. They derive an exact algebraic identity and a master spectral integral equation, yielding a non-perturbative anomalous dimension $\eta \approx 0.0363$ that matches the conformal bootstrap results. The framework shows exact dimensional reduction to known 2D and 4D limits, demonstrating a robust, coupling-independent closure of the theory.|

Combining these constructions provides the first proof that a local Hamiltonian can have a ground state with volume-law entanglement for all contiguous subsystems.|

The key finding is that OTOC relaxation to saturation follows distinct patterns: a slow power-law decay for integrable systems versus a faster, exponential decay for chaotic ones. The study shows that near saturation, long-range spectral statistics better characterize chaos, and that the relaxation regime is sensitive to disorder while the initial scrambling is not. The work provides a precise, ETH-derived expression for OTOC saturation, offering a refined diagnostic for distinguishing quantum chaos from integrability in disordered systems.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Holographic Dual of PT Symmetric BCFT**|Ryota Maeda, Nanami Nakamura, Tadashi Takayanagi|[[arxiv:2606.18629](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18629)]|This paper proposes a holographic dual for two-dimensional conformal field theories with non-Hermitian parity-time (PT) symmetric boundary conditions using the AdS/BCFT correspondence, achieved by introducing an imaginary scalar field on an end-of-the-world brane. The study reveals that increasing the non-Hermitian interaction strength induces spontaneous PT symmetry breaking in the system. In a Wick-rotated quantum quench setup, the model exhibits entanglement entropy growth that surpasses standard predictions from ordinary Cardy states. The results extend holographic methods to non-Hermitian quantum systems and suggest possible accelerated thermalization in PT-symmetric setups.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Probing weak chaos in $\mathcal N=4$ super Yang-Mills and long-range spin chains**|Pawel Caputa, Brian Creed, Rathindra Nath Das, Saskia Demulder, Tristan McLoughlin|[[arxiv:2606.18351](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18351)]|This paper studies the onset of quantum chaos in finite-loop truncations of the planar dilatation operator in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory, which model long-range deformations of an integrable spin chain. Using spectral statistics, eigenvector diagnostics, and spread complexity, the authors find that two- and four-loop truncations exhibit GOE-like level statistics at strong coupling, but with features of weak integrability breaking that is less pronounced at four loops. The three-loop truncation shows no similar chaos onset. The results suggest these finite-loop operators are not generic chaotic long-range Hamiltonians but display patterns consistent with the restoration of integrability toward the all-loop planar limit.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Post-Selection Probability and Fidelity of Bidirectional Teleportation**|Ning Sun, Lei Feng, Pengfei Zhang|[[arxiv:2606.17251](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17251)]|This paper provides a theoretical analysis of a bidirectional teleportation protocol that uses chaotic Hamiltonian evolution to perform a SWAP operation between qubits. The authors characterize the protocol by deriving expressions for its post-selection probability and fidelity, linking these to standard quantum diagnostics like the Loschmidt echo. Key results show that the fidelity depends on the initial state, while the post-selection probability is stable in integrable models. These findings offer practical insights for implementing the protocol on quantum devices.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Infinite temperature at zero energy**|Matteo Ippoliti, David M. The approach modifies the Feynman-Kitaev clock by imposing periodic boundary conditions on the clock register. Key results show that when the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) holds for the underlying circuit, all eigenstates of these Hamiltonians exhibit volume-law entanglement entropy, including the ground state. The significance lies in providing the first construction of local Hamiltonians with provably volume-law-entangled ground states for all contiguous subsystems.|

|**2026-06-18**|**Probing chaos and thermalization through out-of-time-ordered correlators in random field spin chains**|C Jisha, Shivam Mishra, Ravi Prakash|[[arxiv:2606.18982](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18982)]|This paper studies quantum chaos and thermalization using out-of-time-ordered correlators (OTOCs) in a random-field Heisenberg spin chain, which can be tuned from integrable to chaotic dynamics. The authors find that the OTOC's approach to saturation follows a power-law decay in integrable systems, but shifts to a higher-degree power-law followed by an exponential decay in chaotic regimes. They also show that long-range spectral statistics better diagnose chaos near saturation, and that the initial scrambling regime is robust across disorder realizations while relaxation is sensitive to them. The results establish OTOC relaxation as a clear signature of chaos and validate the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis for predicting its long-time behavior.|
|**2026-06-18**|**On the quasi-continuum approximation of some localized patterns in the FPUT lattice**|Su Yang, Wenrong Sun, Lei Liu, Panayotis G. The authors derive a modified Korteweg-de Vries (mKdV) equation from the lattice, which provides analytical solutions for rogue waves, solitons, and breathers on both constant and periodic backgrounds. Numerical simulations using the mKdV-derived initial conditions show strong agreement with the FPUT lattice dynamics. The work demonstrates the mKdV reduction's effectiveness and suggests its broader application for importing quasi-continuum waveforms into discrete systems like mechanical metamaterials.|
|**2026-06-18**|**Heavenly equations in de Sitter space**|Maciej Dunajski, Timothy Moy|[[arxiv:2606.14572](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14572)]|The paper addresses the problem of describing anti-self-dual Einstein metrics with a non-zero cosmological constant. The authors show that such metrics can be locally derived from solutions of a single second-order partial differential equation originally introduced by Lipstein and Nagy. They connect this equation to the hyper-heavenly formalism of Plebański and establish a Lax pair for it. Notably, the study reveals that Plebański's second heavenly equation emerges in the limit as the cosmological constant approaches zero, linking results in curved spacetime to flat-space solutions.|

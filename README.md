|**2026-04-14**|**Parent Hamiltonian Construction of Generalized Calogero-Sutherland Models**|Hari Borutta, Andreas Feuerpfeil, Yasir Iqbal|[[arxiv:2604.11074](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11074)]|This paper addresses the challenge of constructing quantum many-body Hamiltonians for which specific trial wavefunctions, relevant to non-Abelian anyons, are exact ground states. The method involves reverse-engineering positive semi-definite parent Hamiltonians by deriving annihilation operators from the null-vector structure and differential equations of rational conformal field theories. The key result is the explicit application of this general construction to derive continuum Hamiltonians for the Moore-Read and the k=3 Read-Rezayi states, establishing them as exact zero-energy modes. This work is significant because it provides a systematic framework for building models that host exotic topological phases, advancing the study of non-Abelian anyons in quantum physics.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Why the Bethe Ansatz Works: A Structural Explanation via Interaction Propagation**|Joe Gildea|[[arxiv:2604.09844](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09844)]|This paper addresses the puzzle of why the Bethe Ansatz, a method for exact solutions in quantum many-body systems, works only in specific regimes and fails elsewhere. The authors propose a structural explanation centered on the concept of interaction propagation, arguing that solvability depends on whether interactions propagate finitely without hitting structural boundaries. Their key result is a sharp dichotomy: finite propagation leads to exact Bethe-type solutions, while encountering a boundary creates irreducible data that obstructs such solvability. This reframes the Bethe Ansatz from a model-specific construction to a general rigidity phenomenon governed by interaction constraints, offering a unified explanation for its successes and limitations.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Rigorous results for timelike Liouville field theory**|Sourav Chatterjee|[[arxiv:2504.02348](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.02348)]|This paper addresses the challenge of rigorously defining timelike Liouville field theory, a variant of two-dimensional quantum gravity where the kinetic term has a problematic negative sign. The authors develop a novel mathematical framework to handle Gaussian random variables with negative variance, which is required to make sense of this theory. Using this framework, they prove the timelike DOZZ formula for three-point correlation functions under specific conditions and derive expressions for all higher-order correlation functions, showing they converge to the correct semiclassical limits. This work provides the first rigorous mathematical foundation for timelike Liouville theory, a significant step in connecting it with quantum gravity models.|

|**2026-04-14**|**Thermodynamic fluctuations in freely jointed chains under force**|Michael R. Buche, Alvin Chen|[[arxiv:2604.11713](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11713)]|This paper addresses the problem that standard analyses of the freely jointed chain polymer model typically report only ensemble averages, treating them as deterministic values and ignoring their inherent thermodynamic fluctuations. The method involves analytically and numerically calculating the probability densities and standard deviations for various geometric quantities—like chain extensions and angles—under different applied forces and link numbers. A key result is that these fluctuations are significant except at very high forces, and increasing the number of links reduces fluctuations in most measures except the independent link angles. The significance lies in providing crucial quantitative warnings for more accurate modeling of single polymer chains and polymer networks, where ignoring these fluctuations can be misleading.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Exact Criterion for Ground-State Overlap Dominance after Quantum Quenches**|Taisanul Haque|[[arxiv:2604.11420](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11420)]|This paper addresses whether the ground state always has the highest overlap with the initial state after a quantum quench within the same phase. The authors develop an exact analytical criterion for translationally invariant free-fermion models, showing the ground state dominates only if specific conditions on the initial and final Hamiltonians are met. Their key finding disproves a general conjecture by demonstrating that in systems like the Kitaev chain, a same-phase quench can yield a higher overlap with an excited state. This result is significant because it links this overlap structure directly to dynamical quantum phase transitions, showing they can occur without crossing a physical phase boundary.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Absence of thermalization after a local quench and strong violation of the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis**|Peter Reimann, Christian Eidecker-Dunkel|[[arxiv:2604.11392](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11392)]|This paper examines how a localized disturbance in a quantum spin chain can prevent the system from reaching thermal equilibrium, challenging established theories. The authors analytically and numerically study XX spin chains with open boundaries, where a single-spin impurity is suddenly introduced at either the chain's end or center. Their key finding is that this local quench leads to a complete absence of thermalization and a strong violation of the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis, exceeding the weaker violations seen in other integrable systems. This result is significant because it demonstrates that non-thermalizing behavior can be triggered by a minimal, local perturbation, not just a global change, and presents a more extreme breakdown of standard thermalization principles.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Parent Hamiltonian Construction of Generalized Calogero-Sutherland Models**|Hari Borutta, Andreas Feuerpfeil, Yasir Iqbal|[[arxiv:2604.11074](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11074)]|This paper addresses the challenge of constructing many-body quantum Hamiltonians for which specific trial wavefunctions, like those describing non-Abelian anyons, are exact eigenstates. The method leverages conformal field theory, using the null-vector conditions of primary fields to systematically derive annihilation operators and build positive semi-definite parent Hamiltonians. A key result is the explicit construction of such continuum Hamiltonians for the Moore-Read and Read-Rezayi states, proving these states are exact zero-energy modes. This work is significant as it provides a general framework for reverse-engineering physical models from complex topological wavefunctions, advancing the study of non-Abelian anyons and fractional quantum Hall physics.|
|**2026-04-14**|**A Scalable Configuration-Interaction Impurity Solver via Active Learning**|Jeongmoo Lee, Ara Go|[[arxiv:2604.10230](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10230)]|This paper addresses the computational bottleneck in quantum impurity solvers, where the Hilbert space grows prohibitively large when increasing the number of bath orbitals. The authors develop a method called active-learning adaptive-truncation configuration interaction (AL-ATCI), which intelligently selects only the most relevant quantum states for the calculation. Their key result is that this method accurately reproduces benchmark spectra while dramatically reducing computational cost, enabling calculations for systems with up to 18 bath orbitals. This advancement is significant because it alleviates a major limitation in dynamical mean-field theory, making high-accuracy simulations of complex correlated materials more practical.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Critical Temperatures from Domain-Wall Microstate Counting: A Topological Solution for the Potts Universality Class**|David Vaknin|[[arxiv:2602.00962](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00962)]|This paper addresses the challenge of predicting critical temperatures for the Potts model across different lattice geometries and state numbers \( q \). The method introduces a universal formula based on balancing domain-wall energy against configurational entropy, which decomposes the critical condition into lattice-topological and state-space sampling factors. The key results show that this framework exactly reproduces known solutions for standard 2D lattices and achieves high accuracy (within 3%) for various 3D lattices. Its significance lies in unifying the Potts universality class under a single geometric classification, offering a predictive tool that links phase transitions to the topological saturation of interfaces in the lattice.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Dynamics of superconducting pairs in the two-dimensional Hubbard model**|G. Sordi, E. M. O'Callaghan, C. Walsh, M. Charlebois, P. S\'emon, A. -M. S. Tremblay|[[arxiv:2512.01124](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.01124)]|This paper investigates the superconducting pairing mechanism in the two-dimensional Hubbard model, a key framework for understanding cuprate superconductors. Using cellular dynamical mean-field theory, the authors systematically analyze how the superconducting gap and the frequency scales of pairing depend on doping and interaction strength. The key finding is that pair-forming processes are confined to low frequencies set by the superexchange interaction, while the high-energy Coulomb repulsion does not directly participate in pairing. This result clarifies the frequency structure of pairing in the model, suggesting that superconductivity emerges from low-energy spin interactions rather than high-energy electronic correlations.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Bad metal behavior and Lifshitz transition of a Nagaoka ferromagnet**|Jonas Arnold, Peter Kopietz, Andreas R\"uckriegel|[[arxiv:2510.01909](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01909)]|This paper investigates the strongly correlated Hubbard model at infinite repulsion, where traditional metallic behavior breaks down. The method employs an advanced fermionic functional renormalization group technique adapted for constrained Hilbert spaces. The key results show the ground state evolves from a paramagnet to a Nagaoka ferromagnet with doping, and within this ferromagnetic phase, the electronic spectrum is incoherent and marked by a Lifshitz transition separating two distinct regimes. Its significance lies in providing a microscopic mechanism for "bad metal" behavior and linking it to a topological Fermi surface change within a ferromagnetic state.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Electrostatics in semiconducting devices II : Solving the Helmholtz equation**|Antonio Lacerda-Santos, Xavier Waintal|[[arxiv:2507.03131](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.03131)]|This paper addresses the challenge of achieving convergence in self-consistent quantum-electrostatic calculations, which is especially problematic in regimes with strong non-linearities like partial electron depletion. The core method involves mapping the original Schrödinger-Poisson problem onto a Non-Linear Helmholtz (NLH) equation, a generalized Thomas-Fermi approximation, which allows for the construction of provably convergent iterative schemes by minimizing a convex functional. The key result is that this approach, after lifting the approximation to recover the exact solution, achieves convergence empirically in just one or two iterations. This work is significant as it provides a robust, fast, and precise computational framework for modeling electrostatics in quantum nanoelectronic devices.|
|**2026-04-14**|**Spectral statistics, non-equilibrium dynamics and thermalization in random matrices with global $\mathbb{Z}_2$ -symmetry**|Adway Kumar Das|[[arxiv:2506.16176](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.16176)]|This paper investigates how a global ℤ₂ symmetry affects thermalization in quantum systems by using random symmetric centrosymmetric matrices as a model. The method involves analytically computing the time evolution of different initial states and comparing equilibrium values of local observables against standard statistical ensembles. A key result is that certain low-energy states do not decay, leading to spontaneous symmetry breaking in a measure-zero set of matrices, and that an observable's equilibrium value depends on whether it respects the Hamiltonian's symmetry. The significance is that, despite the symmetry constraints, eigenstate thermalization holds and systems still thermalize to the canonical ensemble average.|

A key result is a rigorous proof that functional Kaleidocycles exist for any chain length greater than five tetrahedra. The significance lies in providing a concrete, solvable connection between a real-world geometric constraint system and the theory of integrable systems, yielding explicit formulas for the mechanism's motion.|

## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|

|**2026-04-30**|**Dissipative Losses In Black Hole-Induced Vacuum Decay**|Michael Geller, Ofri Telem|[[arxiv:2604.26009](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26009)]|This paper addresses the theoretical puzzle of whether black holes can trigger false vacuum decay without exponential suppression. The authors study the problem using \(\phi^4\) and sine-Gordon models in the thin-wall regime, incorporating radiative losses. Their key result is that these dissipative effects decelerate highly-boosted true vacuum bubbles, preventing runaway decay and ensuring the production rate remains exponentially suppressed. The work resolves a long-standing inconsistency, showing that black hole-induced vacuum decay is never unsuppressed, though it can be enhanced in certain parameter spaces.|
|**2026-04-30**|**Symmetric polynomials: DIM integrable systems versus twisted Cherednik systems**|A. Mironov, A. Morozov, A. Popolitov|[[arxiv:2601.19878](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19878)]|This paper explores the relationship between two quantum integrable systems: one derived from the Ding-Iohara-Miki (DIM) algebra and the twisted Cherednik system. For specific parameters, eigenfunctions of the DIM Hamiltonians are twisted Baker-Akhiezer functions (BAFs), while those of the twisted Cherednik Hamiltonians are twisted non-symmetric Macdonald polynomials. The authors show that the symmetric ground states of both systems coincide and that excited states from both frameworks can generate identical symmetric functions. These resulting functions serve as eigenfunctions for both sets of Hamiltonians, establishing a concrete correspondence between the DIM algebra and the spherical double affine Hecke algebra (DAHA).|
|**2026-04-30**|**Long-time behaviour of sphalerons in $\phi^4$ models with a false vacuum**|Stephen C. Anco, Danial Saadatmand|[[arxiv:2510.04283](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04283)]|The paper investigates the long-term evolution of sphalerons in $\phi^4$ models with a false vacuum. Using numerical simulations, the authors show that the sphaleron decays into an accelerating kink-antikink pair, with their separation approaching the speed of light at large times. A nonlinear collective coordinate method provides an approximate analytical description of this behavior. The findings suggest that the exact solution should exhibit a gradient blow-up at large times due to energy concentration at the kink-antikink flanks.|

|**2026-04-30**|**On matrix Lax representations for (1+1)-dimensional evolutionary differential-difference equations**|Sergei Igonin|[[arxiv:2604.26870](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26870)]|This paper develops new methods for analyzing matrix Lax representations (MLRs) of integrable differential-difference equations. The authors address three core problems: simplifying MLRs via gauge transformations, constructing Miura-type transformations from MLRs, and detecting "fake" MLRs that are gauge-equivalent to trivial ones. Their general theory applies to (1+1)-dimensional evolutionary equations with arbitrary shifts and non-autonomous dependencies. The significance is demonstrated by deriving new two-component integrable equations and connecting them via novel transformations to known systems, including non-autonomous examples.|
|**2026-04-30**|**The Fock-Darwin-Darboux system: eigenstates, information entropies and dispersion-like measures**|Ignacio Baena-Jimenez, Angel Ballesteros, Ivan Gutierrez-Sagredo|[[arxiv:2604.26466](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26466)]|The paper addresses the problem of characterizing entropy and dispersion measures in the Fock-Darwin (FD) system and its generalization on a negatively curved space, the Fock-Darwin-Darboux (FDD) system. Using the exact solvability of these systems, the authors derive analytical expressions for information-theoretic entropies like Shannon, Rényi, and Tsallis, finding that FD measures mirror those of a harmonic oscillator with an effective magnetic-dependent frequency. For the FDD system, curved space prevents closed-form momentum-space wavefunctions, requiring numerical analysis of entropy behavior under varying curvature and magnetic field. A key result shows that the Landau system on the Darboux III space lacks infinitely degenerate Landau levels, highlighting the significant impact of negative curvature on quantum properties.|
|**2026-04-30**|**The dynamical algebra of the generic superintegrable model on the two-sphere**|Nicolas Cramp\'e, Quentin Labriet, Lucia Morey, Satoshi Tsujimoto, Luc Vinet, Alexei Zhedanov|[[arxiv:2604.26122](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26122)]|The paper identifies the rank two Jacobi algebra \(\mathfrak{J}_2\) as the dynamical algebra for the generic quadratic superintegrable model on the two-sphere. The method involves embedding this algebra within \(\mathfrak{su}(1,1)^{\otimes 3}\) to obtain its physical representation. Key results include the algebraic derivation of the model's exact solution, with wavefunctions expressed as two-variable Jacobi polynomials. This work provides a novel algebraic characterization of these polynomials, advancing the understanding of superintegrable systems and their symmetries.|
|**2026-04-30**|**Symmetric polynomials: DIM integrable systems versus twisted Cherednik systems**|A. Mironov, A. Morozov, A. Popolitov|[[arxiv:2601.19878](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19878)]|This paper explores the connection between two quantum integrable systems: one arising from the Ding-Iohara-Miki (DIM) algebra and the other from the twisted Cherednik system. The authors show that for a specific parameter value, the ground state of the twisted Cherednik system is a particular symmetric Baker-Akhiezer function (BAF). They extend this relationship to excited states, proving that both twisted Cherednik eigenfunctions and BAFs can be combined to produce symmetric functions that are simultaneous eigenfunctions for the Hamiltonians of both systems. This result explicitly demonstrates the deep correspondence between the DIM algebra and the spherical double affine Hecke algebra (DAHA).|
|**2026-04-30**|**Long-time behaviour of sphalerons in $\phi^4$ models with a false vacuum**|Stephen C. Anco, Danial Saadatmand|[[arxiv:2510.04283](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04283)]|The paper investigates the long-term evolution of sphalerons, unstable lump-like solutions connecting true and false vacua in nonlinear Klein-Gordon models. Using numerical simulations, the authors show that the sphaleron transforms into an accelerating kink-antikink pair, with their separation approaching the speed of light at large times. An approximate analytical solution is derived through a nonlinear collective coordinate method to describe this behavior. The findings suggest that the exact solution will develop a gradient blow-up over time due to energy concentration at the pair's flanks, highlighting instability dynamics in vacuum transitions.|
|**2026-04-30**|**Towards an ab initio derivation of generalised hydrodynamics from a gas of interacting wave packets**|Benjamin Doyon, Friedrich H\"ubner|[[arxiv:2307.09307](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.09307)]|This paper aims to derive generalised hydrodynamics from first principles in quantum integrable models, using the repulsive Lieb-Liniger model as an example. The method identifies quasi-particles as classical wave packets formed from Bethe wave functions, which evolve and exhibit two-particle scattering shifts similar to solitons. A key result is a formula for the action of the spectral phase-space density operator on Bethe wave functions, demonstrating its role in generating local conserved densities. The significance lies in providing a foundational step toward rigorously deriving the equations of generalized hydrodynamics for long-wavelength states.|

Using numerical linked-cluster expansion and determinant quantum Monte Carlo methods, the authors examine ferromagnetic correlations and thermodynamic properties. They find that repulsive interactions boost ferromagnetic correlations at high densities, and increasing interaction strength extends these correlations toward half filling, linking to Nagaoka ferromagnetism. The results provide a clearer understanding of magnetic behavior away from half filling and suggest implications for ultracold atom experiments.|

Using an analytic approach, the authors demonstrate that the fully spin-polarized state becomes unstable beyond a critical $t'/t$ ratio, transitioning to a metastable spin-polaron. Large-scale density matrix renormalization group simulations reveal a quantum phase transition to a spiral spin-density wave, which evolves into an antiferromagnet under high frustration and persists at low but finite hole density. The significance lies in its theoretical and experimental accessibility, predicting robust phenomena relevant to cold-atom and moiré Hubbard systems.|

This work provides a foundational step toward rigorously linking microscopic quantum dynamics to the macroscopic GHD equations for long-wavelength states.|

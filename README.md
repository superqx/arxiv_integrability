## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-14**|**Taxonomy of Instanton Corrections in Infinite Distance Limits**|Manuel Artime, Ralph Blumenhagen, Panagiotis Leivadaros|[[arxiv:2605.03005](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03005)]|This paper addresses the problem of identifying which instanton corrections arise in infinite distance limits of moduli space. The authors use the BPS-protected R⁴-term and a one-loop Schwinger integral over light towers of states as their method. A key result is that the Schwinger integral precisely captures instantons whose actions lie within a specific parametric window bounded by the ratio of the gravity cutoff to the lightest tower mass. The significance is that this work provides a taxonomy for determining the emergent instanton spectrum in any infinite distance limit, supported by examples in eight and seven dimensions.|
|**2026-05-14**|**A particle on a ring or: how I learned to stop worrying and love $\theta$ -vacua**|Mohammad Aghaie, Ryosuke Sato|[[arxiv:2601.18248](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18248)]|This paper examines a proposal by Ai, Cruz, Garbrecht, and Tamarit (ACGT) that suggests avoiding the strong CP problem by taking the spacetime volume to infinity before summing topological sectors in the Euclidean path integral. The authors test this proposal using exactly solvable one-dimensional quantum systems on a ring, such as the quantum rotor and pendulum, which have well-known \(\theta\)-dependent spectra. They find that the ACGT procedure fails to reproduce the correct energy spectrum, a direct physical observable. This demonstrates that the proposed order of limits is unjustified and cannot alone support conclusions about CP conservation in QCD.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Generation of gravitating solutions with Baryonic charge from Einstein-Scalar-Maxwell seeds**|Fabrizio Canfora, Anibal Neira, Seung Hun Oh|[[arxiv:2601.17864](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17864)]|This paper establishes an exact correspondence between Einstein-scalar-Maxwell theory and gauged Skyrme-Maxwell-Einstein models in (3+1) dimensions. By constructing a consistent ansatz, the authors show that solution-generating techniques from simpler electrovacuum systems can be transferred to the complex Skyrme-Maxwell framework, yielding configurations with nonvanishing baryonic charge. A key result is demonstrated using a rotating Kerr-Newman-like spacetime, where the baryonic charge quantization leads to a quantization of the Kerr rotation parameter, with an upper bound derived for the charge. This work enables systematic exploration of exact solutions in Skyrme-Maxwell-Einstein theory, with potential applications in cosmology and astrophysics.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-14**|**Nagaoka supermetal in the particle-doped triangular Hubbard model**|Rui Cao, Xiangyue Zhang, Hui Tan, Jian-Shu Xu, Yuan-Yao He, Jianmin Yuan, Yongqiang Li|[[arxiv:2605.13837](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13837)]|This paper addresses the poorly understood metallic state that emerges when particles are added to a doped Mott insulator on a geometrically frustrated lattice. The authors study the triangular-lattice Hubbard model with particle doping, identifying an interaction-driven quantum phase they call the "Nagaoka supermetal." This state exhibits anomalous properties, including a sublinear temperature dependence in DC resistivity and singular charge compressibility, which are explained by a higher-order Van Hove singularity in the electronic band structure. The results provide new insight into non-Fermi liquid behavior in frustrated systems and are testable with current ultracold atom experiments.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Quantifying information flow along a stochastic trajectory**|Yongjae Oh, Euijoon Kwon, Yongjoo Baek|[[arxiv:2605.13509](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13509)]|This paper addresses the challenge of applying Stochastic Information Flow (SIF) empirically due to computational difficulties. The authors propose a scalable deep-learning method to estimate SIF from general time-series data. They validate their approach on an exactly solvable model, Kuramoto oscillators, and real trajectories of motile cells, demonstrating that SIF effectively identifies cooperative structures. This work provides a practical, data-driven tool for quantifying information flow in complex systems.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Multiband Superconductivity in the Exactly Solvable Hatsugai-Kohmoto Model**|Nico Hahn, R. Matthias Geilhufe|[[arxiv:2605.13259](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13259)]|This paper studies multiband superconductivity using an exactly solvable extension of the Hatsugai-Kohmoto model, which features momentum-local interactions. The authors classify all symmetry-allowed superconducting gap structures for a two-orbital system with D₄h point-group symmetry. Using a mean-field approach, they compute critical temperatures and order parameters as functions of interaction and pairing strength for selected channels. The work provides a systematic framework for analyzing correlated superconductivity in multiband systems and extends symmetry-based classification methods to strongly correlated settings.|
|**2026-05-14**|**The critical slowing down in diffusion models**|Luca Maria Del Bono, Giulio Biroli, Patrick Charbonneau, Marylou Gabri\'e|[[arxiv:2605.12597](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12597)]|This paper addresses the problem of critical slowing down in diffusion models, where sampling near criticality becomes inefficient. The authors analyze the training of a one-layer score model on the analytically solvable $O(n)$ model and find that parameter learning exhibits a critical slowing down that scales quadratically with system size. They show that using a two-layer architecture with physical locality overcomes this bottleneck, reducing training time to logarithmic scaling with system size while not increasing the number of parameters. The results demonstrate that appropriate architectural design can enable diffusion models to overcome critical slowing down, providing a controlled theoretical framework for improving generative sampling in statistical physics.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Exact solution of a two-dimensional (2D) Ising model with the next nearest interactions**|Zhidong Zhang|[[arxiv:2601.10749](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10749)]|The paper addresses the exact solution of the two-dimensional (2D) Ising model with next nearest neighbor interactions at zero magnetic field, a long-unsolved problem with topological challenges. The author analyzes transfer matrices using Clifford algebraic, tensor, and schematic representations to reveal nontrivial topological structures, then adapts methods from the 3D Ising model to derive the partition function and spontaneous magnetization. Key results show that increasing intra-cell interactions or adding topological contributions raises the critical point, validated by comparison with other Ising lattices. This work is significant for understanding phase transitions and physical properties of 2D magnetic materials.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Orbital Magnetization of Correlated States in Twisted Bilayer Transition Metal Dichalcogenides**|Xiaoyu Liu, Chong Wang, Haoran Chen, Xiao-Wei Zhang, Ting Cao, Di Xiao|[[arxiv:2510.01727](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01727)]|This paper extends the modern theory of orbital magnetization to correlated Hartree-Fock states, showing that the standard expression remains valid when evaluated with Hartree-Fock orbitals and Hamiltonians. The authors benchmark their theory against the extended Kane-Mele-Hubbard model and find excellent agreement with direct numerical calculations. Applying their method to twisted MoTe2 bilayers, they discover an orbital magnetization of about one Bohr magneton per moiré cell with a non-monotonic twist angle dependence. This work provides a quantitative framework for interpreting Landau fan asymmetry and distinguishing orbital from spin contributions in moiré ferromagnets.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Fusion Rules of Mobility**|Jie-Yu Zhang, Peng Ye|[[arxiv:2508.13961](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.13961)]|The paper addresses the unexplored transformation of quasiparticle mobility when anyonic topological charges fuse in topological phases of matter. The authors introduce exactly solvable models with \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) topological order enriched by subsystem symmetries, where mobility constraints stem from symmetries on specific subsets. Key results reveal that fusion induces deterministic geometric interference, producing complex multi-channel fusion algebras, exemplified by Fibonacci fusion rules and lineon period transmutation. This work establishes that restricted mobility classes obey their own fusion algebras, significantly expanding the theoretical understanding of topological order and mobility.|
|**2026-05-14**|**Electrostatics in semiconducting devices II: Solving the Helmholtz equation**|Antonio Lacerda-Santos, Xavier Waintal|[[arxiv:2507.03131](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.03131)]|This paper addresses the convergence problems of iterative self-consistency schemes in mean field problems like the Schrödinger-Poisson equation, particularly under strong nonlinearities. The authors propose mapping the problem onto a Non-Linear Helmholtz (NLH) equation, which allows them to construct a convex functional whose minimum guarantees convergent iterative solutions. They then lift the NLH approximation to find the exact solution in typically just one or two iterations. The significance lies in providing a robust, fast, and precise computational method for quantum nanoelectronic device simulations.|

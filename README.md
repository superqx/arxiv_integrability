|**2026-07-20**|**Viscous Accretion Disks around Regular Black Holes Embedded in a Quintessence Dark Energy Field: Beyond the Novikov--Thorne Approximation**|Sandip Dutta|[[arxiv:2607.15679](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15679)]|This paper models thin accretion disks around rotating regular black holes, incorporating both a Hayward non-linear electromagnetic field and a quintessence dark energy field. Unlike the standard Novikov-Thorne model, the authors include a non-zero viscous torque at the innermost stable circular orbit, allowing for a more realistic treatment of the inner boundary. The key finding is that the accretion efficiency and viscosity correction depend on both the Hayward length scale and dark energy density, but not on the viscosity parameter, providing a clear method to distinguish between these two physical effects. This work offers testable observational signatures—such as a 0.87–6.76% viscosity amplification—that instruments like NICER and NuSTAR could use to identify the presence of dark energy and regular black hole structure.|
|**2026-07-20**|**Massive Gravity @ 15**|Claudia de Rham|[[arxiv:2607.15507](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15507)]|This paper reviews the development of ghost-free massive gravity as a modified theory of gravity. The method involves a highly constrained nonlinear interaction structure that propagates exactly five degrees of freedom and raises the strong-coupling scale, with consistency ensured by the Vainshtein mechanism. Key results include formulation of the theory as a well-posed effective field theory, where unitarity and causality single it out as a distinguished infrared theory, and recent connections to black-hole entanglement entropy and \(T\bar{T}\) deformations. The significance lies in providing a theoretical laboratory for exploring UV completions and phenomenology in infrared modifications of General Relativity.|

The model's internal \( U(1) \) symmetry gives rise to a variety of solitary wave solutions, including complex kinks, radiative profiles, and Q-ball configurations.|
|**2026-07-20**|**Quantized Quiver Varieties and the Quantum Spin Ruijsenaars-Schneider Model**|Gleb Arutyunov, Lukas Hardi|[[arxiv:2508.07862](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.07862)]|This paper addresses the long-standing problem of quantizing the rational spin Ruijsenaars-Schneider model. Key results include the identification of a loop algebra and Yangian of \(\mathfrak{gl}_\ell\) within this algebra, and a conjecture that the algebra becomes a shifted affine Yangian in the infinite particle limit. The significance lies in providing a rigorous algebraic framework for the model's quantization and establishing connections to well-studied algebraic structures.|

The authors quantize the system using the Wheeler-DeWitt equation and, through a relational time framework, map the problem onto the radial Schrödinger equation of the hydrogen atom. Solving this exactly, they construct a self-adjoint Hamiltonian and show that wave packets evolve unitarily, producing a non-singular quantum bounce that replaces the classical singularity. The significance lies in providing a robust, analytically exact quantum origin for the matter-bounce scenario, which is necessary for generating a scale-invariant spectrum of primordial perturbations.|

## Condensed Matter

|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-07-20**|**Fragmented ETH: Prethermalization, Timescales, and Ensemble Inequivalence**|C. L. Sriram, Soumya Kanti Pal, Lea F. Santos|[[arxiv:2607.15350](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15350)]|This paper investigates how strong long-range interactions cause anomalously slow thermalization in finite quantum systems. The authors show that nearly conserved quantities fragment the Hilbert space into energy bands, leading to long-lived prethermal plateaus, and develop a perturbative theory for the plateaus' heights and timescales. They propose a fragmented eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (fETH) where quantum chaos develops within individual bands, but finite-size scaling follows symmetry-imposed selection rules. The study reveals that microcanonical and canonical ensembles occupy different band structures, causing ensemble inequivalence without equilibrium phase transitions, which has broad implications for systems with Hilbert-space fragmentation.|
|**2026-07-20**|**Thermodynamic Constraints on Perfect Equilibrium Superconducting Diodes**|Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2512.21384](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21384)]|This paper addresses why perfect equilibrium superconducting diodes, which would allow dissipationless current flow in only one direction, are fundamentally constrained. The authors use thermodynamic arguments to prove that a diode with exactly zero reverse critical current is impossible, as it would require a discontinuity in free energy. They demonstrate that standard models impose finite lower bounds on the reverse current, and that high-efficiency diodes reported in Josephson systems achieve their performance by operating outside of unconstrained equilibrium. The work provides a unified thermodynamic framework to understand and interpret efficiency limits across all superconducting diode platforms.|

The authors propose a modified approach where, even when starting from a discrete system with dynamics differing from the target ODE, the ultradiscrete limit is adjusted to produce a cellular automaton whose behavior more closely matches the continuous model.

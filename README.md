|**2026-06-01**|**Two roles of Alexander in two Kashaev phases**|Dmitry Galakhov, Alexei Morozov|[[arxiv:2605.31588](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31588)]|This paper examines the dual role of Alexander polynomials in Chern-Simons theory within a double-scaling limit known as the Kashaev limit. The authors identify a puzzle where classical $A$-polynomials share roots with Alexander polynomials, yet perturbative expansions of Jones polynomials approach the inverse Alexander polynomial. They resolve this by showing the existence of two distinct phases in the quasiclassical limit: one with nontrivial classical action producing hyperbolic volumes and $A$ -polynomials, and another with vanishing action yielding inverse Alexanders. The findings clarify the consistency of resurgence theory and deepen the understanding of non-perturbative behavior in gauge theories.|
|**2026-06-01**|**Eigenstate chaos in the presence of non-Abelian symmetries**|Siddharth Jindal, Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2605.30798](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30798)]|The paper investigates the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) for systems with non-Abelian symmetries, such as SU(2). The authors develop a non-Abelian microcanonical entropy by resolving eigenstates by symmetry and relate it to the entanglement entropy of chaotic eigenstates. A key result is that the symmetry-resolved entanglement entropy differs from conventional entanglement entropy by a universal logarithmic correction, which depends on the global Casimir charge and charge density. The findings deepen the understanding of thermalization in systems with non-commuting conserved charges, providing corrections to the standard ETH framework.|
|**2026-06-01**|**Exact SU(2) Yang-Mills Waves from a Simple Ansatz**|Yu-Xuan Zhang, Jing-Ling Chen|[[arxiv:2605.04964](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04964)]|This paper proposes a simple ansatz that reduces the sourceless SU(2) Yang-Mills equations to nine algebraic constraints, which are then solved to yield three families of exact wave solutions. Family I recovers linear electromagnetic waves, while Family II describes genuinely nonlinear waves that propagate at the speed of light but feature a constant gauge-invariant offset in the color-electric field and a discrete topological parameter. Family III provides a pure gauge solution. These exact solutions offer new insights into non-Abelian self-interactions and serve as benchmarks for numerical simulations and experiments on synthetic gauge fields.|
|**2026-06-01**|**The fate of Schwarzschild--de Sitter black holes: nonequilibrium evaporation**|Damien A. Easson|[[arxiv:2511.11873](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.11873)]|This paper develops a fully analytic, two-dimensional dilaton gravity model to study the evaporation of Schwarzschild–de Sitter black holes, including backreaction from quantum fields. The method incorporates the trace anomaly of conformal matter and solves for the evolution of black-hole mass and geometry in the Unruh–de Sitter state. Key results show that a steady nonequilibrium Hawking flux drives irreversible heat flow from the black hole to the cosmological horizon whenever their surface gravities differ, leading to monotonic entropy growth. The significance lies in providing the first complete, backreacted solution for black-hole evaporation in a cosmological setting, which also naturally yields quantum extremal surfaces and entanglement islands for studying the Page curve.|

|**2026-06-01**|**Two roles of Alexander in two Kashaev phases**|Dmitry Galakhov, Alexei Morozov|[[arxiv:2605.31588](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31588)]|This paper explores the ambiguity inherent in resurgence theory, using Chern-Simons theory in the double-scaling "Kashaev limit" as an example. The authors identify a puzzle: Alexander polynomials emerge in two seemingly opposite roles, with classical $A$-polynomials sharing roots with them, while Jones polynomials approach their inverse. They resolve this by showing that the quasiclassical limit yields two distinct phases, one with non-trivial classical actions leading to $A$ -polynomials, and another with vanishing actions producing inverse Alexander polynomials. The findings are significant for understanding non-perturbative effects and their structural consistency in gauge theories like Yang-Mills.|
|**2026-06-01**|**Hubbard--Heisenberg Thermodynamic Comparison at Half Filling in a Fixed Staggered Field**|Tadahiro Miyao|[[arxiv:2605.30663](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30663)]|This paper presents a rigorous mathematical comparison between the repulsive Hubbard model and the Heisenberg model at half filling in the presence of a staggered magnetic field. The authors use a strong-coupling unitary transformation to isolate the singly occupied spin sector, enabling them to prove that the Hubbard and Heisenberg pressures are uniformly close for large interaction strength \(U\) and low temperatures. Key results include uniform error estimates on pressure differences within a finite staggered-field range, and charge-sector suppression showing that empty or doubly occupied sites and charge fluctuations are negligible. These findings provide a quantitative Gibbs-state foundation for the established physical picture that the half-filled Hubbard model effectively reduces to antiferromagnetic Heisenberg spin degrees of freedom at the Heisenberg energy scale.|
|**2026-06-01**|**Exact SU(2) Yang-Mills Waves from a Simple Ansatz**|Yu-Xuan Zhang, Jing-Ling Chen|[[arxiv:2605.04964](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04964)]|This paper addresses the problem of finding exact wave solutions in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory. The authors propose a simple ansatz that reduces the field equations to algebraic constraints, yielding three closed-form families of solutions. Key results include Family I, which reproduces linear electromagnetic waves; Family II, which describes genuinely nonlinear waves with a constant color-electric field offset and a topological parameter; and Family III, which is a pure gauge solution. The significance lies in providing exact benchmarks for numerical simulations and insights into how non-Abelian self-interactions fundamentally alter wave propagation.|
|**2026-06-01**|**Integral formula for the propagator of the one-dimensional Hubbard model**|Taiki Ishiyama, Kazuya Fujimoto, Tomohiro Sasamoto|[[arxiv:2602.21541](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21541)]|The paper addresses the problem of computing the multi-particle propagator for the one-dimensional Hubbard model on an infinite lattice. The method involves deriving an exact integral formula using the nested Bethe ansatz, avoiding reliance on the string hypothesis. A key result is that this formula provides an explicit integral representation for the time evolution of any finite-particle wave function. This work is significant because it establishes a foundation for the exact analysis of nonequilibrium dynamics in the Hubbard model and can be extended to related open quantum systems.|

They discovered that spin-singlet entanglement emerges precisely at the pseudogap onset and remains confined to nearest-neighbor sites, unlike classical correlations. The method solves a spurious self-interaction problem in the imaginary-time path integral by employing a careful Hubbard-Stratonovich derivation that introduces a necessary correction term in the FRG flow. Key results show that while the free energy is robust to different closure approximations, the chemical potential and density correlators are sensitive diagnostics, with a maximum-entropy closure best reproducing even low-temperature oscillatory features. The work establishes two prerequisites for accurate functional approaches—retaining the equal-time contact subtraction in the flow and ensuring statistical consistency of density correlators—providing a controlled foundation for developing *ab initio* density functionals across physics and chemistry.|

The authors develop a generalized finite-time scaling (FTS) framework that unifies these two regimes, demonstrating robust scaling behavior for the full range of quench rates within the critical region. Key results show the FTS form bridges the KZ scaling in the slow-driving regime and the DGP scaling in the sudden-quench limit, verified through numerical simulations of quantum critical and tricritical points. This work establishes a universal theory for nonequilibrium critical dynamics applicable to any driving rate, with significant implications for quantum quench experiments and out-of-equilibrium statistical mechanics.|

|**2026-06-01**|**Full Quantum Work Statistics for Non-Homogeneous Many-Body Systems**|Antonio Palamara, Francesco Plastina, Antonello Sindona, Irene D'Amico|[[arxiv:2512.18338](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.18338)]|This paper develops a method to compute the full statistics of quantum work in interacting many-body systems by extending thermal time-dependent density functional theory.

|**2026-05-11**|**Multiscale Structure of Eigenstate Thermalization**|Pavel Orlov, Rustem Sharipov, Enej Ilievski|[[arxiv:2605.08079](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08079)]|This paper investigates the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis by examining how the statistical properties of local observables depend on the choice of ensemble used to sample eigenstates. The authors move beyond conventional microcanonical windows and introduce ensembles with a tunable scale parameter that controls charge fluctuations, using an integrable field theory for efficient numerical analysis and thermodynamic extrapolation. They identify a class of states that allows for explicit calculation of the suppression rate of matrix elements. The key result reveals a multiscale structure in the matrix elements, characterized by non-analytic dependence of algebraic exponents on the fluctuation scale, which extends the understanding of thermalization mechanisms in isolated quantum systems.|
|**2026-05-11**|**Global approximations to correlation functions of strongly interacting quantum field theories**|Yuanran Zhu, Yang Yu, Efekan K\"okc\"u, Emanuel Gull, Chao Yang|[[arxiv:2512.18532](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.18532)]|This paper introduces a method to approximate correlation functions in strongly interacting quantum field theories by interpolating between their weak and strong coupling expansions. The method uses two-point Padé approximants, which were benchmarked on two models: the lattice φ⁴ theory and the 2D Hubbard model. Key results show global convergence to the exact correlation function for the φ⁴ theory and reasonable characterization of the Hubbard model’s Matsubara Green’s function, even at second order. The work is significant as it provides a practical way to extend perturbative results into strong-coupling regimes, with convergence properties explained via analytic function theory.|
|**2026-05-11**|**A superintegrable quantum field theory**|Marine De Clerck, Oleg Evnin|[[arxiv:2511.03373](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03373)]|The paper explores the quantum version of the cubic Szegő equation, a classical field theory known for its Lax integrability and turbulent dynamics. The authors study the quantum Hamiltonian, which, along with its conserved quantities, exhibits purely integer spectra, suggesting a superintegrable structure beyond standard integrability. They present both analytic and empirical results on eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and ladder operators to characterize the system. This work initiates a systematic investigation into a quantum field theory with unexpectedly rigid spectral properties and deep algebraic structure.|
|**2026-05-11**|**Enhancing the ergodicity of Worldvolume HMC via embedding Generalized-thimble HMC**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2508.02659](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.02659)]|The paper addresses ergodicity issues in the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method, which can arise when the worldvolume becomes too thin. The authors propose embedding Generalized-thimble HMC (GT-HMC) into the WV-HMC framework to improve phase-space exploration. They prove the consistency of the combined algorithm and validate it on a two-dimensional doped Hubbard model, showing agreement with standalone methods. This approach enables simulations on larger spacetime lattices with controlled statistical errors, demonstrated by extrapolating densities at fixed temperature and interaction strength.|
|**2026-05-11**|**Applying the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo method to the Hubbard model away from half filling**|Masafumi Fukuma, Yusuke Namekawa|[[arxiv:2507.23748](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.23748)]|This paper applies the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method to the two-dimensional Hubbard model doped away from half filling, a system plagued by a severe numerical sign problem. The method mitigates the sign problem and avoids ergodicity issues associated with Lefschetz thimble approaches. Key results show that WV-HMC successfully computes number and energy densities on 6×6 and 8×8 lattices at specific temperature and interaction parameters, remaining effective where standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo fails. The study demonstrates the method's potential for strongly correlated systems, though current computational cost scales as O(N³) due to direct solvers.|

Solving these equations yields a complete classification of new superintegrable families with spin-dependent interactions. The findings enrich the class of integrable models relevant to nucleon-nucleon interactions and advance the broader program of classifying superintegrable quantum systems with spin.|
|**2026-05-11**|**On ratios of theta functions**|Senping Luo, Juncheng Wei|[[arxiv:2605.07580](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07580)]|This paper investigates the minimization and maximization of ratios of theta and Epstein zeta functions, motivated by their appearance in average partition functions in string theory. The authors completely classify the lattices that act as extremizers for these ratios, finding that the hexagonal lattice plays a crucial role. The results are directly applicable to conformal field theory and Liouville field theory through partition functions. Furthermore, the findings inform the study of differences between these functions, with implications for crystallization and interacting particle systems.|

The work reveals a superintegrable quantum field theory with a uniquely constrained and hierarchical structure.|

|**2026-05-11**|**The General Structure of Trilinear Equations**|Takeshi Fukuyama|[[arxiv:2605.05624](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05624)]|This paper extends the Hirota bilinear formalism to trilinear structures in integrable systems. The authors apply this framework to the stationary axisymmetric Einstein equations, decomposing the nonlinear Ernst equation into a cubic sector of second-derivative terms, which is matched to a YTSF-type trilinear kernel. They formulate a general trilinear kernel criterion and demonstrate that Tomimatsu–Sato solutions with δ=2 and δ=3 share the same kernel structure with universal normalization. This result suggests that the trilinear kernel governs the highest-derivative sector of the Ernst system, offering a new perspective on integrability beyond the bilinear hierarchy.|

The key problem is that the quantum Hamiltonian and its conserved charges exhibit purely integer spectra, suggesting a higher level of integrability. The authors present a systematic study using both analytic methods and empirical observations to analyze the system's eigenvalues, eigenvectors, conservation laws, and ladder operators. Their findings reveal a "superintegrable" structure in the quantum field theory, which goes beyond standard integrable models.|

## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-21**|**Entangling Power: A Probe of Symmetry and Integrability in Quantum Many-Body Systems**|Ian Low, Pallab Goswami|[[arxiv:2605.20661](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20661)]|The paper investigates how the entangling power of unitary operators can detect symmetry and integrability in quantum many-body systems, focusing on Heisenberg spin chains. The authors compute the time-averaged entangling power for two-site models and finite-size XXZ chains, finding a monotonic decrease with larger symmetry groups and sharp dips at specific points. Key results include that the free-fermion point shows a slowly decaying dip in finite systems but achieves maximum entangling power in the thermodynamic limit, contrasting with minimal power at SU(2) symmetric points. This demonstrates entangling power as a practical operator-based diagnostic for symmetry and integrability in quantum simulations.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Schr\"odinger-invariance in non-equilibrium critical dynamics**|Malte Henkel, Stoimen Stoimenov|[[arxiv:2510.25429](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25429)]|This paper addresses the challenge of predicting the universal scaling behavior of correlation functions in non-equilibrium critical systems with a dynamical exponent of z=2. The authors introduce a novel time-dependent representation of the Schrödinger algebra to derive explicit functional forms for both single-time and two-time correlators. They validate these theoretical predictions by confirming them against the exact solutions of several known ageing models, such as the spherical model and the kinetic Ising model at its critical point. This work is significant as it provides a unified and precise algebraic framework for understanding the universal non-equilibrium dynamics of critical systems, extending beyond the well-studied equilibrium case.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Integrable systems from Poisson reductions of generalized Hamiltonian torus actions**|L. Feher, M. Fairon|[[arxiv:2507.12051](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.12051)]|This paper develops a general framework for reducing integrable systems with a symmetry group \(K\) to integrable systems on open subsets of the quotient Poisson space \(M/K\). The key method requires the unreduced system to have "action variables" generating a proper, effective action of \(\mathrm{U}(1)^{\ell_1} \times \mathbb{R}^{\ell_2}\), which descend to the reduced system. A significant result is that the reduced systems are often superintegrable, and the framework solves open integrability problems for systems derived from Poisson reductions on compact Lie group doubles. This work has broad applications, particularly in establishing integrability for systems on moduli spaces of flat connections via a quasi-Poisson approach.|

The work’s significance lies in providing a unified approach to construct and confirm integrability for systems on moduli spaces of flat connections and other quotient Poisson spaces.|

|**2026-05-21**|**Strange metallicity in the Kagome metal Ni $_3$ In: a DMFT investigation**|Ruslan Mushkaev, Francesco Petocchi, Philipp Werner|[[arxiv:2605.21386](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21386)]|This paper investigates strange metallicity in the Kagome metal Ni₃In using dynamical mean field theory (DMFT) applied to a single-band Hubbard model. The key results show that, even with moderate Hubbard repulsion and high band filling, the system exhibits non-Fermi-liquid behavior and local magnetic moment formation. Increasing hole doping leads to a crossover into a heavy Fermi-liquid regime. The study explains these findings through an effective model of the partially filled narrow band, revealing how coexisting localized and itinerant electrons drive the strange metallic state.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Fermion condensation in a generalized Hatsugai-Kohmoto model with momentum-mixing Landau interactions**|Jan Heinrich, Andreas R\"uckriegel, Peter Kopietz|[[arxiv:2605.21277](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21277)]|This paper generalizes the exactly solvable Hatsugai-Kohmoto (HK) model by adding momentum-mixing Landau interactions. Using a self-consistent mean-field analysis, the authors find the ground state features a partially flat energy band, consistent with the fermion condensation scenario. They map the model onto a generalized Ising model, where the flat band corresponds to a smoothed magnetic domain wall in pseudospin space. Building on this insight, they propose an exactly solvable variant of the HK model with a unique ground state at all densities, highlighting its significance for understanding fermion condensation.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Strain-Tuned Incommensurate Kekul\'e Spiral Order in Twisted Bilayer Graphene: a Quantum Many-Body Study**|Cheng Huang, Yves H. Kwan, Maksim Ulybyshev, Fakher F. Assaad, Laura Classen, Zi Yang Meng|[[arxiv:2605.20677](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20677)]|This paper investigates the interacting ground state of magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene at filling factors away from charge neutrality. The authors combine quantum Monte Carlo, exact diagonalization, and Hartree-Fock methods to overcome computational challenges and study strain dependence. They identify a transition from a Kramers intervalley coherent state to an incommensurate Kekulé spiral state as strain is tuned. This work provides insight into the phase diagram of flat-band systems and demonstrates a viable computational approach for strongly correlated materials.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Entangling Power: A Probe of Symmetry and Integrability in Quantum Many-Body Systems**|Ian Low, Pallab Goswami|[[arxiv:2605.20661](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20661)]|This paper introduces the entangling power of a unitary operator as a tool to probe symmetries and integrability in quantum many-body systems. The authors compute this measure for anisotropic Heisenberg spin chains, including finite-size systems and the two-magnon S-matrix in the thermodynamic limit. Key results show that entangling power decreases with larger symmetry groups in two-site models, exhibits sharp dips at symmetry-enhanced points in finite chains, and varies from zero to maximum depending on the integrable point in the thermodynamic limit. The significance lies in demonstrating that entangling power serves as an operator-based diagnostic for identifying symmetry and certain integrability features in quantum simulations.|
|**2026-05-21**|**The critical slowing down in diffusion models**|Luca Maria Del Bono, Giulio Biroli, Patrick Charbonneau, Marylou Gabri\'e|[[arxiv:2605.12597](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12597)]|This paper analyzes diffusion models through the lens of the \(O(n)\) statistical field model in the large \(n\) limit. It finds that training a simple one-layer score-matching network exhibits critical slowing down, where learning time scales poorly with system size and hinders generation near criticality. The key result shows that using a two-layer architecture with physical locality overcomes this bottleneck, reducing training time from quadratic to logarithmic scaling without increasing parameter count. This establishes a theoretical framework for understanding and improving learned sampling methods, demonstrating that architectural design can mitigate fundamental sampling difficulties.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Thermal and quantum fluctuations in extended Kitaev-Yao-Lee spin-orbital model**|Jiefu Cen, Hae-Young Kee|[[arxiv:2603.08710](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08710)]|This paper investigates the origin and stability of a nematic spin-orbital phase in the extended Kitaev-Yao-Lee model using classical Monte Carlo simulations and generalized spin wave theory. The study focuses on how thermal and quantum fluctuations affect this model, which combines the Kitaev and Yao-Lee interactions with the Kugel-Khomskii term. Key results show that the additional spin-orbital degree of freedom generates strong fluctuations that stabilize disordered phases. These findings provide insight into the emergence of spin-orbital liquids and the robustness of quantum spin liquids in realistic materials.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Interference-Induced Suppression of Doublon Transport and Prethermalization in the Extended Bose-Hubbard Model**|Zhen-Ting Bao, Kai Xu, Heng Fan|[[arxiv:2601.03694](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03694)]|This paper addresses the problem of doublon mobility in the Bose-Hubbard model, which limits their use as stable information carriers due to second-order hopping processes. The authors propose a method to suppress this mobility by introducing a carefully optimized nearest-neighbor pair-hopping term that creates destructive interference with the dominant virtual hopping channel. Their simulations show that this scheme achieves near-complete dynamical arrest and entanglement preservation in one-dimensional chains, and significantly reduces ballistic spreading in two-dimensional lattices, though with slow residual expansion. The key significance lies in demonstrating that this interference-induced suppression leads to a long-lived prethermal plateau in the many-body regime, stemming from a dramatic separation of microscopic and thermalization timescales.|
|**2026-05-21**|**Schr\"odinger-invariance in non-equilibrium critical dynamics**|Malte Henkel, Stoimen Stoimenov|[[arxiv:2510.25429](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25429)]|The paper addresses the problem of deriving scaling functions for non-equilibrium critical dynamics with a dynamical exponent of z=2. The authors propose a new time-dependent representation of the Schrödinger algebra to predict these functions for single-time and two-time correlators. They validate their theoretical predictions by testing them against several exactly solvable ageing models, finding confirmation. This work provides a unified framework for understanding spatio-temporal scaling in non-equilibrium critical systems, revealing the underlying algebraic structure driving these phenomena.|

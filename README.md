Key findings show that de Sitter space is an exact stable solution, while bouncing cosmologies require fluids with exotic properties like phantom or ghost energy, potentially leading to a new type of future singularity. The results are significant because they reveal universal behavior across different formulations of the theory and lay the groundwork for connecting fractional gravity to observable cosmology.|

The authors construct eigenstates using algebraic Bethe-ansatz techniques and derive Bethe equations for arbitrary matrix field operators, generalizing previous methods. Focusing on the spin-1 (2×2 matrix) case, they obtain integral equations for thermodynamics and compute the ground state phase diagram across chemical potential and magnetic field parameters. The significance lies in demonstrating QCL as a physics-informed error mitigation technique that generates shorter, robust circuits without requiring exponential sampling overhead.|

|**2026-05-01**|**Quantum integrable matrix models of spinor Bose gases in one spatial dimension**|Hannes K\"oper, Thomas Gasenzer|[[arxiv:2604.27885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27885)]|The paper identifies that degenerate spinor Bose gases with repulsive density-density and anti-ferromagnetic spin-spin interactions in one spatial dimension are described by a quantum integrable matrix extension of the nonlinear Schrödinger model. Using algebraic Bethe-ansatz techniques, the authors construct eigenstates and derive Bethe equations for arbitrary matrix field operators, generalizing previous methods to handle arbitrary spin multiplets. For the specific $2\times2$ model, corresponding to a spin-$1$ Bose gas, integral equations are derived to describe equilibrium thermodynamics, and the ground state phase diagram is computed both numerically and analytically. Using numerical simulations in a chaotic quantum model, the authors find exponential relaxation in both cases. Key results show that all moments in deep thermalization relax at the same rate, matching the autocorrelation rate, while higher-order correlation functions in full thermalization relax faster. The significance is the identification that full thermalization accelerates at higher orders, whereas deep thermalization does not, indicating distinct timescales for these two forms of equilibration.|

|**2026-05-01**|**Non-Local Magic Resources for Fermionic Gaussian States**|Daniele Iannotti, Beatrice Magni, Riccardo Cioli, Alioscia Hamma, Xhek Turkeshi|[[arxiv:2604.27049](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27049)]|This paper addresses the computational bottleneck of measuring "non-local magic," a quantum resource combining entanglement and nonstabilizerness, by developing a closed-form expression for fermionic Gaussian states. The work establishes a new criticality class for three-body interactions, with implications for understanding emergent order and phase transitions in higher-order cognitive and network architectures.|

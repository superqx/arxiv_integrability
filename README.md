|**2026-07-22**|**Boundary quenches in (1+1)-dimensional conformal field theory**|Michele Fossati, Colin Rylands, Eytan Grosfeld, Eran Sela, Pasquale Calabrese|[[arxiv:2607.19166](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19166)]|This paper studies a local quantum quench where the boundary condition of a (1+1)-dimensional conformal field theory is suddenly changed. The authors derive a universal formula for the time evolution of one-point functions and find that the entanglement entropy of a subsystem near the boundary undergoes a sharp finite jump, with a magnitude given by the logarithm of the ratio of boundary g-factors. They verify these predictions using Matrix Product State simulations of the critical Ising spin chain. The results reveal universal dynamical signatures of boundary quenches, linking nonequilibrium entanglement dynamics directly to boundary critical phenomena.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Gravitational Memory Beyond Null Infinity through Finite-Distance Carrollian Screens**|Felipe Diaz, Sercan H\"usn\"ugil, Oriana Labrin, Leonardo Sanhueza|[[arxiv:2607.18675](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18675)]|The paper addresses the problem of understanding gravitational memory beyond null infinity by introducing finite-distance null hypersurfaces with Carrollian geometry. The method involves using Robinson–Trautman spacetimes as a solvable model, transforming to Bondi gauge, and analyzing the quasilocal Carrollian dissipative system defined by the null screen’s geometry and Brown–York tensor. Key results show that the Bondi displacement memory emerges as the asymptotic limit of a finite-screen memory, with corrections including focusing and angular drift; late-time screens approaching the Schwarzschild horizon exhibit exponential decay of anisotropic data. The significance lies in demonstrating that Bondi memory is a universal projection of a broader quasilocal Carrollian response, linking asymptotic and near-zone physics in gravitational radiation.|

|**2026-07-22**|**Superintegrable systems of two interacting spin- $\frac12$ particles with first-order vector integrals of motion**|O. Ogulcan Tuncer, I. Yurdusen|[[arxiv:2607.19155](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19155)]|The paper addresses the problem of classifying quantum superintegrable systems for two interacting spin-½ particles in three dimensions. The authors restrict their analysis to Hamiltonians with a \(V_4=0\) spin-momentum term and search for nontrivial first-order vector integrals of motion. By constructing a general Hermitian first-order vector operator and imposing commutativity with the Hamiltonian, they derive an overdetermined system of radial equations that yields the complete set of admissible potentials and corresponding integrals. The results extend prior classifications of scalar and pseudo-scalar integrals for two-spin systems and are significant for identifying exactly solvable cases, including Coulomb- and oscillator-type solutions via scalar reduction.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Integrable multi-species SSEP with reactive particle species**|Mathieu Dabrowski, Lo\"ic Poulain d'Andecy, Eric Ragoucy|[[arxiv:2607.18959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18959)]|This paper introduces a new class of integrable exclusion processes, generalizing the multi-species Symmetric Simple Exclusion Process (SSEP) by including "reactive" particle species that can evaporate, condensate, or transform in pairs. The authors prove integrability of these models using the Yang-Baxter Equation for periodic systems and, for open boundaries, by employing solutions of the reflection equation. Key results include a full combinatorial characterization of the sectors for the simpler $(p,1)$-SSEP and the derivation of physical quantities in the non-equilibrium stationary state for the more general $(p,q)$ -SSEP with specific integrable boundaries. This work broadens the family of solvable non-equilibrium systems, enabling exact analysis of transport phenomena in models with chemical reactions.|

|**2026-07-22**|**Dynamical correlation functions of extensive charges after global quantum quenches**|Riccardo Travaglino, Katja Klobas, Bruno Bertini, Pasquale Calabrese|[[arxiv:2607.19208](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19208)]|This paper investigates the behavior of dynamical correlation functions for extensive U(1) charges after global quantum quenches. Using space-time duality, the authors derive a method to characterize the n-time cumulant generating function for initial states symmetric under the charge. They show that under weak temporal correlations, such as ballistic transport, this function factorizes into a sum of single-time functions, leading to a dramatic simplification where time-ordered correlators depend only on the smallest time. Supported by analytical and numerical tests in free and interacting models, these results provide a fundamental insight into the structure of out-of-equilibrium quantum systems.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Boundary quenches in (1+1)-dimensional conformal field theory**|Michele Fossati, Colin Rylands, Eytan Grosfeld, Eran Sela, Pasquale Calabrese|[[arxiv:2607.19166](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19166)]|This paper studies local quantum quenches where the boundary condition of a (1+1)-dimensional conformal field theory is abruptly changed. The authors derive a universal expression for the time evolution of one-point functions on the half-line and use it to determine the dynamics of bipartite entanglement for subsystems near the boundary. A key result is that the entanglement entropy undergoes a sharp finite jump equal to the logarithm of the ratio of boundary g-factors, which is verified by numerical simulations of the critical Ising spin chain. The findings reveal universal dynamical signatures of boundary quenches and link nonequilibrium entanglement dynamics to boundary critical phenomena.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Integrable multi-species SSEP with reactive particle species**|Mathieu Dabrowski, Lo\"ic Poulain d'Andecy, Eric Ragoucy|[[arxiv:2607.18959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18959)]|This paper introduces integrable multi-species exclusion processes that extend the symmetric simple exclusion process (SSEP) by adding reactive particle species. The authors construct these processes using set-theoretical solutions of the Yang-Baxter equation and prove integrability for both periodic and open boundary conditions. Key results include a full combinatorial study of sectors in the periodic case and computation of physical quantities in the non-equilibrium stationary state for the open system. The significance lies in providing exactly solvable models that incorporate particle reactions, such as evaporation and condensation, broadening the scope of integrable non-equilibrium systems.|
|**2026-07-22**|**State $k$-designs from Hamiltonian evolution**|Shengxian Hou, Zong-Yue Hou, Zhi-Cheng Yang|[[arxiv:2607.18537](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18537)]|The paper investigates how time evolution under a fixed Hamiltonian can generate state $k$-designs, where the ensemble consists of states evolved from initial states sampled from another ensemble. For Hamiltonians from the Gaussian unitary ensemble, the authors derive a relation showing that the evolved ensemble forms an exact state $k$-design in the thermodynamic limit if the initial ensemble is a state 1-design. They also demonstrate through analytic and numerical results that a simple nonintegrable mixed-field Ising Hamiltonian can generate approximate state $k$-designs from product states, with finite-time corrections scaling as $O(1/T)$, and propose an $M$ -step quench protocol to reduce this. The work provides a unified framework for understanding how quantum quenches can produce unitary and state designs, with implications for quantum information and many-body physics.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Quantum phase diagrams for bosons in hexagonal optical potentials: A continuous-space quantum Monte Carlo study**|Danilo Nascimento Guimaraes, Laurent Sanchez-Palencia|[[arxiv:2605.06171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06171)]|This paper investigates the phase diagrams of ultracold bosonic atoms in honeycomb (graphene-like) and hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) optical lattices using continuous-space quantum Monte Carlo methods, rather than the standard discrete Bose-Hubbard model. The study reveals that in honeycomb lattices, the continuous treatment predicts suppressed Mott insulator lobes and the absence of higher-order insulating phases, a deviation caused by density-assisted tunneling. For the asymmetric h-BN lattice, richer phase diagrams with multiple Mott lobes and varying sublattice occupations emerge from the interplay between lattice asymmetry, interactions, and particle filling. The findings underscore the necessity of continuous-space approaches to accurately capture bosonic phases in hexagonal geometries, guiding future ultracold-atom experiments and theory.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Information in Many-body Eigenstates: A Question of Learnability**|Maksymilian Kliczkowski, Jaros{\l}aw Paw{\l}owski, Masudul Haque|[[arxiv:2605.03043](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03043)]|This paper introduces "learnability" as a new metric to quantify how much information individual eigenstates contain about their parent Hamiltonian. Using an encoder-decoder neural network on a non-integrable spin chain, the authors show that low-entanglement eigenstates near the spectral edges allow for much higher-accuracy Hamiltonian reconstruction from fewer states than high-entanglement, near-random mid-spectrum eigenstates. The results highlight a stark difference in the information content of eigenstates across the spectrum. This framework offers a new perspective on the spectral structure of many-body quantum systems.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Pseudogap, Fermi liquid, Van Hove singularity and maxima of the compressibility and of the Knight shift as a function of doping in the two-dimensional Hubbard model**|Y. M. Tremblay|[[arxiv:2602.06298](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06298)]|The paper investigates the pseudogap-to-Fermi liquid transition in the two-dimensional Hubbard model, focusing on a maximum in the isothermal compressibility observed in recent cold-atom experiments. Using the two-particle self-consistent plus (TPSC+) approach, the authors connect this maximum to the point where the precursor spin density wave (SDW) band crosses zero frequency, marking the spectral transformation from pseudogapped to metallic behavior. Key results show that the compressibility and Knight shift maxima coincide with this doping level, driven by critical thermal SDW fluctuations, and that the correlation length at this point is controlled by dynamic fluctuations. The significance lies in linking thermodynamic and single-particle properties across the pseudogap transition, with implications for understanding cuprate superconductors and providing a framework for further studies with realistic hopping parameters.|
|**2026-07-22**|**Thermodynamic-Limit Evidence for Chiral Superconductivity Induced by Doping Chiral Topological Phases**|Sen Niu, D. N. They find that the SU(3) model exhibits magnetic correlations, non-s-wave pairing symmetry, and enhanced binding energies that are strikingly similar to the well-known SU(2) case on a square lattice.

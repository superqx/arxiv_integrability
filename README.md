|**2026-04-16**|**Gravitational Sommerfeld Effects: Formalism, Renormalization, and Perturbation to $O(G^{10})$**|Chih-Hao Chang, Chia-Hsien Shen, Zihan Zhou|[[arxiv:2604.14112](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14112)]|This paper addresses the problem of accurately modeling gravitational waveforms from binary inspirals by systematically accounting for universal "tail effects" (gravitational Sommerfeld effects) that arise from the curved spacetime background. The method develops a worldline effective field theory (EFT) framework, combining renormalization techniques with black hole perturbation theory to analytically compute the Sommerfeld factor. The key results include a closed-form expression for this factor, a proof linking its phase to elastic Compton scattering, and an analytical solution for its magnitude and phase to tenth order in Newton's constant (O(G¹⁰)). The significance lies in providing high-precision data and exact relations that enable improved waveform resummation, which is crucial for gravitational-wave astronomy and testing general relativity.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Universal analytic dependence of the stress-energy tensor at thermodynamic equilibrium in curved space-time**|F. Becattini (University of Florence,INFN), F. Palli (University of Florence,INFN)|[[arxiv:2604.13975](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13975)]|This paper addresses the problem of describing the average stress-energy tensor for a quantum field in curved spacetime at global thermodynamic equilibrium, which typically involves complex non-perturbative contributions. The method involves analyzing exact solutions for a free massless scalar field in specific spacetimes (Minkowski, de Sitter, anti-de Sitter, and a closed Einstein universe) and using a procedure called analytic distillation to separate the gradient expansion. The key result is that the analytic part of this expansion—consisting of integer powers of derivatives of the temperature field and metric—is finite and takes the same covariant form across these different spacetimes, while non-universal, non-analytic terms depend on boundary conditions. The significance is the proposed universality of this analytic structure, suggesting it may extend to any quantum field theory on a curved background, offering a general framework for thermodynamic contributions in gravitational settings.|

## Mathematical Physics

|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-16**|**Pseudo-Riemannian Lie algebras with coisotropic ideals and integrating the Laplace-Beltrami equation on Lie groups**|A. A. Magazev, I. V. Shirokov|[[arxiv:2603.09535](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09535)]|This paper addresses the challenge of solving the Laplace-Beltrami equation on Lie groups, which is a second-order PDE that is generally difficult to integrate exactly. The authors develop a method for a specific class of pseudo-Riemannian Lie groups characterized by the existence of a coisotropic commutative ideal in the Lie algebra. Using the noncommutative integration technique based on the orbit method, they reduce the governing equation to a simpler, first-order PDE that can be solved explicitly.  The key result is that this reduction procedure not only yields exact solutions but also generates nonlocal, integro-differential symmetry operators for the original Laplace-Beltrami equation. This is demonstrated through two examples, including a four-dimensional case where standard separation of variables fails.  The significance of this work lies in identifying a new, integrable class of metrics and extending the known landscape of symmetry operators for such equations beyond polynomial types to nonlocal ones, providing a powerful tool for constructing exact solutions.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-04-16**|**Configuration interaction extension of AGP for incorporating inter-geminal correlations**|Airi Kawasaki, Fei Gao, Gustavo E. Scuseria|[[arxiv:2604.14115](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14115)]|This paper addresses the challenge of improving the antisymmetrized geminal power (AGP) wavefunction, which lacks explicit correlations between electron pairs (geminals). The method introduces an AGP-based configuration interaction (AGP-CI) expansion, which is made computationally practical by reformulating it as a compact linear combination of AGPs. Key results from tests on the Hubbard model and small molecules show this approach achieves high accuracy, especially outperforming simpler expansions for larger, strongly correlated systems. Its significance lies in providing a systematically improvable and efficient framework for capturing crucial inter-pair correlations in quantum chemistry.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Hole and spin dynamics in an anti-ferromagnet close to half filling**|Magnus Callsen, Jens H. Nyhegn, Kristian Knakkergaard Nielsen, Georg M. Bruun|[[arxiv:2604.14039](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14039)]|This paper addresses how adding holes (missing electrons) to a half-filled antiferromagnetic system disrupts its magnetic order and creates complex spin-charge dynamics. The authors develop a specialized diagrammatic technique to study the strongly repulsive, doped Fermi-Hubbard model. Their key findings show that doping creates damped hole pockets and softens magnons, suppressing antiferromagnetism and producing a lattice modulation response consistent with experimental pseudogap signatures. The work's significance lies in providing a systematic theoretical framework to analyze the competition between spin and charge that underpins pseudogap physics in strongly correlated materials.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Bosonic Working Media in a Frustrated Rhombi Chain: Otto and Stirling Cycles from Flat Bands, Caging, and Flux Control**|Francisco J. Pe\~na, Rafael Garc\'ia-Zamora, Gabriele De Chiara, Jorge Flores, Santiago Henr\'iquez, Felipe Barra, Patricio Vargas|[[arxiv:2604.13185](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13185)]|This paper investigates how geometric frustration and flat-band engineering can enhance quantum heat engines. The authors study noninteracting bosons on a rhombi-chain lattice, where an applied magnetic flux tunes the band structure from dispersive to completely flat, inducing an Aharonov-Bohm caging regime. Key results show that an Otto cycle near this flat-band regime significantly boosts both work output and efficiency by suppressing heat release, while a Stirling cycle extracts more work over a broader range but at lower efficiency. The work establishes spectral control via synthetic gauge fields as a practical method for optimizing thermodynamic performance in bosonic systems.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Superconductivity near two-dimensional Van Hove singularities: a determinant quantum Monte Carlo study**|Gustav Romare, Daniel Shaffer, Alex Levchenko, Edwin Huang, Ilya Esterlis|[[arxiv:2604.13161](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13161)]|This paper investigates how the superconducting transition temperature (\(T_c\)) in a two-dimensional material is affected by the shape of its electronic energy spectrum, specifically near points known as Van Hove singularities. The researchers used determinant quantum Monte Carlo simulations to study the attractive Hubbard model, comparing scenarios with standard and enhanced singularities. Their key finding is that while \(T_c\) is boosted near these singularities for weak electron interactions, the enhancement is less than simple theory predicts, and the highest possible \(T_c\) actually occurs at intermediate interaction strengths away from the singular density. This work is significant because it clarifies the limits of using Van Hove singularities to engineer high-temperature superconductors, showing that strong electron correlations ultimately dictate the optimal conditions.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Programmable Fermionic Quantum Processors with Globally Controlled Lattices**|Gabriele Calliari, Charles Fromonteil, Francesco Cesa, Torsten V. Zache, Philipp M. Preiss, Robert Ott, Hannes Pichler|[[arxiv:2604.13160](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13160)]|This paper addresses the challenge of achieving universal quantum computation with fermionic particles, which is difficult due to their complex statistics and the need for precise local control. The proposed method uses globally controlled parameters, like tunneling and interaction strengths in a Fermi-Hubbard model, to manipulate itinerant fermions in systems like optical lattices. The key results include a constructive proof that this global-control framework is universal for fermionic quantum processing and the development of protocols for simulating extended models. Its significance lies in providing a scalable and potentially simpler pathway to programmable fermionic quantum simulators and computers, applicable to various physical platforms beyond neutral atoms.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Symmetry-Fractionalized Skin Effects in Non-Hermitian Luttinger Liquids**|Christopher Ekman, Emil J. Bergholtz, Paolo Molignini|[[arxiv:2603.28849](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28849)]|This paper investigates how strong interactions influence the non-Hermitian skin effect—a phenomenon where particles accumulate at boundaries—in one-dimensional gapless systems. The authors develop a theoretical framework showing that in interacting systems, the skin effect can fractionalize, meaning distinct symmetry sectors like spin and charge exhibit independent skin accumulations, analogous to spin-charge separation. Key results include a numerical demonstration of this separation in the non-Hermitian Hubbard model and the theoretical construction of an exotic interaction-induced skin effect with an \(E_8\) symmetry structure, which cannot exist in free systems. This work is significant as it reveals a new interplay between non-Hermitian topology and strong correlations, expanding the classification of non-Hermitian phenomena.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Dynamics of two particles with quasiperiodic long-range interactions**|Yun Zou|[[arxiv:2603.25045](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25045)]|This paper studies how two fermions move on a one-dimensional lattice when their long-range interaction strength varies in a quasiperiodic pattern. The authors use exact numerical simulations to analyze this system and discover a stable regime where the particles remain a roughly fixed distance apart. This correlated behavior, which depends on the interaction details and system boundaries, can lead to localized states or specific oscillatory motions and reduces the generation of entanglement. The findings demonstrate that quasiperiodically modulated interactions can create and control unique correlated quantum dynamics in few-body systems.|
|**2026-04-16**|**Minimal model of self-organized clusters with phase transitions in ecological communities**|Shing Yan Li, Mehran Kardar, Zhijie Feng, Washington Taylor|[[arxiv:2509.24985](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.24985)]|This paper addresses the problem of understanding how species can self-organize into distinct clusters within ecological communities, bridging concepts from neutral and niche theories. The method involves a minimal generalized Lotka-Volterra model where species compete with neighbors in niche space via a common interaction strength, deliberately avoiding random heterogeneity. The key results show that varying this single parameter generates an exponential number of steady states with diverse cluster patterns and triggers sharp, multiple phase transitions between these patterns, many accumulating near critical points. The significance lies in providing an analytically tractable, mechanistic model that captures complex community structure and phase transitions without relying on random interactions, offering a new lens for ecological theory.|

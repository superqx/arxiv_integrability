The significance lies in providing a unified, covariant framework for constructing and analyzing compact objects and strong-field regimes beyond General Relativity.|

Heeger, Stefano Martiniani|[[arxiv:2605.25278](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25278)]|The paper addresses the limitation of the Kac-Rice formula, which only provides the mean rate of level crossings and ignores the temporal correlation structure of the process. The authors derive exact analytical formulas for the variance and Fano factor of arbitrary level crossings in smooth stationary Gaussian processes. Key results show that oscillatory correlations cause sub-Poissonian statistics (crossings are regular), while non-oscillatory systems can produce super-Poissonian statistics (crossings cluster), and relaxational systems exhibit reentrant transitions between these behaviors based on threshold level. This work significantly advances beyond mean-rate analysis, enabling improved parameter estimation and model selection in fields relying on Gaussian processes, such as neuroscience and physics.|
|**2026-05-26**|**Dynamical susceptibility and quantum Fisher information in the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model with Hatsugai-Kohmoto interactions**|Sepide Mohamadi, Jahanfar Abouie|[[arxiv:2605.25231](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25231)]|This paper investigates dynamical spin and charge susceptibilities and quantum Fisher information in the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model with Hatsugai-Kohmoto interactions (SSH-HK), contrasting it with Hubbard-type models treated via random-phase approximation. Unlike standard interactions, the SSH-HK model shows distinct behavior due to the momentum-diagonal HK interaction, allowing derivation of closed-form expressions for susceptibility that reveal unique filling-controlled features. Key results include a finite response at zero wave vector, spectral weight restructuring across filling sectors, and the quantum Fisher information acting as an efficient probe that distinguishes integer from fractional fillings. The findings highlight the SSH-HK model as a valuable analytical platform for studying topology and strong correlations, while revealing that standard dynamical responses are insensitive to topological transitions in uniform-density regimes.|
|**2026-05-26**|**Strong Correlation Effect and In-gap State in the Doped Electron-Hole Two-Band Hubbard Model Based on the Dynamical Mean-Field Theory**|Masashi Akiyama, Yusuke Inokuma, Yoshiaki Ono|[[arxiv:2605.24927](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24927)]|This paper investigates the strong correlation effects in a spinless electron-hole two-band Hubbard model using dynamical mean-field theory. The study reveals that at half filling, increasing the interband Coulomb interaction \(U\) drives a first-order Lifshitz transition from a correlated semimetal to a band insulator at a critical \(U\) near the bandwidth. A key result is that slight hole doping of the band insulator for large \(U\) creates a remarkable correlated semimetal with a small renormalization factor \(Z \sim 0.1\), accompanied by the emergence of in-gap states whose weight grows with doping. The significance lies in demonstrating that doped band insulators in this model exhibit behavior similar to doped Mott insulators, providing insights into in-gap state formation.|
|**2026-05-26**|**Strong Eigenstate Thermalization from Mean-Ergodic Non-chaotic Dynamics**|Avadhut V. Purohit, Harshit Sharma, Udaysinh T. They find that diagonal matrix element fluctuations scale with Hilbert space dimension as D^{-1/2} and off-diagonal elements exhibit Gaussian statistics, hallmarks of strong ETH. The key result shows that strong ETH is possible even without chaotic dynamics, suggesting it may be a quantum analog of mean-ergodicity rather than chaos.|

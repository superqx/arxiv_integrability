|**2026-05-07**|**Expectation values after an integrable boundary quantum quench**|Zolt\'an Bajnok, D\'avid F\"ulepi, M\'at\'e Lencs\'es|[[arxiv:2605.04823](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04823)]|This paper investigates the real-time dynamics following a sudden switch between two integrable boundary conditions. The authors develop a general framework using form factors of bulk and boundary-changing operators to analyze the resulting quantum quench. They study the time evolution of the vacuum state in the conformal Lee-Yang model and its massive perturbation, computing the matrix elements of local operators. The analytical results are validated through numerical simulations, demonstrating the framework's effectiveness for integrable boundary quenches.|
|**2026-05-07**|**Exact solutions, trajectories and radiation patterns in the classical relativistic St\"{o}rmer problem**|Tiberiu Harko, Francisco S. N. Lobo|[[arxiv:2605.04790](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04790)]|This paper addresses the relativistic version of the Störmer problem, which models charged particle motion in a magnetic dipole field. The authors derive relativistic equations of motion and conservation laws, presenting an exact parametric solution for planar trajectories. Numerical simulations then explore particle dynamics across relativistic regimes, and the study calculates the resulting electromagnetic radiation, specifically the total power and frequency spectrum. The key finding is a distinct peak structure in the power spectral density, showing that the radiation contains preferred frequency bands, which characterizes magnetic dipole radiation from Störmer-like motion.|
|**2026-05-07**|**Hierarchical entanglement transitions and hidden area-law sectors in quantum many-body dynamics**|Tarun Grover|[[arxiv:2605.04540](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04540)]|This paper reveals a hierarchical entanglement structure in quantum many-body dynamics after local quenches. The authors identify a Renyi-index-tuned transition where, at long times, higher-order Renyi entropies (\(S_{\alpha>1}\)) obey an area law while lower-order ones (\(S_{\alpha \le 1}\)) are volume-law. They further find that the linear response to the quench is confined to a low-dimensional Schmidt sector, which itself exhibits its own area-to-volume-law transitions at critical indices. Using exact diagonalization of random circuits and chaotic spin chains, they demonstrate a "hidden" area-law sector and a universal classification of entanglement scaling, revealing a previously unknown layer of structure in quantum dynamics.|
|**2026-05-07**|**Spherical singularities in compactified Ruijsenaars--Schneider systems**|L. Feher, H. R. Dullin|[[arxiv:2604.18023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18023)]|The paper studies compactified trigonometric Ruijsenaars–Schneider systems, focusing on a class where globally continuous action variables exist only on a dense open subset. The authors analyze the fibers of the action map lying outside this domain, showing they are smooth, connected isotropic submanifolds. By modeling these fibers as quotient spaces of subgroups of SU(n), they identify fibers over singular vertices as diffeomorphic to S^3 (SU(2)) in certain cases. This work expands the known examples of Liouville integrable systems with spherical singularities.|
|**2026-05-07**|**Non-Commutative Gauge Theory at the Beach**|Roland Bittleston, Simon Heuveline, Surya Raghavendran, David Skinner|[[arxiv:2509.20643](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20643)]|This paper proposes a novel Lagrangian formulation for the KP equation, a key integrable system, by embedding it within a non-commutative five-dimensional Chern-Simons theory defined on the projective spinor bundle of spacetime. The construction relies on a 2-form pulled back from minitwistor space, and its dispersionless limit corresponds to a Poisson-Chern-Simons theory. A key result is that all tree-level amplitudes vanish, consistent with integrability, and the vertex algebra on a two-dimensional surface defect is \( W_{1+\infty} \), linking its operator products to collinear splitting functions. The significance lies in providing a higher-dimensional gauge-theoretic origin for the KP equation and connecting integrable systems to vertex algebras, with the dispersionless limit collapsing \( W_{1+\infty} \) to \( w_{1+\infty} \).|

The work is significant for connecting these polynomials to zonal spherical functions in a fermionic Fock space, with potential applications in quantum integrability and probability.|

Using mean-field analysis and perturbative calculations, the authors find that in the strong-Kitaev regime, the spin sector develops magnetic order while the orbital sector retains topological order. For a hybrid Yao-Lee and square-lattice model, they observe a rich evolution of Majorana Dirac bands and Lifshitz transitions, with exact solvability restored when the two couplings are equal and opposite. The significance lies in assessing which model better aligns with experimental observations, advancing understanding of pairing mechanisms in moiré materials.|

The key finding is that lattice reconstructions strongly modify on-site exciton interactions, while long-range interactions experience a crossover from repulsion to attraction due to anisotropic screening. The authors derive an imbalance-only Lagrangian with a position-dependent mass and quantize it, computing leading quantum corrections via a one-loop effective action method. Key results show explicit expressions for the quantum-corrected effective potential and mass, leading to a modified Josephson frequency. The significance lies in demonstrating that this imbalance-only formulation outperforms the phase-only approach for weak interactions, as validated by exact diagonalization of the Bose-Hubbard model.|

Dullin|[[arxiv:2604.18023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18023)]|This paper studies compactified trigonometric Ruijsenaars–Schneider systems, which are Liouville integrable systems on compact symplectic manifolds. The method involves analyzing the fibers of the action map in the component of the phase space where the Hamiltonian torus action is only densely defined. This theory provides a Lagrangian formulation of the KP equation, a classic integrable system, with its dispersionless limit governed by Poisson-Chern-Simons theory. Key results include the vanishing of all tree-level amplitudes and the identification of the universal vertex algebra on a surface defect as \(W_{1+\infty}\), whose operator products match collinear splitting functions.

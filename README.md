|**2026-06-10**|**Exceptional Points as Manifestations of Analyticity Breakdown in the 't Hooft Model**|Kejun Liu|[[arxiv:2606.10141](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10141)]|The paper investigates the breakdown of analyticity in causal response functions using the exactly solvable 't Hooft model of large-N QCD in 1+1 dimensions. By applying a PT-symmetric deformation (an imaginary chemical potential) to the meson operator, the authors drive the lowest two meson states to an exceptional point (EP), where the resolvent is recast as a Jacobi continued fraction to derive the threshold analytically. Key results include a closed-form expression for the critical deformation parameter, confirmation of the square-root exponent associated with the EP via Jordan form and finite-size scaling, and a clear time-domain signature—propagator norm growth transitioning from bounded to linear to exponential. This work provides the first analytically controlled demonstration of EP-induced analyticity breakdown in a confining gauge theory, with observable consequences in photonic and topolectrical analogues.|
|**2026-06-10**|**Analytic asymptotics of the integrable XXX critical spin chains in the small external-field limit from TBA**|Yizhuang Liu|[[arxiv:2310.15134](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.15134)]|This paper studies the asymptotic expansions of integrable XXX spin chains in the infrared (IR) limit at the anti-ferromagnetic critical point, using linear thermodynamic Bethe ansatz (TBA) equations. The method generates perturbative series in minimal-scheme coupling constants and, for spin one-half, relates these expansions to those of the massive sine-Gordon model via a sign flip of the coupling constant. Key results include the computation of perturbative coefficients for exponentially small corrections in higher spin cases (\(s>1\)), revealing that the largest such corrections survive while Borel singularities in the perturbative series are canceled for generic \(s>1\). The significance lies in partially supporting conjectured field-theoretical interpretations of these critical points and highlighting dramatic behavior in the ’t Hooft large \(s\) limit, where perturbative series converge but exponential corrections persist.|

|**2026-06-10**|**Exceptional Points as Manifestations of Analyticity Breakdown in the 't Hooft Model**|Kejun Liu|[[arxiv:2606.10141](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10141)]|The paper addresses the breakdown of analyticity in causal response functions within a confining gauge theory, using the exactly-solvable 't Hooft model in 1+1D large-\(N_c\) QCD as a testbed. The method involves introducing a PT-symmetric deformation to the meson operator, recasting the resolvent as a Jacobi continued fraction to analytically derive the exceptional point (EP) threshold where the lowest two mesons coalesce. Key results include a closed-form expression for the EP threshold at the two-pole level, a fixed square-root exponent \(\nu = 1/2\) from Jordan form analysis, and distinct time-domain signatures—bounded, linearly growing, and exponential propagator norms—across the transition. This work provides the first analytically controlled instance of EP-driven analyticity breakdown in a confining gauge theory, with implications for observable non-Hermitian phenomena in photonic and topolectrical analogues.|
|**2026-06-10**|**Symmetries and exact solutions of a reaction-diffusion system arising in population dynamics**|Philip Broadbridge, Roman Cherniha, Vasyl' Davydovych, Ian Marquette|[[arxiv:2412.13097](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.13097)]|The authors investigate a system of two cubic reaction-diffusion equations modeling gene frequencies in population dynamics. Using Lie and Q-conditional (nonclassical) symmetry methods, they identify all possible symmetries depending on coefficient values. They construct a wide range of new exact solutions, including those involving a Lambert function that cannot be derived from Lie symmetries alone. The work provides a practical algorithm for finding Q-conditional symmetries in general nonlinear evolution systems and demonstrates a novel real-world application of the model.|
|**2026-06-10**|**Analytic asymptotics of the integrable XXX critical spin chains in the small external-field limit from TBA**|Yizhuang Liu|[[arxiv:2310.15134](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.15134)]|This paper studies asymptotic expansions in the small external-field limit of integrable XXX spin chains at the antiferromagnetic critical point. Using the linear thermodynamic Bethe ansatz (TBA) integral equation, the authors generate perturbative series in minimal-scheme coupling constants. A key result shows that for spin one-half, the series relates to the massive sine-Gordon model via a sign flip of the coupling constant, with generalizations for arbitrary spin to certain deformations of WZW models. The significance lies in revealing that the largest exponentially-small corrections survive even in the large spin limit, where the perturbative series has a finite radius of convergence, leading to a cancellation of Borel singularities.|

|**2026-06-10**|**Thermal Signatures of the Slater-Mott Crossover in the Hubbard Model: From Double Occupancy to Antiferromagnetic Correlation Length**|Mingzhong Lu, Yu-Feng Song, Youjin Deng, Yuan-Yao He|[[arxiv:2606.10976](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10976)]|The paper establishes a thermal criterion for the Slater-Mott crossover in the Hubbard model by analyzing the sign change of the temperature derivative of double occupancy near zero temperature. Using exact diagonalization and quantum Monte Carlo simulations, the authors find that a positive derivative indicates Slater behavior dominated by charge fluctuations, while a negative derivative signals Mott behavior governed by spin superexchange. They determine the crossover boundary at U_cross/t = 4.0(2) and confirm it independently via a maximum in antiferromagnetic correlation length. The results provide a physically intuitive and experimentally accessible framework for identifying this crossover in optical lattice experiments.|

The authors derive explicit closed-form solutions for discrete coagulation-fragmentation equations with size-independent rates under monodisperse initial conditions. They also report exact solutions for continuous, three-particle, and multi-component generalizations. A key finding is that source-driven aggregation produces steady distributions with a universal $s^{-3/2}$ power-law decay and a cutoff mass scaling $s_{*} \sim t^{2}$ , highlighting the method's broad applicability.|

Key results show that viscoelastic memory induces fractional short-time transport and enhanced long-time persistence, with the memory kernel controlling anomalous scaling exponents and activity governing transitions between diffusive regimes. The work is significant as it provides a general theoretical framework for understanding the motility of biological and synthetic microswimmers in complex environments.|

A key result extends this to a ruby lattice construction and generates a whole family of solvable topological dimer liquids, including cascades beyond standard planar models. The significance lies in extending Kasteleyn methods to these non-planar graphs, allowing for controlled wave-function deformations and efficient correlation function calculations.|

They demonstrate that the approximation's validity is governed by the relationship between the time step and the system's relaxation times, and that detailed balance violations cause differences in dynamics despite identical stationary distributions. The work serves as a practical, solvable example for teaching the limitations of continuous-time models in stochastic processes.|

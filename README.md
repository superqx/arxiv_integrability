This work significantly reduces preprocessing computational resources, paving the way for more scalable digital-analog quantum simulation protocols.|

|**2026-06-04**|**Many body localization in Disordered One-Dimensional Fermi-Hubbard Model**|Harmanjeet Kaur, Vinod Ashokan|[[arxiv:2606.04948](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04948)]|This paper studies many-body localization (MBL) in the one-dimensional Fermi-Hubbard model by simulating its non-equilibrium dynamics after starting from a charge-density-wave state. The method involves tracking the evolution of charge and spin imbalances along with entanglement entropy as disorder strength is varied. Key results show a clear transition from fast thermalization and rapid entanglement growth at weak disorder to persistent imbalance and slow, logarithmic entanglement growth at strong disorder. The findings are significant because they demonstrate robust MBL signatures in this system and reveal distinct relaxation behaviors in the charge and spin sectors, highlighting the complex interplay between disorder and interactions.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Stabilizing the parquet problem**|Herbert E{\ss}l, Stefan Rohshap, Marcel Gievers, Markus Wallerberger, Alessandro Toschi, Anna Kauch|[[arxiv:2606.04936](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04936)]|This paper investigates convergence problems in the iterative solution of the parquet equations, a key method in quantum many-body physics. The authors analyze the Jacobian of the fixed-point iteration to determine when the physical solution becomes unstable, and they show that misleading convergence issues often arise even without divergences in the two-particle irreducible vertex. Building on this understanding, they introduce a controlled stabilization strategy that ensures convergence to the correct solution. The method is demonstrated on the zero-point model and the atomic-limit Hubbard model, enabling stable solutions deep in the non-perturbative regime.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Adiabatic Ramp Dynamics Across the ETH--MBL Transition in Disordered XXZ Spin Chain**|Nidhi Kumari, Vinod Ashokan|[[arxiv:2606.04926](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04926)]|This paper examines non-equilibrium dynamics across the many-body localization (MBL) transition in a disordered XXZ spin chain by adiabatically ramping interaction strength. Using exact diagonalization and time-dependent methods, the authors monitor diagonal entropy and entanglement entropy growth at different ramp speeds and system sizes. The key result shows that localized phases remain robust under slow ramps, while faster ramps generate greater excitations and entropy growth. This work highlights the strong sensitivity of adiabatic dynamics to driving rates in disordered interacting quantum systems.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Pushing the Classical Frontier of 1D Fermi-Hubbard Quench Dynamics Beyond Current Quantum Simulations**|Roman Rausch, Sukhbinder Singh, Saeed S. Jahromi, Augustine Kshetrimayum, Roman Orus|[[arxiv:2606.04771](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04771)]|This paper addresses the challenge of verifying quantum advantage claims in simulations of the one-dimensional Fermi-Hubbard model. By leveraging full U(1)×SU(2) symmetry and GPU-accelerated tensor contractions, the authors achieved converged classical simulations with bond dimensions up to ~62,000, far exceeding previous benchmarks. They successfully validated the previously unresolved high-entanglement regime and extended simulations beyond the quantum hardware experiment. The results reduce the claimed 3000× quantum speedup to roughly 36×, significantly narrowing the quantum-classical performance gap.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Monotonicity of eigenstate thermalization hypothesis in two-dimensional systems**|Nilakash Sorokhaibam, Anjan Daimari|[[arxiv:2510.25711](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25711)]|This study numerically investigates the envelope f-function of the fluctuation term in the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) for two-dimensional systems. The key finding is that this f-function generally increases monotonically with entropy and locally flattens as system size grows, with the flattening rate being proportional to both system size and particle number. This behavior is significant because it describes fluctuations at subleading order, making it relevant for experimentally accessible intermediate-size systems. A notable exception is that the f-function for the order parameter in a thermal phase transition does not follow this monotonic trend.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Entanglement-enhanced correlation propagation in the one-dimensional SU( $N$) Fermi-Hubbard model**|Mathias Mikkelsen, Ippei Danshita|[[arxiv:2510.25358](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25358)]|In this paper, the authors study how correlations spread in a one-dimensional SU($N$) Fermi-Hubbard model after suddenly reducing the interaction strength from a large value. They develop an approximate analytical model based on doublon excitations, revealing that for $N>2$, initial entanglement causes a collective enhancement of the propagation velocity, which approaches the Bose-Hubbard velocity in the large-$N$ limit. Numerical simulations for $N=2,3,4,6$ confirm these findings by tracking density-density correlations. The work demonstrates that internal symmetry degrees of freedom can significantly accelerate information and correlation spreading beyond simple fermionic or bosonic limits.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Comparing Symmetrized Determinant Neural Quantum States for the Hubbard Model**|Louis Sharma, Ahmedeo Shokry, Rajah Nutakki, Olivier Simard, Michel Ferrero, Filippo Vicentini|[[arxiv:2510.11710](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.11710)]|This paper addresses the challenge of achieving high numerical precision in simulations of the Hubbard model to understand strongly correlated electron phenomena. The authors compare two Neural Quantum State architectures—the Hidden Fermion Determinant State and a Vision Transformer-based Jastrow-Backflow ansatz—finding they yield similar accuracy, with output averaging being the most effective symmetrization strategy despite its computational cost for larger systems. Key results show that on cylindrical systems, the model consistently predicts filled stripes, while on the torus, it behaves like a doped Mott insulator with antiferromagnetic correlations and suppressed density fluctuations. The work highlights the potential and current limitations of neural quantum states for simulating correlated fermions.|
|**2026-06-04**|**Altermagnetism and Superconductivity: A Short Historical Review**|Zhao Liu, Hui Hu, Xia-Ji Liu|[[arxiv:2510.09170](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.09170)]|This review examines the deep connections between electronic liquid crystals, multipole expansions, and the newly identified magnetic phase called altermagnetism, finding that all three share a foundation in nonrelativistic spin-momentum locking. The authors explore how altermagnetism, which hosts anisotropic magnetization across sublattices, can give rise to a rich variety of unconventional superconducting states, including finite-momentum pairing and d-wave superconductivity. The paper discusses both the emergence of superconductivity from static altermagnetic order or fluctuations, as well as the competition between altermagnetism and superconductivity. Overall, this work provides an accessible introduction to altermagnetism while serving as a conceptual guide for harnessing its associated unconventional superconductivity in future quantum technologies.|

However, an exception is noted for the order parameter of a thermal phase transition, which does not follow this monotonic behavior. This variation of the f-function is significant for understanding subleading order physics in intermediate-sized quantum systems (hundreds of qubits) that are currently experimentally accessible.|

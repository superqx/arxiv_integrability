|**2026-05-20**|**Static four-charge squashed black hole in five-dimensional $STU-W^2U$ supergravity and its thermodynamics**|Di Wu, Shuang-Qing Wu|[[arxiv:2601.14315](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.14315)]|This paper presents a new exact solution in five-dimensional STU supergravity, describing a static, squashed black hole carrying four independent electric charges. The solution was derived using a specific prepotential and is asymptotically locally flat with a compact extra dimension. The authors computed thermodynamic quantities via a counterterm method and confirmed the first law and Bekenstein-Smarr mass formula when the compact dimension's length is treated as a thermodynamic variable. This work provides a simple expression for a multi-charge black hole and clarifies its thermodynamic consistency within an extended phase space framework.|

|**2026-05-20**|**Proof of the absence of local conserved quantities in the Holstein model**|Fuga Ishii, Mizuki Yamaguchi|[[arxiv:2605.19606](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19606)]|The paper addresses the problem of proving the absence of local conserved quantities, or nonintegrability, in electron-phonon coupled systems. The authors apply analytical methods to demonstrate that the one-dimensional Holstein model possesses no nontrivial local conserved quantities beyond the Hamiltonian and total fermion number. They further extend this proof to the more general Holstein-Hubbard model. This work advances nonintegrability proofs by including systems that mix particles with different statistical properties, which is significant for understanding thermalization and transport in quantum many-body systems.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|

|**2026-05-20**|**Band Structure and topology of a periodically deformed Kitaev honeycomb model**|Abdullah AlJishi, Ali AlSwaid, Moayad Ekhwan, Hocine Bahlouli, Raditya Weda Bomantara, Michael Vogl|[[arxiv:2605.20181](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20181)]|This paper investigates the effects of periodic deformation and magnetic fields on the honeycomb Kitaev model, a prototypical spin liquid system. Using a simplified solution to the undeformed model, the authors extend their analysis to a deformed case with hexagonal symmetry, finding that deformation shrinks the Brillouin zone and opens new band gaps. Introducing a magnetic field leads to multiple band-gap closings and openings, revealing nontrivial Chern numbers and numerous topological transitions. The results highlight the rich phenomenology at the intersection of deformation engineering and quantum spin liquids, suggesting potential thermal Hall or Nernst-type responses.|
|**2026-05-20**|**Controlled expansion for correlated electrons with concentrated kinematics**|Pavel A Nosov, Eslam Khalaf, Patrick Ledwith|[[arxiv:2605.20171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20171)]|This paper introduces a systematic expansion method for strongly correlated electron systems, controlled by a small parameter \(s^2\) that measures the area of momentum space where kinematic interactions are concentrated. The key result is that in the \(s^2 \ll 1\) limit, long, self-avoiding tunneling paths dominate, enabling controlled analytical calculations of response functions and finite DC transport. The method is applied to three models: a Hubbard model with concentrated dispersion, revealing a bad metal with \(T\)-linear resistivity and long-lived quasiparticles; a correlated-hopping model with electron-trion dynamics; and a Chern band model for twisted bilayer graphene, where it computes spectral broadening for a Mott semimetal. This approach provides a controlled framework to analytically address challenging problems in strongly correlated systems, including transport and spectral properties, with potential for broader generalizations.|
|**2026-05-20**|**Towards a Matrix Product Ansatz in Two Dimensions**|Chandraniva Guha Ray, Aikya Banerjee, P. K. Mohanty|[[arxiv:2605.20160](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20160)]|This paper introduces a matrix product ansatz (MPA) for two-dimensional non-equilibrium stochastic processes, extending a framework previously limited to one dimension. The authors apply the method to exactly solve a novel "assisted exclusion model" with constrained hopping and birth-death dynamics, deriving steady-state weights and observables like density moments and currents. A key result is that, in the particle-conserving limit, the system exhibits an absorbing phase transition at a critical density of 1/2 with an order-parameter exponent of 3. The work is significant because it provides a systematic exact-solution method for 2D interacting stochastic systems and links nonequilibrium dynamics to equilibrium hard-square lattice gases.|
|**2026-05-20**|**Non-equilibrium quantum dynamics of interacting integrable models by Monte Carlo sampling Lehmann representations**|Riccardo Senese, Fabian H. L. Essler|[[arxiv:2605.20065](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20065)]|This paper introduces a Monte Carlo sampling method to compute the Lehmann representation for time-dependent expectation values, addressing the challenge of simulating the dynamics of interacting integrable quantum systems after a quench. The method efficiently evaluates both the full Lehmann sum and Quench Action formalism, enabling access to larger system sizes and longer times than previous approaches. Key results show excellent agreement with exact solutions for non-interacting models and reproduce short-time behavior in weakly interacting systems, while also demonstrating the time evolution of the order parameter in the repulsive Lieb-Liniger model across various interaction strengths. This work provides a powerful numerical tool for studying non-equilibrium quantum dynamics in integrable systems, though it notes a potential "sign problem" for more complex correlators.|
|**2026-05-20**|**Proof of the absence of local conserved quantities in the Holstein model**|Fuga Ishii, Mizuki Yamaguchi|[[arxiv:2605.19606](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19606)]|The paper addresses the problem of proving nonintegrability—the absence of local conserved quantities—in electron-phonon coupled systems, which is crucial for understanding thermalization and transport but lacks a formal proof. The authors develop a method to analytically show that the one-dimensional Holstein model has no nontrivial local conserved quantities beyond the Hamiltonian and total fermion number operator. They extend this result to the more general Holstein-Hubbard model. The significance lies in advancing nonintegrability proofs to systems mixing particles with different statistics, providing a rigorous foundation for studying dynamics in condensed matter physics.|
|**2026-05-20**|**Exact Criterion for Ground-State Overlap Dominance after Quantum Quenches**|Taisanul Haque|[[arxiv:2604.11420](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11420)]|The paper investigates the "ground-state overlap dominance" conjecture, which claims that after a quantum quench within the same phase, the initial ground state most strongly overlaps with the final ground state. Using free-fermion systems with independent momentum sectors, the authors derive an exact criterion requiring the dot product of initial and final Bloch vectors to be positive at every momentum. They find this condition can fail even within the same phase, as demonstrated in Kitaev chains where excited states dominate the overlap. This result overturns the phase-based conjecture and links the failure to dynamical quantum phase transitions, which can occur without crossing an equilibrium phase boundary.|
|**2026-05-20**|**Topology of the Fermi surface and universality of the metal-metal and metal-insulator transitions: $d$ -dimensional Hatsugai-Kohmoto model as an example**|Gennady Y. Chitov|[[arxiv:2602.13050](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.13050)]|The paper advances a theory of quantum phase transitions driven by changes in Fermi surface topology (FST), introducing the order parameter \(\mathcal{P}\) (the d-volume of the Fermi sea) and a special FST universality class. Using the exactly solvable Hatsugai-Kohmoto (HK) model in \(d=1,2,3\) dimensions, the authors confirm the Luttinger theorem and show that this FST universality class governs transitions between metals and band/Mott insulators, as well as Lifshitz and van Hove gapless-to-gapless transitions. Key results include the classification of gapless phases as Landau Fermi liquids and the quantification of FST via the Euler characteristic, with homology theory identifying critical points of a Morse function. The significance lies in demonstrating that the FST universality class is robust against interactions and model details, provided critical points are non-degenerate, thus offering a unified framework for metal-metal and metal-insulator transitions.|

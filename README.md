|**2026-05-22**|**Moving Boundary Problems for a Cuspon Equation and Reciprocal Associates: Exact Solution via Painleve' Symmetry Reduction**|Colin Rogers, Sandra Carillo|[[arxiv:2605.22565](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22565)]|This paper addresses Stefan-type moving boundary problems for a nonlinear equation from cuspon theory and its reciprocally linked solitonic counterparts. The method employs Painlevé II symmetry reduction to transform these free-boundary problems into tractable forms. Key results include the explicit derivation of exact similarity solutions that satisfy both the governing equations and the moving boundary conditions. The significance lies in demonstrating that integrable systems beyond the standard soliton hierarchy can yield closed-form solutions to practical, moving-boundary problems.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Aggregation-Fragmentation Processes with Broken Detailed Balance**|P. L. Krapivsky|[[arxiv:2605.22383](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22383)]|This paper studies aggregation-fragmentation processes where reaction rates are mass-independent, leading to broken detailed balance except in a special case. The authors derive exact solutions for the steady-state mass distribution using Laplace transforms for a class of models. Key results show that for non-negative fragmentation rate exponents (β ≥ 0), steady states behave similarly to the mass-independent case, while negative exponents (β < 0) cause an instantaneous shattering transition with continuous mass loss. This work provides insight into nonequilibrium steady states in systems where detailed balance does not hold, advancing understanding of cluster dynamics beyond traditional equilibrium frameworks.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Semi-global symplectic invariant of the champagne bottle**|Ognyan Christov|[[arxiv:2605.21985](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21985)]|This paper investigates a completely integrable Hamiltonian system with a champagne bottle potential, which lacks global action variables due to nontrivial monodromy. The authors compute the Birkhoff normal form and the nontrivial action near the focus-focus singularity to derive the semi-global symplectic invariant introduced by Vũ Ngọc. A key result is the explicit calculation of this invariant for the champagne bottle, which is then compared with the known invariant for the spherical pendulum. This work is significant because it provides a rare explicit example of such an invariant, deepening the understanding of symplectic geometry near singularities in integrable systems.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Perfect fluid equations with nonrelativistic conformal symmetry: Exact solutions**|Anton Galajinsky|[[arxiv:2604.03621](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03621)]|This paper uses a group-theoretic method to find exact solutions for perfect fluid equations that obey nonrelativistic conformal symmetries, such as the Schrödinger, l-conformal Galilei, and Lifshitz groups. The resulting velocity fields resemble the Bjorken flow from relativistic hydrodynamics. A key result is that by adjusting parameters like l, it is possible to achieve arbitrarily high density and pressure for a brief time. This work demonstrates the power of symmetry-based approaches for constructing analytical fluid solutions with potential applications in nonrelativistic contexts.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Birational Weyl Group Action on the Symplectic Groupoid and Cluster Algebras**|Woojin Choi|[[arxiv:2601.18636](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18636)]|The problem is to understand a birational Weyl group action on Bondal's symplectic groupoid of triangular bilinear forms, which induces a Poisson structure on unipotent upper-triangular matrices. The method involves constructing this action via cluster transformations associated with cycles of the \(\mathcal{A}_n\)-quiver, and applying a cluster Poisson reduction based on a rank condition. Key results include proving that the Poisson subalgebra of Weyl group invariants is a finite central extension of the matrix entry algebra, and that the longest Weyl group element corresponds to a cluster DT-transformation for even \(n\). The significance lies in determining the exact image of Song's embedding of the \(\imath\)-quantum group into the quantum cluster algebra, and in showing that irreducible components of the rank-constrained solution set are conjugate under the Weyl group, enabling a unified description of the cluster structure.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-05-22**|**Aggregation-Fragmentation Processes with Broken Detailed Balance**|P. L. Krapivsky|[[arxiv:2605.22383](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22383)]|This paper addresses aggregation-fragmentation processes where detailed balance is broken, meaning the system operates out of equilibrium. The researchers solve exactly for the steady state using a Laplace transform for models with constant aggregation and fragmentation rates, but then focus on cases where fragmentation rates scale as mass to a power \(\beta\). They find that detailed balance only holds when \(\beta=1\), and for \(\beta \geq 0\), the steady states resemble those of the mass-independent case. A key result is the discovery of an instantaneous shattering transition with continuous mass loss when \(\beta<0\), which is significant for understanding nonequilibrium phase behavior in such systems.|
|**2026-05-22**|**The exact solution of the Koga-Widom-Indekeu model and related models of wetting in fluid mixtures**|A. O. Parry, C. Rasc\'on|[[arxiv:2605.22084](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22084)]|The paper addresses the problem of exactly solving two-component square-gradient models for wetting in fluid mixtures, focusing on the Koga-Widom-Indekeu (KWI) model. The authors use complex analysis and algebraic curve theory to derive exact density profiles and surface tensions, confirming the absence of critical point wetting predicted in earlier numerical studies. Key results include the precise mapping of microscopic profiles to macroscopic drop shapes and the identification of a universality principle linking critical point wetting to underlying symmetry—present with Ising-like criticality but absent with local XY symmetry. This resolves contradictions in prior research and has implications for both theoretical wetting transitions and experimental fluid mixtures.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Conductivity of a Non-Galilean--Invariant Fermi Liquid: Exact Solution of the Kinetic Equation**|Tatia Kiliptari, Vladimir I. Yudson, Dmitrii L. Maslov|[[arxiv:2605.21774](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21774)]|This paper exactly solves the kinetic equation to calculate the conductivity of a disordered, non-Galilean-invariant Fermi liquid with screened Coulomb and critical interactions. The key result is that electron-electron interactions affect conductivity only through the quasiparticle scattering time, \(\tau_\mathrm{ee}\), with crossovers between collisionless and hydrodynamic regimes governed by the larger of the impurity scattering rate or probe frequency \(\Omega\). The solution also captures the optical response in the hydrodynamic regime inaccessible by perturbation theory. A significant finding near a \(z=3\) quantum critical point is that consistency with the Kubo approach requires mass renormalization, ensuring the collisionless-to-hydrodynamic crossover in optical conductivity occurs at the Planckian scale \(\Omega \sim T\).|
|**2026-05-22**|**Robust fluctuating intertwined charge stripes in the Emery model**|Rong Zhang, Sijia Zhao, Hong-Chen Jiang, Brian Moritz, Edwin W. Huang, Thomas P. Devereaux|[[arxiv:2605.21585](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21585)]|This paper investigates charge stripes in the Emery model, a multi-band system that is not yet as well understood as the single-band Hubbard model. Using DMRG and DQMC methods, the authors confirm the presence of oxygen-centered charge stripes with reduced amplitude compared to the Hubbard model. Key results show that charge density modulation is stronger on specific oxygen orbitals, and at low temperatures, the system exhibits unidirectional spin and charge stripes with dominant \( B_{1g} \) nematic susceptibility. The significance lies in establishing a connection between nematicity and charge stripes in the Emery model, providing a more comprehensive understanding of intertwined orders in strongly correlated electron systems.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Equilibrium and dynamical quantum phase transitions in dipolar atomic Josephson junctions**|Cesare Vianello, Giovanni Mazzarella, Luca Salasnich|[[arxiv:2602.20322](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20322)]|This paper studies a dipolar atomic Josephson junction, where dipolar interactions lead to nearest-neighbor pair tunneling. Using mean-field theory and exact diagonalization, the authors investigate how this process alters the system's equilibrium and dynamical properties. They find that pair tunneling reshapes the phase diagram, modifies quantum phase transitions to NOON states, and shifts critical points, while also affecting macroscopic self-trapping and causing dynamical quantum phase transitions. The work highlights the importance of correlated tunneling in dipolar systems for both equilibrium and non-equilibrium quantum behavior.|

Using Birkhoff normal forms, the authors compute the nontrivial action and the semi-global symplectic invariant near the focus-focus singularity. This provides a rare explicit calculation of this invariant, which is compared to the known result for the spherical pendulum. The work is significant for deepening the understanding of integrable systems lacking global action variables due to monodromy.|
|**2026-05-22**|**Collective coordinate descriptions of a kink in a driven-damped $\phi^4$ model**|Jacek Gatlik, Tomasz Dobrowolski, Jean-Guy Caputo, Panayotis G. By systematically comparing these models to full numerical solutions under space- and time-dependent perturbations, they find that the model incorporating both position and width agrees best. Its key significance lies in accurately capturing complex kink dynamics when the driving frequency is moderate, offering a practical description for driven-damped systems.|

|**2026-05-13**|**Magnetized dynamical black holes**|Jibril Ben Achour, Adolfo Cisterna, Amaro D\'iaz, Keanu M\"uller|[[arxiv:2601.08628](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08628)]|This paper introduces a new exact solution to the Einstein-scalar-Maxwell equations that models a black hole in a dynamic universe with a time-dependent external magnetic field. The method involves dressing a Schwarzschild black hole with a scalar field and applying a symmetry transformation to incorporate the electromagnetic field's backreaction. A key result is that the time-dependence of the spacetime can cloak curvature singularities that would otherwise be naked in a static version of the solution. This work is significant because it provides a more realistic, analytically tractable model for studying black holes in cosmologically relevant, time-varying electromagnetic environments, with potential applications to primordial black holes.|

|**2026-05-13**|**Optical Response of a screw dislocated GaAs Quantum Wire: Temperature and Pressure Effects**|Vinod Kumar, Shweta Kumari, Surender Pratap|[[arxiv:2605.12371](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12371)]|This paper investigates how a screw dislocation affects the optical properties of a parabolic GaAs quantum wire under varying temperature, hydrostatic pressure, and magnetic field. The study uses a torsion-modified metric and obtains exact wavefunction solutions using Whittaker functions. Key results show that the dislocation breaks symmetry between opposite angular momentum transitions, causing a redshift and amplitude suppression for the m=0→+1 transition and a blueshift with peak enhancement for the m=0→−1 transition, while temperature and pressure further tune these effects. This work highlights the potential of screw dislocations as a mechanism for controlling nonlinear optical responses in quantum wires.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Observation of sine-Gordon-like solitons in a spinor Bose-Einstein condensate**|Yannick Deller, Alexander Schmutz, Raphael Sch\"afer, Alexander Flamm, Florian Schmitt, Ido Siovitz, Thomas Gasenzer, Panayotis G. Kevrekidis, Helmut Strobel, Markus K. Oberthaler|[[arxiv:2605.11861](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11861)]|This paper addresses the challenge of generating and studying solitons in a highly controllable quantum system. The researchers experimentally created sine-Gordon-like solitons in a spinor Bose-Einstein condensate using a local phase-imprinting method, with soliton velocity tunable via the quadratic Zeeman shift. Key results include the observation of elastic collisions between solitons and a quantitative measurement of their spatial phase shift, matching numerical simulations. This work establishes spinor BECs as a powerful platform for exploring the dynamics of integrable sine-Gordon models.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Relationship between doping-induced in-gap states and spin excitations in Kitaev-Hubbard models**|Si-Qi Hou, Shun-Li Yu, Zhao-Yang Dong, Jian-Xin Li|[[arxiv:2605.11830](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11830)]|This study investigates how doping-induced in-gap states reflect underlying spin excitations in Mott insulators, using cluster perturbation theory on one-dimensional Kitaev-Hubbard models. By tuning Kitaev-like hopping terms to control spin anisotropies, the authors show a direct correspondence between the kinetic dispersion of in-gap states and the spin excitation spectra, with gaps scaling consistently (e.g., \(2t^{\prime 2}/U\) for the Ising spin gap). In two-leg ladders, a broad continuum of in-gap states emerges, reflecting spin excitation fractionalization and signaling topological \(Z_2\) visons. The findings establish that in-gap states can serve as spectroscopic probes of exotic quantum spin phenomena, such as fractionalization and topological order, providing a new route to study spin liquids through charge excitations.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Altermagnons at the metal-insulator transition**|Jonas Issing, Matteo D\"urrnagel, Sarbajit Mazumdar, Alena Lorenz, Niklas Witt, Giorgio Sangiovanni, Michael Klett, Lennart Klebl, Ronny Thomale, Jannis Seufert|[[arxiv:2605.11669](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11669)]|This paper theoretically investigates the spin dynamics of altermagnets as they transition from a metal to a Mott insulator. Using slave-boson theory on a Hubbard model for a checkerboard lattice, the authors calculate dynamical spin susceptibilities. They identify a crossover from chirality-dependent magnon damping to coherent, but distorted, chiral magnon modes. This work provides a quantitative framework for understanding collective spin excitations in strongly correlated altermagnetic materials.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Layer-antisymmetric pair-phase resonance at the bonding-antibonding splitting in the AA-stacked bilayer attractive Hubbard model**|Yogeshwar Prasad|[[arxiv:2605.10387](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10387)]|The paper investigates the collective pair-phase dynamics in an AA-stacked bilayer attractive-Hubbard model, focusing on the layer-antisymmetric phase mode. Using Gaussian fluctuation theory and Bogoliubov-de-Gennes calculations, the authors show that this mode resonates at twice the interlayer hopping energy, corresponding to the bonding-antibonding band splitting, rather than the interaction-driven Leggett mode frequency. A key result is that the resonance scale is fixed by single-particle hybridization, and the analytical identity is verified numerically across different chemical potentials. The significance lies in identifying a Raman-forbidden, layer-antisymmetric excitation that can be probed via layer-imbalance drives, with potential applications in cold-atom systems at sub-kilohertz scales.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Incipient magnetic instability in RuO $_2$ with random phase approximation**|Diana Csontosov\'a, Kyo-Hoon Ahn, Jan Kune\v{s}|[[arxiv:2603.28355](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28355)]|This paper investigates magnetic instabilities in RuO₂ using a three-orbital Hubbard model analyzed with Hartree-Fock and random phase approximation methods. The study finds that spin susceptibility is the dominant response, leading to commensurate altermagnetic order in the stoichiometric system at low temperatures, while incommensurate wave vectors appear at higher temperatures or with hole doping. By analyzing band splitting from a staggered Weiss field, the authors clarify the qualitative distinction between altermagnets and antiferromagnets. The significance lies in identifying the conditions and nature of magnetic ordering in RuO₂ without spin-orbit coupling.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Correlation lengths of flat-band superconductivity from quantum geometry**|S. S. Elden, M. Iskin|[[arxiv:2601.12969](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12969)]|This paper investigates superconducting length scales in flat-band systems, where pairing is driven by interactions and quantum geometry rather than kinetic energy. Using two-band Hubbard models like the Creutz ladder and χ lattice, the authors show that the two-body bound-state size and the average Cooper-pair size are controlled by the quantum metric and remain finite and small, even in the weak-coupling limit. In contrast, the coherence length diverges in the dilute limit and near insulating regimes. The findings establish that pair size and coherence length are fundamentally distinct in flat-band superconductors, emphasizing quantum geometry's crucial role.|
|**2026-05-13**|**Quantum criticality and nonequilibrium dynamics on a Lieb lattice of Rydberg atoms**|Mark R. Hirsbrunner, Milan Kornja\v{c}a, Rhine Samajdar, Siva Darbha, Majd Hamdan, Jan Balewski, Ermal Rrapaj, Sheng-Tao Wang, Daan Camps, Fangli Liu, Pedro L. S. The researchers map out the system’s phase diagram, identifying density-wave phases—including one stabilized solely by quantum fluctuations—and demonstrate good agreement between theory and experiment. They then discover a quantum analog of the classical liquid-vapor transition between two density-wave phases and observe anomalously slow relaxation dynamics after quantum quenches, consistent with an emergent string phase. The findings highlight how geometric control in neutral-atom simulators can access complex phases, metastability, and nonequilibrium dynamics, advancing programmable quantum matter.|

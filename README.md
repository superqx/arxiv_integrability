|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|

|**2026-04-20**|**Exact solution of two-dimensional Palatini Gauss-Bonnet theory on a strip**|M\'aximo Ba\~nados, Marc Henneaux|[[arxiv:2604.15471](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15471)]|This paper addresses the two-dimensional Palatini formulation of Gauss-Bonnet gravity on a spacetime strip, a theory that lacks bulk degrees of freedom and is entirely governed by its boundaries. The method involves a Hamiltonian analysis, revealing that the physical phase space corresponds to the cotangent bundle of the SL(2,R) group manifold, constrained by a quadratic condition. A key result is that with a trivial boundary Hamiltonian, the classical dynamics describe geodesic motion on SL(2,R), where the coupling constant of the theory sets an effective mass. The significance lies in providing an exactly solvable model that elucidates the nature of boundary symmetries in gravitational theories, with potential insights for quantization.|
|**2026-04-20**|**Groenewold-Moyal twists, integrable spin-chains and AdS/CFT**|Riccardo Borsato, Miguel Garc\'ia Fern\'andez|[[arxiv:2604.07291](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07291)]|This paper investigates the spectral problem in AdS/CFT correspondences deformed by Groenewold-Moyal twists. The method involves analyzing a twisted, coupled spin-chain model, which is shown to have a Hamiltonian that can be rendered diagonalizable in a specific basis, revealing a deformed spectrum calculated via the Baxter equation. A key result is the matching, at leading order, of the deformed spin-chain ground state energy with a conserved charge from a corresponding deformed string theory solution. The significance lies in advancing the integrability approach for these non-local deformations, demonstrating a non-standard, non-local conserved charge on the string side that is nevertheless consistent with the structure of integrable models.|
|**2026-04-20**|**Sine-Liouville gravity as a Vertex Model on Planar Graphs**|Ivan Kostov|[[arxiv:2512.18916](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.18916)]|This paper studies a seven-vertex model on planar graphs, a generalization of the six-vertex model where loop dynamics are coupled to the local lattice geometry. The authors derive explicit formulas for the model's partition functions and show its continuum limit corresponds to sine-Liouville gravity. A key finding is that this vertex model and Matrix Quantum Mechanics provide two complementary, non-perturbative realizations of this gravitational theory, describing different types of branes. The work establishes a gravitational analogue of a massless sine-Gordon flow, connecting phases described by a free boson on circles of different radii coupled to Liouville gravity.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-20**|**Fluctuating Pair Density Wave in Finite-temperature Phase Diagram of the $t$-$t^\prime$ Hubbard Model**|Qiaoyi Li, Yang Qi, Wei Li|[[arxiv:2604.16293](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16293)]|This paper investigates the competing electronic phases in the doped Hubbard model, a key model for high-temperature superconductors. Using advanced thermal tensor network simulations, the authors map the model's phase diagram as a function of temperature and doping. A key finding is a stark asymmetry: while electron-doped regions show conventional d-wave superconductivity, the hole-doped side exhibits no robust superconductivity but is instead dominated by strong, finite-temperature pair-density-wave (PDW) fluctuations with non-zero momentum pairing. This result is significant as it identifies fluctuating PDW as a primary instability in the pseudogap regime, offering a new perspective on the enigmatic hole-doped cuprates.|
|**2026-04-20**|**On the role of the slowest observable in one-dimensional Markov processes to construct quasi-exactly-solvable generators with $N=2$ explicit levels**|Cecile Monthus|[[arxiv:2604.15908](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15908)]|This paper addresses the challenge of constructing quasi-exactly-solvable quantum models where only the two lowest energy eigenstates are explicitly known. The method reinterprets these quantum Hamiltonians through the lens of one-dimensional Markov processes, using the slowest decaying observable as the central object for reconstruction. The key result is that this Markovian perspective provides a more intuitive and technically simpler framework for building these models. The significance lies in offering a unified approach applicable to both continuous Fokker-Planck and discrete lattice Markov jump processes.|
|**2026-04-20**|**Exact Analysis of a One-Dimensional Yang-Gaudin Model with Two-Body Loss**|Ryutaro Katsuta, Shun Uchino|[[arxiv:2604.15595](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15595)]|This paper addresses how two-body particle loss affects the exact solvability and dynamics of a one-dimensional quantum gas (the Yang-Gaudin model). The method involves mapping the dissipative system's Liouvillian spectrum to a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian with a complex interaction. A key result is that the two-body problem admits exact steady states for bosons, while in larger many-body systems, dissipation fundamentally inverts the stability of magnetic order—favoring antiferromagnetic-like states in bosons but ferromagnetic-like states in fermions. This work is significant for demonstrating exact solvability in open quantum integrable systems and revealing how dissipation can engineer opposite magnetic correlations in bosonic versus fermionic gases.|
|**2026-04-20**|**Singlet-only always-on gapless exchange (SAGE) spin qubits: Charge noise effects and two-qubit gates**|Nathan L. Foulk, Katharina Laubscher, Silas Hoffman, Sankar Das Sarma|[[arxiv:2604.15419](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15419)]|The paper addresses the challenge of charge noise sensitivity in singlet-only always-on gapless exchange (SAGE) spin qubits, which, while protected from magnetic noise, are vulnerable to electrical fluctuations. Using a Hubbard model, the authors analyze the impact of 1/f charge noise on both idle coherence and two-qubit gate fidelity. Key results show that coherence times can be significantly extended using CPMG-like pulse sequences, and two-qubit gate errors can be mitigated through refocusing techniques and optimized pulse ramps. This work is significant as it demonstrates practical strategies to enhance the performance of SAGE qubits, advancing their potential as robust, exchange-only qubits for quantum computing.|
|**2026-04-20**|**Groenewold-Moyal twists, integrable spin-chains and AdS/CFT**|Riccardo Borsato, Miguel Garc\'ia Fern\'andez|[[arxiv:2604.07291](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07291)]|This paper addresses the spectral problem in AdS/CFT dualities deformed by Groenewold-Moyal twists, focusing on a twisted spin-chain model. The method involves analyzing the spin-chain Hamiltonian, which exhibits a Jordan-block structure in one basis but becomes diagonalizable with a deformed spectrum in another, and using the Baxter equation to compute perturbative energies. A key result is the matching, at leading order, of the spin-chain ground state energy with a non-local conserved charge from a deformed string theory solution. The significance lies in extending integrability techniques to these non-commutative deformations, revealing novel non-local charges and advancing the understanding of deformed holographic correspondences.|
|**2026-04-20**|**Spin-spiral instability of the Nagaoka ferromagnet in the crossover between square and triangular lattices**|Darren Pereira, Erich J. Mueller|[[arxiv:2510.05226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05226)]|This paper investigates how geometric frustration influences magnetic ordering in a doped Hubbard model as the lattice structure transitions from square to triangular. The authors analyze the instability of the Nagaoka ferromagnetic state—stable on a square lattice with a single hole—when lattice geometry is varied. Their key result is the exact determination of the critical point where ferromagnetism becomes unstable to a spin-spiral order induced by frustration. This work is significant for understanding the fundamental competition between kinetic-driven ferromagnetism and geometrically frustrated magnetic states in strongly correlated systems.|

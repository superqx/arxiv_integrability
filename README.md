|**2026-06-03**|**Almost local integrable models from supersymmetry algebras**|Somnath Maity, Pramod Padmanabhan, Jarmo Hietarinta, Vladimir Korepin|[[arxiv:2508.04315](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.04315)]|This paper extends a previous algebraic construction of constant Yang-Baxter solutions from supersymmetry algebras to obtain invertible braid operators. The resulting operators satisfy relations like those in the Iwahori-Hecke and Birman-Murakami-Wenzl algebras, enabling the Baxterization of these constant solutions into spectral parameter-dependent \(R\)-matrices. Depending on the representation of the SUSY generators, these \(R\)-matrices are either regular or "almost" regular, the latter being non-invertible yet still suitable for constructing integrable models. The key result is the systematic derivation of new integrable models, including both nearest-neighbor spin-\(\frac{1}{2}\) systems and their higher-spin analogs, with Hamiltonian densities that can be local or non-local based on the representation chosen.|

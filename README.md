Matveev|[[arxiv:2604.03195](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03195)]|This paper addresses the problem of classifying and constructing finite-dimensional integrable systems with quadratic integrals, specifically the long-standing Eisenhart–Stäckel problem for non-diagonal cases. The method involves studying Frobenius algebras of operator fields and introducing a new concept of duality for these algebras. The key result is that under certain conditions, the dual operator fields also form mutual symmetries, enabling the systematic construction of new infinite-dimensional integrable hydrodynamic systems. The significance lies in providing a complete solution to the Eisenhart–Stäckel problem for any dimension and Segre characteristic, describing all nondegenerate systems with commuting quadratic integrals.|
|**2026-04-06**|**Scattering of TE and TM waves by inhomogeneities of a 2D material, low-frequency behavior of the scattering amplitude, and low-frequency invisibility**|Farhang Loran, Ali Mostafazadeh|[[arxiv:2604.03149](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03149)]|This paper addresses the problem of how two-dimensional (2D) materials scatter electromagnetic waves, specifically analyzing the low-frequency behavior of the scattering amplitude and the conditions for invisibility. The method involves developing a dynamical formulation of stationary scattering, introducing a fundamental transfer matrix operator that admits a Dyson series expansion, and using this to construct a power series expansion for the scattering amplitude. The key results include deriving analytic expressions for the leading terms of this expansion and demonstrating a low-frequency cloaking scheme that works for both transverse electric (TE) and transverse magnetic (TM) waves. The significance lies in providing a unified theoretical framework for low-frequency scattering and invisibility in 2D materials, with direct applications to analogous acoustic wave systems.|

This work is significant as it initiates a geometric interpretation for open boundary integrable systems, connecting them to $q$ -opers and broadening the scope of the geometric Langlands correspondence.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-04-06**|**Semiclassical representation of the Hubbard model**|Yuki Yamasaki, Hidemaro Suwa, Cristian D. Batista, Shintaro Hoshino|[[arxiv:2604.02769](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02769)]|This paper addresses the challenge of developing a nonperturbative theoretical method for the Hubbard model, a fundamental model of strongly correlated electrons. The authors introduce a semiclassical approximation using an unconventional coherent-state representation, where some dynamical variables are treated as static, enabling application at finite temperatures and for systems with intersite correlations. Key results show that this method qualitatively reproduces exact solutions for small systems, capturing behaviors like double occupancy and spin correlations, though quantitative deviations occur due to the continuous density of states approximation. The significance lies in providing a versatile, nonperturbative framework that can be extended to multiorbital systems, offering new insights into the model's representation beyond conventional perturbative approaches.|
|**2026-04-06**|**Transport and Temperature 1: Exact spectrum and resistivity for the one-dimensional infinite- $U$ Hubbard model**|Shuo Liu, Yuhao Ma, Hitesh J. Changlani, Philip W. Phillips, B. Andrei Bernevig|[[arxiv:2604.02426](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02426)]|This paper addresses the challenge of explaining the linear-in-temperature resistivity observed in strange metals by studying a simplified, strongly correlated model. The authors employ an exact analytical method to solve the one-dimensional infinite-interaction Hubbard model in the dilute limit, deriving explicit expressions for the energy spectrum and the charge Drude weight. Their key result is the identification of a linear-in-temperature correction to the Drude weight, which, when regularized, leads to an effective linear-in-T resistivity. This finding is significant as it provides a rare, exact analytical demonstration of strange-metal-like transport from a microscopic model, offering a potential foundation for understanding this phenomenon in more complex two-dimensional systems.|
|**2026-04-06**|**Weiner's theory for exactly solvable Schr\"odinger equation with symmetric double well potential**|A. E. Sitnitsky|[[arxiv:2603.01573](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01573)]|This paper addresses the need for a more accurate theoretical model to calculate proton transfer rates, particularly for transitions governed by quantum tunneling. The method develops a modified version of Weiner's theory (mWT) by applying it to an exactly solvable Schrödinger equation with a symmetric trigonometric double-well potential, which avoids severe approximations of the original approach. The key result is an analytic formula for the proton transfer rate, demonstrated by calculating the rate constant for the proton-bound ammonia dimer cation, successfully capturing the transition from thermally activated to quantum tunneling regimes. The significance of this work is that it provides a more precise and computationally accessible framework for modeling proton transfer and vibrationally enhanced tunneling in chemical systems.|

Using free probability theory, the authors derive analytical expressions for subleading corrections to matrix-element correlations, improving the standard ETH ansatz. The method links statistical properties from random basis transformations to empirical energy-window averages in a single system. The findings are validated with numerical simulations in non-integrable Floquet systems, demonstrating that LRI provides a more precise characterization of thermalization in quantum many-body systems.|

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation by mapping it to Heun equations for both linear and circular (rotating-wave approximation) driving cases. They establish a direct analytic link between these exact solutions through a perturbative procedure involving hypergeometric functions, continued fractions, and consistency conditions. This connection not only recovers the rotating-wave approximation but also correctly yields Stark and Bloch-Siegert shifts, demonstrating deeper mathematical relationships between driving regimes.|
|**2026-07-02**|**Yangian Doubles and off-Shell Bethe Vectors**|A. Liashyk, S. Pakuliak, E. Ragoucy|[[arxiv:2607.00594](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00594)]|The paper develops a unified construction of off-shell Bethe vectors for integrable models invariant under any classical Lie algebra, using the algebraic framework of Yangian doubles. The method expresses Bethe vectors in terms of currents from these Yangian doubles and verifies they satisfy the same recursive and eigenvalue properties previously established for $\mathfrak{gl}_n$ models. The key result is the explicit demonstration that these vectors fulfill the defining relations necessary for proving rectangular recurrence and on-shell eigenvalue properties. This work significantly extends the algebraic Bethe ansatz to all classical series, providing a foundation for analyzing integrability across a broader class of models.|
|**2026-07-02**|**The half-wave maps equation on $\mathbb{T}$: Global well-posedness in $H^{1/2}$ and almost periodicity**|Patrick G\'erard, Enno Lenzmann|[[arxiv:2602.20895](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20895)]|This paper studies the half-wave maps equation on a one-dimensional periodic domain, aiming to establish global well-posedness for initial data in the critical energy space \(H^{1/2}\). The authors construct a unique continuous flow map for such data by extending from rational initial data, using an explicit formula derived from the equation’s Lax pair structure on a Hardy space. Key results include the almost periodicity in time of all solutions, with quasi-periodicity and uniform \(H^s\) bounds for rational data. The work’s significance lies in its development of a stability principle for Lax pair formulae, which is applicable to other integrable PDEs, and its generalization to matrix-valued systems on Grassmannian manifolds.|
|**2026-07-02**|**Subspace Selected Variational Quantum Configuration Interaction with a Partial Walsh Series**|Koray Aydo\u{g}an, Anna R. Spak, Kade Head-Marsden, Anthony W. The method can solve both full and selected CI problems, enabling exact or near-exact electronic ground-state calculations without requiring costly classical matrix diagonalizations. Key results demonstrate successful application to several molecules on quantum simulators and hardware. This approach offers a scalable and hardware-efficient path to quantum chemistry simulations, particularly for large systems.|
|**2026-07-02**|**Orthogonal Dualities of Dynamic Stochastic Higher Spin Vertex Models, using the Drinfeld Twister**|Jeffrey Kuan, Zhengye Zhou|[[arxiv:2305.17602](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.17602)]|The paper introduces a new algebraic method, based on the Drinfeld twister of the quantum group \( U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2) \), to construct duality functions for dynamic integrable models. Applied to dynamic stochastic higher spin vertex models, the method produces duality functions with non-dynamic counterparts expressed as \( {}_3 \varphi_2 \) hypergeometric functions, which degenerate to dual \( q \)-Krawtchouk polynomials—matching known orthogonal polynomial dualities for the dynamic ASEP. A key result is the explicit derivation of these duality functions and their connection to existing dualities. The significance lies in the method's general algebraic framework, which is expected to generate dualities for a wide range of other dynamic integrable systems.|

The authors propose new interlayer triplet-pairing operators and show that exact eigenstates with off-diagonal long-range order can be constructed for bilayer and trilayer systems, deriving from restricted spectrum generating algebra. This method approximates high-dimensional integrals in a tensor-train format, avoiding stochastic sampling and reducing the sign problem that limits traditional Monte Carlo approaches. Key results show accurate agreement with exact solutions for simple models, and in DMFT for the Hubbard model, it captures fast thermalization at half-filling while revealing a crossover instead of a sharp transition at 3/4-filling, even under severe sign problem conditions. The solver's significance lies in its ability to handle nonequilibrium and steady-state DMFT problems reliably, without analytic continuation, outperforming standard CT-QMC methods.|
|**2026-07-02**|**Entropy-Driven Structural Phase Transition in Nb $_3$Cl$_8$ via Density Functional Theory and an Effective Model**|Chenjie Zhu, Shuai Zhang, Zhong Fang, Zhijun Wang, Quansheng Wu, Hongming Weng|[[arxiv:2607.00599](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00599)]|This paper studies the structural phase transition in Nb₃Cl₈, which suppresses its potential quantum spin liquid (QSL) state. Using first-principles calculations and an extended Hubbard model, the authors develop a free-energy framework showing the transition is driven by competing phonon and spin entropy. The high-temperature phase is stabilized by softer phonons and paramagnetic spin entropy, while the low-temperature phase is favored by dimerization that hardens phonons and quenches spin. The findings explain how c-axis pressure prevents the transition, potentially allowing the QSL state to be accessed at low temperatures.|
|**2026-07-02**|**Work Statistics Under Quantum-Jump and Quench Dynamics in Monitored Ising Chains**|Manali Malakar, Alessandro Silva|[[arxiv:2607.00131](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00131)]|This paper analyzes work statistics in monitored transverse-field Ising chains during quantum quenches. The authors derive a trajectory-resolved generating function for work and evaluate it using a fermionic Gaussian-state formalism. Key results show that stochastic quantum jumps cause the work distribution to transition from a comb-like pattern to a Gaussian form, while controlled jumps yield linear or sublinear average work growth depending on causal connectivity. The significance lies in demonstrating how continuous measurement and monitoring fundamentally alter work statistics, driving them toward Gaussian behavior and washing out isolated-quench fine structure.|
|**2026-07-02**|**Interaction-Split Edge Spectral Flow in an Integer Hubbard Thouless Pump**|Yong-Feng Yang, Zhao-Rui Tian, Chen Cheng, Hong-Gang Luo|[[arxiv:2605.23769](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23769)]|The paper investigates how local Hubbard correlations affect the boundary behavior of an integer Thouless pump. Between these events, a low-energy, nearly charge-neutral triplet edge excitation emerges. This demonstrates that local interactions can reorganize the boundary realization of quantized bulk transport into separate charged and neutral spinful sectors.|

Despite these local changes, the low-frequency Hall conductivity remains quantized at \( e^2/h \). The study establishes the Haldane-Hubbard model as a minimal platform for exploring odd-parity altermagnetic topology in correlated systems.|

The validity of these refined predictions is confirmed through numerical simulations of non-integrable Floquet systems, enhancing the descriptive power of the ETH framework.|

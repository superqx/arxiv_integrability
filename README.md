|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|

|**2026-04-24**|**Electromagnetic, gravitational wave, and static gravitational transmission through throat spacetimes: a constraint-wave asymmetry**|Jeff Riley|[[arxiv:2604.14238](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14238)]|This paper investigates how electromagnetic waves, gravitational waves, and static gravitational fields transmit through the throats of spherically symmetric spacetimes. The authors compute transmission properties by decomposing the field equations into an effective Schrödinger problem, revealing a centrifugal barrier for propagating modes (ℓ≥1). Key results show that low-frequency electromagnetic and gravitational wave modes are strongly suppressed by tunneling below their barrier-top frequencies, while the static gravitational monopole (ℓ=0) faces no such barrier and transmits with only polynomial attenuation. These findings establish a universal qualitative asymmetry for static, spherically symmetric throats, independent of the matter content sourcing the geometry.|
|**2026-04-24**|**Regular Vaidya solutions of effective gravitational theories**|Valentin Boyanov, Ra\'ul Carballo-Rubio|[[arxiv:2506.14875](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.14875)]|This paper introduces a method to find exact, time-dependent solutions describing spherical collapse in modified theories of gravity. By focusing on the symmetries of the field equations, the authors prove the existence of regular Vaidya-type solutions that model the formation and growth of regular black holes or their mimickers through radiation. A key result is that modifications to gravitational dynamics can eliminate curvature singularities through an effective energy transfer to gravitational fields. This work provides a simple theoretical framework for studying regular gravitational collapse and supports previously ad-hoc geometric models as fully dynamical solutions.|
|**2026-04-24**|**Derivation of a $\PT$ -Symmetric Sine-Gordon Model from a Nonequilibrium Spin-Boson System via Keldysh Functional Integrals**|Vinayak M. Kulkarni|[[arxiv:2211.00333](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.00333)]|This paper derives a \(\mathcal{PT}\)-symmetric non-Hermitian sine-Gordon (NH-SG) model from a nonequilibrium spin-boson system using Keldysh functional integrals, along with polaron and bosonization transformations. The key result is an exact dictionary linking microscopic spin-boson parameters to NH-SG couplings, including a bias ratio \(\mathcal{I}\) that acts as a renormalization group invariant. The renormalization group analysis reproduces the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition, an exceptional point (EP) at \(\mathcal{I}=1\), and a mass gap formula. Near the EP, the model reduces to an integrable Lieb-Liniger gas, allowing exact computation of soliton bound-state energies and identification of the EP as a many-body threshold, demonstrating the significance of nonequilibrium asymmetry in generating non-Hermitian physics.|

## Mathematical Physics

|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-24**|**Yang-Baxter Integrability and Exceptional-Point Structure in Pseudo-Hermitian Quantum Impurity Systems**|Vinayak M. Kulkarni|[[arxiv:2604.21547](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21547)]|This paper addresses the problem of understanding integrability in pseudo-Hermitian quantum impurity systems, specifically those with exceptional points (EPs) where Hamiltonians become non-diagonalizable. The method involves constructing a Lax operator from a biorthogonal projector and deriving an η-modified RTT structure and Bethe equations, which are then regularized at the EP. Key results show that the Gaudin matrix becomes defective at the EP, leading to square-root coalescence of Bethe rapidities and Z₂ monodromy, and a diagnostic parameter R = κ(G)/det G/ is introduced to distinguish EP singularities from Kondo criticality. The significance lies in establishing a controlled Yang-Baxter integrability framework for non-Hermitian systems, revealing the unique quantum signatures of exceptional points within an integrable setting.|
|**2026-04-24**|**Derivation of a $\PT$-Symmetric Sine-Gordon Model from a Nonequilibrium Spin-Boson System via Keldysh Functional Integrals**|Vinayak M. Kulkarni|[[arxiv:2211.00333](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.00333)]|This paper derives a $\PT$-symmetric sine-Gordon (SG) model from a nonequilibrium spin-boson system using Keldysh functional integrals. The method incorporates polaron and bosonization transformations, yielding an imaginary coupling term that arises from a bias-induced asymmetry in the Keldysh distribution. Key results include renormalization group equations identical to those of Ashida et al., revealing a Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) separatrix at $K=2$ and an exceptional-point (EP) manifold at $\mathcal{I}=1$, where bound-state thresholds and Jordan partners emerge. The work is significant because it provides explicit microscopic initial conditions for the $\PT$ -symmetric SG model and uncovers the EP as a many-body bound-state threshold with exact Bethe ansatz solvability.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-24**|**$\eta$ -pairing in a two-band model of spinless fermions**|Igor N. Karnaukhov|[[arxiv:2604.21318](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21318)]|This paper investigates a two-band model of spinless fermions where itinerant and localized fermions interact via two-particle hybridization. Using the Bethe ansatz for an exact 1D solution, the authors show that this hybridization reduces repulsion between itinerant fermions, and under strong interaction, the effective force becomes attractive, enabling \(\eta\)-pairing. Key results demonstrate that this pairing mechanism can produce \(p\)-wave superconducting states with \(\eta\)-pairing. The significance lies in proposing \(\eta\)-pairing of spinless fermions as a potential explanation for high-temperature superconductivity observed in hydrogen-rich materials under high pressure.|
|**2026-04-24**|**Chaos, thermalization and breakdown of quantum-classical correspondence in a collective many-body system**|\'Angel L. Corps, Sebasti\'an G\'omez, Pavel Str\'ansk\'y, Armando Rela\~no, Pavel Cejnar|[[arxiv:2601.05627](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.05627)]|The paper investigates thermalization and quantum-classical correspondence in the four-site collective Bose-Hubbard model. By analyzing classical phase-space structure and excited-state quantum phase transitions, the authors identify three dynamical regimes, including an intermediate region where quantum and classical equilibrium states strongly disagree. Key results show that classical intermittency above the first excited-state quantum phase transition contrasts with quantum dynamics, which remain trapped in symmetry-breaking sectors despite a classically connected phase. This mismatch, caused by imbalance-carrying eigenstates, persists even for large particle numbers, revealing unexpectedly slow convergence to the classical limit and robust finite-size effects.|
|**2026-04-24**|**A microscopic model of fractionalized Fermi liquid**|P. Coleman, A. Panigrahi, A. Tsvelik|[[arxiv:2511.01115](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.01115)]|This paper identifies a relationship between two microscopic models: the Kondo lattice model with spinons and chargons, and the ancilla layer formulation of the Hubbard model. The authors introduce a "cross product" between these models, leading to a toy model of a fractionalized Fermi liquid (FL*). Key results show this combined model exhibits both central charge anomalies from the ancilla layer and non-zero fractionalized quasiparticle weight. This connection is significant as it unifies two distinct theoretical frameworks, providing a simpler microscopic pathway to study non-Fermi liquid behavior in strongly correlated systems.|
|**2026-04-24**|**Intertwined charge, spin, and orbital degrees of freedom under electronic correlations in the one-dimensional Fe $^{3+}$ chalcogenide chain**|Yang Zhang, Pontus Laurell, Gonzalo Alvarez, Adriana Moreo, Thomas A. Maier, Ling-Fang Lin, Elbio Dagotto|[[arxiv:2507.09870](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.09870)]|This paper investigates the electronic properties of a one-dimensional Fe³⁺ chalcogenide chain using first-principles calculations and a three-orbital Hubbard model solved with the density matrix renormalization group method. The authors find a robust antiferromagnetic coupling along the chain and, in the intermediate correlation regime, an orbital-selective Mott phase where localized and itinerant electrons coexist. Crucially, no superconducting pairing tendency is observed in the Fe³⁺ chain, contrasting with prior findings in iron ladders. The study clarifies key similarities and differences between Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺ chains and iron ladders, suggesting superconductivity is unlikely in these Fe³⁺ systems.|

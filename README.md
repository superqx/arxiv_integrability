|**2026-05-06**|**Classical correlation functions at strong coupling from hexagonalization**|Benjamin Basso, Erkan Kalu\c{c}, Didina Serban|[[arxiv:2605.03887](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03887)]|This paper investigates correlation functions of half-BPS operators in planar \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super-Yang-Mills at strong coupling, specifically in the classical limit where operator dimensions scale with the coupling. The authors apply the hexagon formalism to demonstrate that these correlation functions exponentiate and are governed by the free energy of Thermodynamic Bethe Ansatz (TBA) equations, which are structurally analogous to Gaiotto-Moore-Neitzke equations. A key methodological advance is the use of wall-crossing techniques to extend the TBA framework, formulating a \(\chi\)-system that applies to both polygonal hexagon tilings and closed geometries. The significance of this work lies in generalizing prior results for minimal surfaces in \(AdS_{2}\times S^{1}\) to a broader class of geometries and operator configurations.|
|**2026-05-06**|**Preparing High-Fidelity Thermofield Double States**|Brian J. J. Khor, Nadie LiTenn, Martin Sasieta, Brian Swingle|[[arxiv:2605.03017](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03017)]|The paper addresses the problem of preparing high-fidelity thermofield double (TFD) states on quantum computers, which are important for quantum many-body physics and quantum gravity. The method involves constructing a gapped parent Hamiltonian from two copies of the target system with ultra-local couplings, then adiabatically evolving from strong coupling to prepare an approximate TFD ground state. Key results show that for "spin Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev" models and mixed field Ising models, the simpler variant achieves high overlap for near-term devices but with exponential decay in global overlap, while an added penalty term can mitigate system-size scaling. The significance lies in proposing a broadly applicable TFD preparation method not limited to specific temperatures or geometric locality, supported by eigenstate thermalization hypothesis analysis.|
|**2026-05-06**|**Taxonomy of Instanton Corrections in Infinite Distance Limits**|Manuel Artime, Ralph Blumenhagen, Panagiotis Leivadaros|[[arxiv:2605.03005](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03005)]|This paper studies which non-perturbative instanton corrections appear in infinite distance limits of string theory moduli space. The method uses a BPS-protected \( R^4 \)-term and a one-loop Schwinger integral to compute the effect of integrating out the light towers of states that become massless in such limits. A key result is that the Schwinger integral exactly reproduces only those instantons whose action lies within a specific parametric window, bounded by the ratio of the gravity cutoff to the mass scale of the lightest tower. The significance is that it provides a taxonomy of these corrections, predicting the emergent instanton spectrum for any given infinite distance limit, which was confirmed in eight-dimensional toroidal compactifications and several seven-dimensional limits.|
|**2026-05-06**|**The Penrose Transform and the Kerr-Schild double copy**|Emma Albertini, Michael L. The authors demonstrate that for a specific class of self-dual vacuum solutions called "twistorial Kerr-Schild spacetimes," these two ostensibly distinct prescriptions are equivalent. Using null Lorentz transformations and homogeneous functions on twistor space, they prove this equivalence, illustrating it with the self-dual (Kerr)-Taub-NUT spacetime as an example. The work is significant as it unifies two important double copy frameworks, suggesting a deeper geometric connection between them.|

|**2026-05-06**|**Page Curve for Local-Operator Entanglement from Free Probability**|Neil Dowling, Silvia Pappalardi|[[arxiv:2605.02995](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02995)]|The paper addresses the problem of determining the "Page curve" for local-operator entanglement (LOE) from random unitary dynamics, which measures the classical simulability of Heisenberg operators. The authors use free probability and Weingarten calculus to analytically compute the average LOE for all Rényi indices, finding that it asymptotically reproduces the Page curve for random states, with exponentially small, operator-dependent corrections. A key result is that the leading-order LOE depends only on the two-point normalization of the initial operator, not on higher-order free cumulants, unlike out-of-time ordered correlators. This suggests that the long-time LOE in chaotic systems is independent of the finer structure of the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis, distinguishing it from other scrambling diagnostics.|

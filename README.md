## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-02**|**Exact interpolation between Fick and Cattaneo diffusion in relativistic kinetic theory**|Lorenzo Gavassino|[[arxiv:2604.00984](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00984)]|This paper addresses the need for a microscopic model that bridges the gap between standard parabolic diffusion (Fick's law) and hyperbolic, wave-like diffusion (Cattaneo's law). The method constructs a family of exactly solvable relativistic kinetic theories in 1+1 dimensions, where a single parameter tunes the nature of particle scatterings from infinitely soft to maximally hard. The key result is an exact analytical solution for all modes, showing how purely diffusive modes continuously deform into damped propagating waves as the scattering dynamics change. Its significance lies in providing a concrete, tractable microscopic framework that unifies two fundamental transport regimes, offering deep insight into the transition from diffusive to telegraphic behavior.|
|**2026-04-02**|**Superconformal index for $\mathcal{N} = 4$ Super Yang-Mills and Elliptic Macdonald Polynomials**|Gao-fu Ren, Min-xin Huang|[[arxiv:2604.00924](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00924)]|This paper connects the superconformal index of a key supersymmetric gauge theory to an integrable system. The method involves expressing the index using elliptic Macdonald polynomials, which allows for a systematic expansion in terms of an elliptic parameter. The key result is that this expansion can be computed perturbatively by solving the associated integrable model. This work is significant because it provides a new, powerful computational framework for the index, unifying and generalizing several known limits.|
|**2026-04-02**|**Parallel surface defects, Hecke operators, and quantum Hitchin system**|Saebyeok Jeong, Norton Lee, Nikita Nekrasov|[[arxiv:2304.04656](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.04656)]|This paper studies two specific half-BPS surface defects within a four-dimensional \(\mathcal{N}=2\) supersymmetric gauge theory. The method involves analyzing integrals over moduli spaces of parabolic framed sheaves and applying analytic techniques from supersymmetric gauge theory. The key results show that one defect produces a twisted \(\mathcal{D}\)-module, while the other acts as a Hecke operator, leading to a Hecke eigensheaf property whose eigenvalues are opers. The significance is that this framework derives, from gauge theory, how the geometric Langlands correspondence provides the spectral equations for the quantum Hitchin integrable system, linking it to dualities in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) theory.|

The key result is the derivation of an exact, closed-form expression for the H function. The authors' method leverages a mathematical property called the "no-mixed adjoint condition" to drastically simplify the Zassenhaus formula, a tool for exponentiating sums of non-commuting operators. The key result is an exact quantum circuit ansatz for strongly correlated systems that requires a finite number of gates equal to the number of parameters, eliminating the need for Trotter steps. The significance is a more efficient and fundamentally exact framework for quantum computational chemistry, which also provides new theoretical insight into when Trotterized approximations can be optimized to exact solutions.|

A key result is that in integrable systems, this framework demonstrates that hydrodynamic noise and the associated bare diffusion must vanish entirely. This finding is significant because it confirms a recent conjecture and establishes that ballistic macroscopic fluctuation theory provides the complete hydrodynamic description for integrable models.|

|**2026-04-02**|**Principal component analysis of wavefunction snapshots in non-equilibrium dynamics**|Dharmesh Yadav, Devendra Singh Bhakuni, Bijay Kumar Agarwalla|[[arxiv:2604.01140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01140)]|This paper addresses the challenge of extracting meaningful physical insights from the complex, high-dimensional data generated by quantum dynamics simulations. The method involves applying principal component analysis to time-series snapshots of wavefunctions, but with a specific data transformation designed to maximize the information captured by the leading component and link it directly to physical observables. The key results, demonstrated on a Heisenberg spin chain, show that this approach successfully explains dynamical features and can be extended to analyze higher-order correlations. Its significance lies in providing a general, interpretable framework for dimensionality reduction in quantum dynamics, which is particularly valuable for analyzing data from experimental quantum simulators.|
|**2026-04-02**|**Scattering phase shift in quantum mechanics on quantum computers: non-Hermitian systems and imaginary-time simulations**|Peng Guo, Paul LeVan, Frank X. Lee, Yong Zhao|[[arxiv:2604.00127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00127)]|This paper addresses the challenge of extracting scattering phase shifts from quantum simulations, where standard real-time methods produce fast oscillations that obscure the signal. The authors propose two alternative approaches: simulating Hermitian systems in imaginary time or simulating non-Hermitian systems in real time, both of which lead to non-unitary evolution. They solve this non-unitarity by combining block encoding and the Hadamard test into a quantum algorithm that avoids mid-circuit measurements and scales linearly with evolution time. The significance is that these methods successfully circumvent the oscillatory problem in benchmark tests, offering a promising pathway for studying scattering in more complex quantum systems on quantum computers.|
|**2026-04-02**|**Mpemba effect in a two-dimensional bistable potential**|Hisao Hayakawa, Satoshi Takada|[[arxiv:2603.24148](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24148)]|This paper investigates the Mpemba effect—the counterintuitive phenomenon where a hotter system can cool faster than a colder one—in a two-dimensional system. The authors construct an exactly solvable model using an overdamped particle in a radially symmetric, bistable potential, enabling them to map the Fokker-Planck equation to a solvable Schrödinger-type eigenvalue problem. Their key analytical result shows that the amplitude of the slowest relaxation mode depends non-monotonically on the initial temperature, providing a sufficient condition for observing the Mpemba effect when the potential minima are suitably separated. The significance of this work lies in providing a rare, analytically tractable two-dimensional model that demonstrates anomalous relaxation without physical boundaries, clarifying the role of geometry in such nonequilibrium phenomena.|
|**2026-04-02**|**A Remarkable Application of Zassenhaus Formula to Strongly Correlated Electron Systems**|Louis Jourdan (LJAD), Patrick Cassam-Chena\"i (LJAD)|[[arxiv:2510.24364](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24364)]|This paper addresses the challenge of simulating strongly correlated electron systems on quantum computers, where standard methods require approximations like Trotterization. The authors introduce a method that applies the Zassenhaus formula to unitary coupled cluster ansatzes, specifically when operators meet a "no-mixed adjoint" condition. A key result is that this approach eliminates the need for Trotterization, allowing for an exact implementation with a finite set of quantum gates matching the number of parameters. This work is significant as it provides an exact, efficient framework for quantum simulations of electron correlations and explains when optimization of Trotterized approximations can yield exact solutions.|
|**2026-04-02**|**The Frenkel line and the pseudogap: an analogy between classical and electronic fluids**|J. Fournier, P. -O. Downey, O. Gingras, C. -D. H\'ebert, M. Charlebois, A. -M. S. Its significance lies in providing a well-defined, high-temperature crossover for electronic systems, analogous to a fundamental concept in classical fluid physics.|
|**2026-04-02**|**The odd-parity altermagnetism: A spin group study**|Minghuan Zeng, Zheng Qin, Ling Qin, Shiping Feng, Lin Wu, Dong-Hui Xu, Rui Wang|[[arxiv:2507.09906](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.09906)]|This paper addresses the need to define and understand odd-parity altermagnetism, a new type of magnetism with unconventional spin splitting.

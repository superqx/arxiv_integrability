## High Energy Theory

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-09**|**Groenewold-Moyal twists, integrable spin-chains and AdS/CFT**|Riccardo Borsato, Miguel Garc\'ia Fern\'andez|[[arxiv:2604.07291](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07291)]|This paper addresses the spectral problem in AdS/CFT dualities deformed by Groenewold-Moyal twists, which couple two spin-chains. The method involves analyzing a twisted spin-chain Hamiltonian, showing it can appear in a non-diagonal Jordan form but becomes diagonalizable in a specific basis, leading to a deformed energy spectrum calculated via the Baxter equation. A key result is the matching of the ground state energy from the spin-chain with a conserved charge from a deformed string theory solution, though this charge is non-local and not a standard symmetry. The significance lies in advancing the integrability approach for non-commutative deformations of AdS/CFT, revealing novel non-local conserved structures in the string sigma-model.|
|**2026-04-09**|**Loops and legs: ABJM amplitudes from $f$-graphs**|Song He, Yao-Qi Zhang|[[arxiv:2601.21980](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21980)]|This paper addresses the challenge of deriving individual scattering amplitudes in ABJM theory from a generating function that only directly provides "squared" amplitudes. The method involves analyzing a generating function built from planar $f$-graphs, which are combinatorial objects with a hidden permutation symmetry, and then extracting specific loop and leg configurations using techniques like Yangian invariants and a basis of dual conformal integrands. The key results include the successful extraction of four-point amplitudes up to six loops and multi-leg amplitudes up to two loops, demonstrating the generating function's sufficiency. This work is significant because it establishes a systematic, graph-based framework for computing amplitudes in ABJM theory, drawing a powerful parallel to the successful use of similar methods in ${\cal N}=4$ super Yang-Mills theory.|

## Condensed Matter

|Publish Date|Title|Authors|arXiv|Summary|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**2026-04-09**|**Interaction-Mediated Non-Reciprocal Dynamics in Open Quantum Systems: From an Exactly Solvable Model to Generic Behavior**|Pietro Borchia, Johannes Knolle, Andreas Nunnenkamp|[[arxiv:2604.07346](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07346)]|This paper addresses the challenge of engineering and understanding non-reciprocal dynamics—where excitations flow asymmetrically—in interacting quantum many-body systems. The method employs an exactly solvable model of spinful fermions with Hatsugai-Kohmoto interactions coupled to an engineered reservoir, alongside analysis of a Fermi-Hubbard chain. The key result is that particle interactions can transfer bath-induced non-reciprocity to other system degrees of freedom, inducing directional drift even in parts not directly coupled to the environment. Its significance lies in providing a minimal, solvable framework that reveals a generic mechanism for interaction-mediated non-reciprocity, advancing the design of quantum materials with directional transport.|
|**2026-04-09**|**Groenewold-Moyal twists, integrable spin-chains and AdS/CFT**|Riccardo Borsato, Miguel Garc\'ia Fern\'andez|[[arxiv:2604.07291](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07291)]|This paper addresses the challenge of studying AdS/CFT dualities deformed by non-commutative Groenewold-Moyal twists using integrability techniques. The method involves analyzing a twisted spin-chain model that couples two standard chains, finding that its Hamiltonian can be diagonalized in a specific basis to reveal a deformed spectrum, which is then computed perturbatively. A key result is the successful matching of the ground state energy from the spin-chain calculation with a conserved charge from a corresponding deformed string theory solution, despite this charge being non-local. The significance lies in extending the integrability framework to these non-commutative deformations, providing a new quantitative test of holography in a modified geometric setting.|
|**2026-04-09**|**Perpendicular electric field induced $s^\pm$-wave to $d$-wave superconducting transition in thin film La$_3$Ni$_2$O$_7$**|Yongping Wei, Xun Liu, Fan Yang, Mi Jiang|[[arxiv:2604.07185](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07185)]|This paper investigates how a perpendicular electric field can change the superconducting pairing symmetry in the bilayer nickelate La₃Ni₂O₇. Using dynamical cluster quantum Monte Carlo simulations on a two-orbital Hubbard model, the authors found that the field suppresses the material's intrinsic $s^\pm$-wave pairing. The key result is that the field induces a transition to $d$-wave superconductivity, driven by orbital redistribution, with this $d$ -wave pairing strength forming a dome-shaped dependence on the field strength. This work is significant as it confirms theoretical predictions with advanced many-body calculations and demonstrates a potential method to electronically control superconducting order.|
|**2026-04-09**|**Exact Solution for Current-Driven Domain-Wall Dynamics Beyond Lorentz Contraction in Antiferromagnets with Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya Interaction**|Mu-Kun Lee, Rub\'en M. Otxoa, Masahito Mochizuki|[[arxiv:2604.06730](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06730)]|This paper addresses the problem of describing how electric currents drive domain walls in antiferromagnets that have a Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction (DMI), where exact analytical solutions were lacking. The method involves deriving an exact analytical model for spiral domain wall dynamics, specifically applicable when the magnetic easy axis and DMI vector are aligned. The key results show the domain wall moves at a constant velocity and rotates, but most notably, its width changes with current in unconventional ways—either simply elongating or first contracting and then elongating—deviating from standard Lorentz contraction. The significance is that this exact solution provides a foundation for predicting and detecting distinct DMI signatures in synthetic antiferromagnets through experimental measurements of domain wall width.|
|**2026-04-09**|**Decomposing momentum scales in the Hubbard Model: From Hatsugai-Kohmoto to Aubry-Andr\'e**|Dmitry Manning-Coe, Barry Bradlyn|[[arxiv:2604.06588](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06588)]|This paper addresses the challenge of solving interacting lattice models like the Hubbard model, which are computationally intractable due to all-to-all momentum coupling. The authors introduce a method that simplifies the problem by retaining only a selected subset of key interaction channels in momentum space, generalizing concepts like twist-averaged boundary conditions and Hatsugai-Kohmoto models. A key result demonstrates the method's accuracy and efficiency when applied to the Aubry-André-Hubbard model, where small momentum clusters can approximate the ground state energy with less than 1% error compared to precise benchmarks. The significance lies in providing a physically motivated, computationally feasible framework for studying complex systems, such as interacting moiré materials in two dimensions.|
|**2026-04-09**|**An Approach to Probing Particles and Quasi-particles in the Condensed Bose-Hubbard Model**|Huy Nguyen, Yu-Xin Wang, Jacob M. Taylor|[[arxiv:2602.05924](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05924)]|This paper addresses the challenge of distinguishing between bare particles and collective quasiparticle excitations when probing quantum many-body systems, using cold atoms in a Bose-Hubbard model as a testbed. The method involves theoretically analyzing how tuning the parameters of a standard phase-contrast imaging measurement affects both the system's observation and the measurement's backaction. The key results show that specific measurement regimes allow for the selective probing of either bare particle or quasiparticle dynamics, and even enable control over the measurement-induced creation and diffusion of quasiparticles. This work is significant because it provides a concrete framework for manipulating and observing fundamental excitations in quantum simulators, with implications for testing foundational quantum theories.|

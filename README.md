|**2026-05-19**|**From classical Lax ODEs to quantum integrable theories: the moduli**|Davide Fioravanti, Marco Rossi|[[arxiv:2605.18695](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18695)]|This paper presents a method to derive quantum integrable theories from classical integrable equations, specifically a generalized sinh-Gordon equation with moduli. The approach uses the ODE/IM correspondence, where functional relations from the classical linear problem, combined with asymptotic conditions, define eigenvalues of quantum Baxter and transfer matrix operators. The key results include the derivation of Y-systems and thermodynamic Bethe Ansatz equations directly from the classical system, with all relations modified by the moduli, which describe the quantum homogeneous sine-Gordon model. This work proves two of Zamolodchikov's conjectures and provides a novel, invariant parametrization of the monodromy space, linking directly to scattering amplitudes in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM theory.|
|**2026-05-19**|**The classical Yangian symmetry of Auxiliary Field Sigma Models**|Daniele Bielli, Christian Ferko, Michele Galli, Gabriele Tartaglino-Mazzucchelli|[[arxiv:2605.18213](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18213)]|This paper addresses the challenge of understanding the symmetries of integrable field theories after they have been deformed, which often obscures their structure. The authors provide a systematic method by generalizing a known recursive procedure (BIZZ) to generate non-local charges for a wide class of sigma models. They establish sufficient conditions for these charges to form a Yangian algebra and confirm this structure across multiple examples. The significance lies in offering a unified explanation for the persistence of integrability and symmetry in a broad landscape of deformed sigma models.|
|**2026-05-19**|**Resurgent structure of the 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole**|Michal Malinsk\'y|[[arxiv:2602.14620](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.14620)]|This paper applies resurgence theory to analyze the differential equations that define the spatial profile of the 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole. The method exploits the simplicity of the Borel transform and associated Volterra equations to control Borel-plane singularities at all orders. A key result is the discovery of simple, universal analytic non-perturbative background profiles that enable a uniformly convergent global perturbative expansion. This work provides analytic control over numerical parameters at the origin and infinity, revealing the profound universality in the monopole's structure.|
|**2026-05-19**|**Yangian Symmetry Escapes from the Fishnet**|Niklas Beisert, Benedikt K\"onig|[[arxiv:2511.19788](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.19788)]|This paper investigates Yangian symmetry in classical bi-scalar and supersymmetric fishnet models. The authors show that Yangian symmetry is classically realized in these models but does not extend to generic quantum correlation functions in the bi-scalar model, providing explicit counter-examples of Feynman graphs and octagon-shaped loop diagrams. The key result is that quantum Yangian invariance fails beyond well-known classes of invariants, highlighting a non-zero dual Coxeter number as an obstacle. This finding suggests significant limitations for complete integrability in planar quantum field theories.|
|**2026-05-19**|**Quiver Yangians as Coulomb branch algebras**|Tiantai Chen, Wei Li|[[arxiv:2502.01323](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.01323)]|This paper proposes that the quantum Coulomb branch algebra of a 3D N=4 quiver gauge theory, deformed by an Ω-background, is equivalent to a truncated shifted quiver Yangian. The method involves analyzing the action of monopole operators on 1/2-BPS vortex configurations, which represent states in the theory. The authors explicitly verify their conjecture for all tree-type quivers and demonstrate that the Hilbert spaces of vortices provide distinct representations of the Yangian. This work establishes a direct algebraic framework for the quantum Coulomb branch, unifying and extending existing results for non-tree-type quivers.|

|**2026-05-19**|**From classical Lax ODEs to quantum integrable theories: the moduli**|Davide Fioravanti, Marco Rossi|[[arxiv:2605.18695](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18695)]|This paper starts from a classical integrable system—a generalized sinh-Gordon equation with \(2N-1\) complex moduli—and its underlying matrix linear differential problem. The key method involves showing that Wronskians of solutions satisfy functional relations analogous to Baxter's \(Q\) and \(T\) operators in quantum integrable models, leading to Y-systems and thermodynamic Bethe Ansatz equations. A major result is that all these relations are modified by the moduli, enabling an invariant parametrization of the monodromy space. This work is significant because it connects classical Lax equations to quantum theories like the homogeneous sine-Gordon model and proves two of Zamolodchikov's conjectures, with applications to scattering amplitudes in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM.|
|**2026-05-19**|**The classical Yangian symmetry of Auxiliary Field Sigma Models**|Daniele Bielli, Christian Ferko, Michele Galli, Gabriele Tartaglino-Mazzucchelli|[[arxiv:2605.18213](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18213)]|This paper addresses the challenge of understanding symmetries in integrable field theories after deformations like T¯T or auxiliary field modifications, which obscure the underlying algebraic structure. The authors provide a systematic method by generalizing the BIZZ recursive procedure to generate non-local charges for a broad class of sigma models and their auxiliary field deformations. Key results show that these charges satisfy a Yangian algebra and a Maillet bracket structure across multiple examples, demonstrating that Hamiltonian integrability is preserved. The work offers a unified framework explaining the persistence of Yangian symmetry in many deformed sigma models, deepening the theoretical foundation of integrable systems.|
|**2026-05-19**|**Exact Current Fluctuations in a Tight-Binding Chain with Dephasing Noise**|Taiki Ishiyama, Kazuya Fujimoto, Tomohiro Sasamoto|[[arxiv:2504.06989](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06989)]|This paper derives the first exact solution for the full counting statistics (FCS) of current in a diffusive quantum many-body system, specifically a tight-binding chain with dephasing noise. The authors leverage an SU(2) symmetry and a mapping to the one-dimensional Hubbard model to obtain an exact Fredholm determinant representation for the moment generating function of the time-integrated current. Their long-time asymptotic analysis reveals that the cumulant generating function exhibits diffusive scaling for any nonzero dephasing, with a crossover from ballistic to diffusive behavior in the weak-dephasing limit. This exact result provides a crucial microscopic benchmark for macroscopic fluctuation theory and confirms the experimentally observed diffusive scaling of current fluctuations.|

The authors use both the robustness of magic and stabilizer Renyi entropy to quantify magic at zero and finite temperatures, as well as during quantum quench dynamics. Key results show that the stabilizer Renyi entropy fails to detect mixed stabilizer states common in such systems, whereas the robustness of magic is more reliable. The study demonstrates that non-stabilizerness is a distinct quantum resource, offering insights into quantum-state complexity that complement measures like fermionic non-Gaussianity and entanglement.|

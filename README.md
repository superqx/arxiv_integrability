The authors derive a first-principles resolution that yields closed-form predictions for entanglement growth and charge fluctuations after general quantum quenches. They benchmark their results against exact solutions of a quantum cellular automaton and numerical simulations of an XXZ spin chain, showing agreement with the quasiparticle picture for entanglement entropy. Key results include deriving product and determinant formulas for the norms and overlaps of these states, providing a complete framework for analyzing this integrable model. The significance lies in enabling detailed physical calculations for a chain that models fractional statistics and conformal field theory.|

The work provides a systematic method for analyzing boundary quantum quenches in integrable systems, advancing the understanding of non-equilibrium dynamics in such settings.|

The method reconstructs dynamics using Lax pair theory, showing that the requirement of continuous spectrum preservation inherently forces a Lax form of evolution. Key results demonstrate that the Heisenberg equation, Schrödinger equations, and conservation laws emerge as theorems rather than postulates, with the Hamiltonian itself arising as a necessary generator. The significance lies in providing a dynamical bridge from measurement structure to standard quantum evolution without assuming the Hamiltonian a priori.|

Key results include the recovery of known low-compactness approximations and the derivation, in the Buchdahl limit, of a regularity condition for static modes and an analytical description of dynamic modes diverging at the star's central pressure singularity. Key results include uncovering initial-state-dependent nonequilibrium behavior, analytical results for non-thermal relaxation under perturbations, and numerically exact entanglement growth across various initial states. The work enables unified study of thermalizing and nonthermalizing regimes in a tractable model, with experimental testability in quantum simulators like Rydberg atom arrays.|

The significance lies in providing experimentally relevant signatures of chiral topological order and conceptually linking two-dimensional Kitaev spin liquids to one-dimensional quantum wires.|

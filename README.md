|**2026-05-05**|**Quantum scars from holographic boson stars**|Yan Liu, Ya-Wen Sun, Yuan-Tai Wang|[[arxiv:2605.02446](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02446)]|This paper demonstrates that asymptotically AdS mini-boson stars serve as holographic duals to quantum many-body scars. The authors show that while the full gravitational system, including black holes, exhibits chaos consistent with the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis, boson stars form an integrable subsector within this chaotic spectrum. These boson stars also feature anomalously low entanglement and robust revivals in Krylov complexity, contrasting with thermal black holes at the same energy density. The findings establish a new link between many-body scar physics, quantum chaos, and compact gravitational objects without horizons.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Universality of Quantum Gates in Particle and Symmetry Constrained Subspaces**|Andreas Stergiou, Nicolas PD Sawaya|[[arxiv:2605.00979](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00979)]|This paper addresses the challenge of simulating quantum systems within constrained subspaces, such as fixed particle number, on near-term quantum computers. The authors use Lie algebraic methods to prove that standard, hardware-efficient quantum gates are universal for state preparation in these subspaces, enabled by a mechanism called Pauli \(Z\) dressing that generates the required algebra. They provide a practical Jacobian criterion to verify a circuit’s ability to explore the target manifold and demonstrate universality for both real and complex state preparation. The framework is applied to Fermi-Hubbard and Bose-Hubbard models, molecular electronic structure, and the fuzzy sphere regularization of 3D Ising conformal field theory, enabling variational ground and excited state preparation for extracting scaling dimensions.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Menagerie of Euclidean constructions for 3D holographic cosmologies**|Mark Van Raamsdonk, Alejandro Vilar L\'opez|[[arxiv:2601.10906](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10906)]|This paper generalizes a previous construction (by Antonini, Sasieta, and Swingle) of three-dimensional gravity solutions that represent CFT states dual to spacetimes containing a closed baby universe. The authors develop a method to start with an existing heavy-particle cosmology and glue on an arbitrary number of AdS tubes connecting the past and future boundaries of the associated Euclidean wormhole. A key result is the identification of a necessary condition for the cosmological wormhole to dominate the Euclidean path integral, which the original construction typically fails to meet. The significance lies in providing a flexible framework to construct more realistic, approximately homogeneous cosmologies and identifying alternative, potentially dominant saddle points.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Medicine show: A Calogero model with principal series states**|Tarek Anous, Jackson R. Fliss, Jeremy van der Heijden|[[arxiv:2507.17811](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.17811)]|This paper presents a deformed version of the Calogero model, an integrable N-particle quantum system, to incorporate states from the unitary principal series representation of \(\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})\). The method involves changing the domain of quantum operators, which preserves unitarity and \(\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})\)-invariance but breaks the model's original integrability. Key results include explicit solutions for the deformed model with \(N=2\) and \(3\) particles, along with a general solving procedure for any \(N\). The significance lies in providing a tractable example for understanding principal series states, with implications for theories like interacting massive quantum fields on de Sitter space.|

## Condensed Matter

|**2026-05-05**|**Enhancing supercurrent-based inertial sensing via interactions in atomtronic angular accelerometers**|S. Carmona-L\'opez, A. Matos-Abiague, F. Isaule, L. Morales-Molina|[[arxiv:2605.02048](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02048)]|This paper theoretically studies supercurrents of ultracold atoms in rotating, angularly shaken ring lattices to create high-precision angular accelerometers. Using a Bose-Hubbard model, the authors show that a strong net atomic current emerges when the driving frequency matches a fraction of the Bloch frequency, while away from resonance the current averages to zero. Key results reveal that in non-interacting systems, sensitivity is Fourier-limited by averaging time, but introducing weak atomic interactions surpasses this fundamental limit, improving sensitivity by at least two orders of magnitude. This work demonstrates interaction-enhanced sensing, paving the way for superior atomtronic inertial sensors beyond single-particle limitations.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Ergodic and Discrete Time Crystal Phases in Periodically Kicked Many-Body Quantum Systems: An Analytical Study**|Vijay Kumar, Dibyendu Roy|[[arxiv:2605.01969](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01969)]|This paper analytically investigates the time evolution of observables in periodically kicked many-body quantum systems. The authors derive criteria for when the system reaches an infinite-temperature state at long times, and show that violating these criteria leads to persistent, subharmonic oscillations that are robust to perturbations, indicating a discrete time crystal phase. A key finding is that, in a specific spin chain, both ergodic and time crystal phases can appear for the same system parameters, depending solely on the initial state. The work provides a rigorous analytical framework for understanding the emergence of ergodicity and time-crystalline order in driven systems without relying on numerical approximations.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Decoherence due to the sudden coupling of an impurity to a metal**|Felipe D. Picoli, Gustavo Diniz, Luiz N. Oliveira|[[arxiv:2605.01612](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01612)]|This paper studies decoherence in a quantum impurity system by suddenly coupling the impurity to a metal, using the spinless resonant level model. The authors analyze the survival probability and impurity occupation to probe dephasing from particle-hole excitations, and introduce a mixed linear-logarithmic discretization method to suppress unphysical revivals in finite systems. Key results show that this method eliminates finite-size artifacts by rendering excitation energies incommensurate, revealing a crossover from coherent Rabi oscillations in small systems to effectively irreversible decoherence in large lattices. The significance lies in providing a unified analytical and numerical framework for understanding the transition from coherent dynamics to relaxation in open quantum systems.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Universality of Quantum Gates in Particle and Symmetry Constrained Subspaces**|Andreas Stergiou, Nicolas PD Sawaya|[[arxiv:2605.00979](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00979)]|The paper addresses the problem of preparing quantum states within constrained subspaces, such as fixed particle number or spin, on near-term quantum computers. The authors use Lie algebraic methods to prove that hardware-efficient gates are universal for state preparation in these subspaces, with the key mechanism being Pauli Z dressing—a process where gate commutators generate operators that decompose multi-plane rotations into single-plane generators spanning the full so(w) algebra. They also provide a Jacobian criterion to verify circuit expressivity and demonstrate the framework's applicability to systems like Fermi-Hubbard and Bose-Hubbard models, as well as molecular electronic structures. The significance lies in enabling arbitrary state preparation within constrained subspaces, with applications validated through symmetry-preserving circuits for the fuzzy sphere regularization of the 3D Ising conformal field theory, where ground and excited states extract CFT scaling dimensions.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Functional renormalization group study of a dissipative Bose--Hubbard model**|Oscar Bouverot-Dupuis, Vincent Grison, Nicolas Paris|[[arxiv:2511.09643](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09643)]|This paper uses the functional renormalization group (FRG) to study a one-dimensional dissipative Bose-Hubbard model where each lattice site couples to an independent bath. The method captures non-Markovian dissipation and identifies two competing low-energy phases: a Luttinger liquid and a dissipative fixed point with finite compressibility but zero superfluid stiffness. These phases are separated by a Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition for ohmic, sub-ohmic, and super-ohmic bath spectra. Crucially, the FRG approach accesses the complete renormalization group flow from a single microscopic action, providing a unified framework beyond perturbative or variational methods.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Enhanced Superconducting Diode Effect in the Asymmetric Hatsugai-Kohmoto Model**|Kai Chen, Pavan Hosur|[[arxiv:2510.17452](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.17452)]|This paper investigates the superconducting diode effect (SDE) in strongly correlated systems, a regime that has been largely unexplored. The authors use the solvable Hatsugai-Kohmoto (HK) interaction model to study asymmetric band metals. Their results show that strong electron-electron interactions can significantly enhance the quality factor of the SDE. This work provides new insights into the role of correlations in nonreciprocal superconductivity.|
|**2026-05-05**|**YCl Electride as a Multi-Orbital Correlated Topological Dice Lattice System**|Jianqi Zhong, Songyuan Geng, Teng-Fei Ying, Haoxiang Li, Benjamin T. Zhou|[[arxiv:2509.05958](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.05958)]|This paper reveals that the recently discovered flat band in the layered electride YCl cannot be accurately described by a simple dice lattice model due to a fundamental layer-orbital-valley coupling. To address this, the authors construct a multi-orbital Hubbard model using ab initio methods that correctly captures the system's symmetry, topology, and correlation effects. Their key results predict that this multi-orbital flat band supports a robust ferromagnetic ground state and electrically tunable correlated topological phases, which are absent in a single-orbital model. The work establishes YCl as a new platform for exploring the interplay of correlation and topology within a multi-orbital framework.|
|**2026-05-05**|**Phase-Sensitive Measurements on a Fermi-Hubbard Quantum Processor**|Alberto R. Cavallar, Luis Escalera-Moreno, Titus Franz, Timon Hilker, J. Ignacio Cirac, Philipp M. Preiss, Benjamin F. Schiffer|[[arxiv:2509.01637](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.01637)]|This paper addresses the challenge of measuring complex expectation values of the time-evolution operator (Loschmidt echoes) on fermionic quantum processors. The authors propose a hardware-efficient protocol that combines global quench dynamics with short imaginary time evolution, implemented via tailored pulse sequences in an optical superlattice. Numerical simulations show the method efficiently obtains Loschmidt echoes for large many-body states across a wide spectral range, particularly for the Fermi-Hubbard model at half-filling and finite doping. This work is significant because it enables the measurement of spectral properties, such as the local density of states, and facilitates the study of finite-temperature phenomena in current fermionic quantum simulators.|
